人形机器人因其类人形态与运动能力，在家庭服务、工业装配等场景中具有广泛应用前景。然而，现有方法在全身协调操作方面面临两大挑战：一是从高维视觉输入中提取任务相关的运动表征，二是生成同时满足动力学约束与任务目标的全身轨迹。传统基于模型的控制方法虽能保证稳定性，但难以适应复杂多变的操作任务；而纯数据驱动的强化学习方法则面临样本效率低与泛化能力不足的问题。Opt2Skill 正是针对这一矛盾，提出了一种融合模型先验与数据驱动的混合框架。

Opt2Skill 的核心创新在于其“技能路由”机制。系统将移动操作任务拆解为若干可复用的原子技能（如抓取、放置、行走），每个技能对应一个预训练的策略网络。在执行阶段，一个基于视觉感知的高层模块根据当前场景状态动态选择并组合这些技能，从而在不重新训练的情况下适应新任务。这种模块化设计显著提升了系统的可扩展性与零样本泛化能力，同时保证了全身运动的动态可行性。

## Overview

Opt2Skill proposes a whole-body mobile manipulation framework for humanoid robots, aiming to address the end-to-end learning problem from visual perception to dynamically feasible trajectory generation. By decomposing complex tasks into routable skill modules and combining reinforcement learning with imitation learning, this research enables the generation and combination of diverse manipulation behaviors. Its main contribution lies in constructing a scalable system from scene understanding and motion representation extraction to whole-body trajectory planning and execution, offering a new paradigm for dexterous manipulation of humanoid robots in unstructured environments.

## Content

(a) Research Background and Problem  
Humanoid robots, due to their human-like morphology and locomotion capabilities, have broad application prospects in scenarios such as household services and industrial assembly. However, existing methods face two major challenges in whole-body coordinated manipulation: first, extracting task-relevant motion representations from high-dimensional visual inputs; second, generating whole-body trajectories that simultaneously satisfy dynamic constraints and task objectives. While traditional model-based control methods can ensure stability, they struggle to adapt to complex and varied manipulation tasks. Conversely, purely data-driven reinforcement learning approaches suffer from low sample efficiency and insufficient generalization. Opt2Skill addresses this contradiction by proposing a hybrid framework that integrates model priors with data-driven methods.

(b) Method or Model Framework  
The framework first recovers scene geometry and target poses using multi-view camera images, simulation interaction data, and tactile signals to construct task-relevant motion representations. It then employs a training paradigm combining PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) and ACT (Action Chunking Transformers): PPO explores dynamically feasible whole-body trajectories in simulation, while ACT learns fine manipulation patterns from expert demonstrations via behavior cloning. Finally, inverse kinematics (IK) redirects high-level action commands into joint-space position/torque commands, generating complete whole-body trajectory sequences.

(c) Key Technical Innovations  
The core innovation of Opt2Skill lies in its "skill routing" mechanism. The system decomposes mobile manipulation tasks into several reusable atomic skills (e.g., grasping, placing, walking), each corresponding to a pre-trained policy network. During execution, a high-level module based on visual perception dynamically selects and combines these skills according to the current scene state, thereby adapting to new tasks without retraining. This modular design significantly enhances system scalability and zero-shot generalization while ensuring the dynamic feasibility of whole-body motion.

(d) Experiments/Validation or Application Value  
Although the paper does not disclose specific experimental data, its technical approach suggests potential for validation in both simulation environments and real humanoid robot platforms. By comparing with pure reinforcement learning and pure imitation learning baselines, this method is expected to achieve advantages in metrics such as task success rate, trajectory smoothness, and energy efficiency. Its application value lies in the ability to complete diverse manipulations by combining existing skills without redesigning controllers for each new task, providing a feasible technical pathway for deploying humanoid robots in dynamic scenarios such as household services and disaster rescue.
