## Overview

BeyondMimic proposes a general framework for converting human motion data (videos or motion capture trajectories) into executable whole-body control commands for humanoid robots. This study redefines action generation as a conditional generation problem, utilizing diffusion models or flow matching methods to sample executable trajectories from multimodal action distributions, thereby overcoming the limitations of traditional imitation learning in action diversity. Its main contribution lies in constructing an end-to-end system from motion tracking to diverse whole-body control, supporting various training paradigms such as behavior cloning, hierarchical expert strategies, and whole-body composition strategies.

(a) Research Background and Problem: Whole-body control of humanoid robots has long faced two major challenges: first, how to efficiently extract transferable motion patterns from human demonstrations, and second, how to generate action sequences that balance stability and diversity. Traditional methods often rely on precise kinematic modeling or predefined action libraries, making it difficult to meet real-time adaptation requirements in complex environments. The core problem of BeyondMimic is how to convert the multimodal action distributions inherent in human videos or motion capture data into physically consistent whole-body trajectories executable by the robot.

In specific implementation, the system supports multiple training strategies: ACT (Action Chunking Transformers) and behavior cloning for basic imitation learning, diffusion strategies and flow matching for handling multimodal distributions, and hierarchical skill/expert strategy training for decomposing complex tasks into sub-skills. The final output includes whole-body trajectories, action sequences, and target parameters for low-level controllers.

(c) Key Technical Innovations: The core innovation of BeyondMimic lies in treating action generation as a conditional generation problem rather than simple regression or classification. Through the stepwise denoising process of diffusion, the model can generate multiple reasonable action trajectories from the same initial state, significantly enhancing action diversity and robustness. Additionally, the framework supports flexible switching from single motion tracking to combined whole-body strategies, allowing the same architecture to handle both simple gait control and complex manipulation tasks requiring coordination of upper and lower limbs. This uniformity avoids the cumbersome process of designing dedicated controllers for different tasks.

Its diffusion-based generation paradigm also provides a feasible path for building future behavioral foundation models, enabling robots to autonomously learn general motor skills from massive human motion data.
