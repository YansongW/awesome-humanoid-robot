## 概述

TextOp 旨在解决仿人机器人运动生成中的数据闭环问题，通过将语言指令、遥操作与外骨骼采集的人类操作数据及机器人状态信息进行整合，利用扩散策略或流匹配方法，结合全身控制器（WBC/MPC），将多模态数据转化为可训练、可复用的全身轨迹与动作序列。该工作的核心贡献在于将动作生成建模为条件生成问题，从而能够在多模态动作分布中高效采样可执行轨迹，实现实时、交互式的文本驱动运动控制。

当前仿人机器人运动生成面临两大挑战：一是如何有效利用人类示范数据与机器人状态信息构建闭环数据流，二是如何将自然语言指令转化为可执行的全身运动。传统方法往往依赖手工设计的运动库或预编程轨迹，难以适应复杂、多变的交互场景。TextOp 针对这一问题，提出将运动生成视为条件生成问题，利用扩散模型或流匹配技术，从多模态动作分布中采样出符合语言指令的全身轨迹，从而打通从语言理解到运动执行的完整链路。

在方法框架上，TextOp 构建了一个端到端的实时运动生成与控制系统。系统首先通过遥操作或外骨骼设备采集人类操作数据，同时记录机器人状态信息。这些数据被转化为统一的轨迹表示，并作为训练数据输入扩散策略或流匹配模型。模型以语言指令为条件，在动作分布中采样出全身关节轨迹，随后由全身控制器（WBC）或模型预测控制（MPC）进行底层跟踪与稳定控制。这一框架实现了从数据采集、模型训练到实时控制的数据闭环。

关键技术创新体现在三个层面：其一，将扩散策略与流匹配引入仿人机器人运动生成，使得模型能够处理多模态、高维度的动作分布，生成自然且多样化的运动；其二，通过语言指令作为条件输入，实现了零样本或小样本下的运动泛化，用户无需编程即可通过自然语言控制机器人执行复杂动作；其三，结合全身控制器与模型预测控制，保证了生成轨迹的物理可行性与实时性，使得系统能够在动态环境中稳定运行。

在实验验证方面，TextOp 在多种仿人机器人平台上进行了测试，涵盖行走、抓取、姿态调整等典型任务。实验结果表明，系统能够根据语言指令实时生成连贯的全身运动，并在遥操作数据有限的情况下仍保持较高的生成质量。此外，通过与纯运动学方法对比，TextOp 在轨迹平滑度、任务完成率以及人机交互自然度上均展现出显著优势。该工作为未来人机协作、家庭服务等场景下的仿人机器人应用提供了可行的技术路径。

## Overview

TextOp aims to address the data closed-loop problem in humanoid robot motion generation. By integrating language instructions, teleoperation, human operation data collected via exoskeletons, and robot state information, it utilizes diffusion policies or flow matching methods, combined with whole-body controllers (WBC/MPC), to transform multimodal data into trainable and reusable whole-body trajectories and action sequences. The core contribution of this work lies in modeling motion generation as a conditional generation problem, enabling efficient sampling of executable trajectories from multimodal action distributions, thereby achieving real-time, interactive text-driven motion control.

## Content

Current humanoid robot motion generation faces two major challenges: first, how to effectively utilize human demonstration data and robot state information to construct a closed-loop data flow; second, how to convert natural language instructions into executable whole-body motions. Traditional methods often rely on manually designed motion libraries or pre-programmed trajectories, making it difficult to adapt to complex and dynamic interaction scenarios. To address this, TextOp proposes treating motion generation as a conditional generation problem, using diffusion models or flow matching techniques to sample whole-body trajectories that align with language instructions from multimodal action distributions, thereby bridging the complete pipeline from language understanding to motion execution.

In terms of the methodological framework, TextOp constructs an end-to-end real-time motion generation and control system. The system first collects human operation data via teleoperation or exoskeleton devices while simultaneously recording robot state information. This data is transformed into a unified trajectory representation and fed as training data into diffusion policy or flow matching models. The model, conditioned on language instructions, samples whole-body joint trajectories from the action distribution, which are then tracked and stabilized at a low level by a whole-body controller (WBC) or model predictive control (MPC). This framework achieves a data closed loop encompassing data collection, model training, and real-time control.

Key technological innovations are reflected in three aspects: First, introducing diffusion policies and flow matching into humanoid robot motion generation allows the model to handle multimodal, high-dimensional action distributions, producing natural and diverse motions. Second, using language instructions as conditional input enables zero-shot or few-shot motion generalization, allowing users to control the robot to perform complex actions via natural language without programming. Third, integrating whole-body controllers with model predictive control ensures the physical feasibility and real-time performance of the generated trajectories, enabling stable operation in dynamic environments.

In terms of experimental validation, TextOp has been tested on various humanoid robot platforms, covering typical tasks such as walking, grasping, and posture adjustment. Experimental results show that the system can generate coherent whole-body motions in real time based on language instructions and maintain high generation quality even with limited teleoperation data. Furthermore, compared to purely kinematic methods, TextOp demonstrates significant advantages in trajectory smoothness, task completion rate, and naturalness of human-robot interaction. This work provides a feasible technical pathway for future applications of humanoid robots in scenarios such as human-robot collaboration and home service.

From an application perspective, TextOp lowers the barrier to programming humanoid robot motions, enabling non-expert users to interact with robots via natural language. At the same time, its data closed-loop design lays the foundation for continuous learning and model iteration, potentially advancing humanoid robots from the laboratory to practical applications. This research holds significant demonstrative value at the intersection of artificial intelligence and robotics, particularly offering a systematic solution for language-driven whole-body motion generation and control.
