Traditional methods rely on manually designed motion libraries or pre-programmed trajectories, making them difficult to adapt to complex and dynamic real-world environments. While reinforcement learning-based methods offer generalization capabilities, they incur high training costs and struggle to guarantee real-time performance. Addressing this bottleneck, TextOp proposes using language instructions as high-level semantic input, combined with human demonstration data collected via teleoperation or exoskeletons, to construct an end-to-end motion generation and control closed-loop, thereby reducing reliance on manual coding and enhancing system flexibility and scalability.

The core framework of TextOp comprises three key modules: the data acquisition layer, the motion generation layer, and the control execution layer. The control execution layer then converts the generated trajectories into low-level control objectives through a whole-body controller (WBC) or model predictive controller (MPC), driving the robot's joint motors to achieve stable motion. The entire process forms an iteratively optimizable data closed-loop, allowing new data from each execution to be fed back into the generative model, continuously improving motion quality.

Finally, by integrating a whole-body controller with the generative model, it achieves real-time conversion from high-level semantics to low-level control, maintaining motion naturalness while ensuring the robot's dynamic balance and tracking accuracy.
