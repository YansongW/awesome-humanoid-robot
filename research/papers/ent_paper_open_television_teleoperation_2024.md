**研究背景与问题**：人形机器人的行为学习高度依赖大规模、高质量的示范数据，而遥操作是获取此类数据的主要手段。然而，传统遥操作系统存在两大瓶颈：一是视觉反馈通常为被动式，操作者无法像在真实环境中那样主动调整视角，导致对深度和空间关系的感知受限；二是采集到的数据（如关节序列、末端执行器轨迹）往往与特定机器人平台强耦合，难以直接迁移或复用。Open-TeleVision 正是针对这一“数据闭环”困境，旨在构建一个既能提供沉浸式主动视觉反馈、又能将原始操作数据转化为通用可训练格式的开放框架。

**方法或模型框架**：Open-TeleVision 的系统架构围绕数据采集与动作生成两条主线展开。在数据采集端，系统通过多视角相机图像、本体状态传感器以及外骨骼设备，同步记录人类操作者的动作与机器人状态，形成包含视觉观测、关节序列和末端执行器目标的完整数据包。在动作生成端，系统采用行为克隆（ACT）作为基础模仿学习范式，并引入扩散策略或流匹配技术，将动作生成建模为条件生成问题。具体而言，模型以多模态观测（如相机图像、本体状态）为条件，通过扩散过程或流匹配在动作分布中逐步去噪，最终采样出平滑且物理可行的关节位置/力矩命令或腕手目标。

**关键技术创新**：该工作的创新点主要体现在三个方面。第一，沉浸式主动视觉反馈机制允许操作者通过头戴式显示器或可旋转摄像头实时调整观察视角，显著提升了对复杂操作任务（如抓取、装配）的远程控制精度。第二，将动作生成从确定性映射转变为概率生成问题，利用扩散模型或流匹配处理多模态动作分布中的不确定性，使系统能够从同一观测状态生成多种合理的执行轨迹，增强了策略的鲁棒性与泛化能力。第三，采用 MM-DiT（多模态扩散 Transformer）或标准 Transformer 作为动作头，将原始关节序列编码为可复用的动作块（action chunk/token），实现了数据格式与机器人硬件平台的解耦，降低了迁移成本。

## Overview

Remote operation is a key technology for obtaining high-quality humanoid robot demonstration data, but existing systems are often limited by the latency and passivity of visual feedback, making it difficult to capture the fine-grained interaction dynamics between the operator and the robot. The Open-TeleVision system proposed in this paper addresses the synchronization and representation challenges between human operation and robot state in the data loop by constructing an immersive active visual feedback mechanism. The core contribution of this work is to redefine action generation as a conditional generation problem, utilizing diffusion models or flow matching methods to sample executable trajectories from multimodal action distributions, thereby achieving efficient transformation from teleoperation data to reusable robot control commands.

## Content

**Research Background and Problem**: Behavioral learning for humanoid robots heavily relies on large-scale, high-quality demonstration data, with teleoperation being the primary means of acquiring such data. However, traditional teleoperation systems suffer from two major bottlenecks: first, visual feedback is typically passive, preventing operators from actively adjusting their perspective as they would in a real environment, leading to limited perception of depth and spatial relationships; second, the collected data (e.g., joint sequences, end-effector trajectories) is often tightly coupled with specific robot platforms, making it difficult to directly transfer or reuse. Open-TeleVision is specifically designed to address this "data loop" dilemma, aiming to build an open framework that not only provides immersive active visual feedback but also converts raw operational data into a general, trainable format.

**Method or Model Framework**: The system architecture of Open-TeleVision revolves around two main lines: data collection and action generation. On the data collection side, the system synchronously records the human operator's actions and the robot's state through multi-view camera images, proprioceptive state sensors, and exoskeleton devices, forming a complete data package containing visual observations, joint sequences, and end-effector targets. On the action generation side, the system adopts behavioral cloning (ACT) as the foundational imitation learning paradigm and introduces diffusion policies or flow matching techniques to model action generation as a conditional generation problem. Specifically, the model conditions on multimodal observations (e.g., camera images, proprioceptive states) and progressively denoises within the action distribution through a diffusion process or flow matching, ultimately sampling smooth and physically feasible joint position/torque commands or wrist-hand targets.

**Key Technical Innovations**: The innovations of this work are mainly reflected in three aspects. First, the immersive active visual feedback mechanism allows operators to adjust their viewing perspective in real time via head-mounted displays or rotatable cameras, significantly improving remote control precision for complex manipulation tasks (e.g., grasping, assembly). Second, action generation is transformed from a deterministic mapping to a probabilistic generation problem, using diffusion models or flow matching to handle uncertainty in multimodal action distributions, enabling the system to generate multiple reasonable execution trajectories from the same observation state, thereby enhancing policy robustness and generalization. Third, employing MM-DiT (Multimodal Diffusion Transformer) or standard Transformer as the action head encodes raw joint sequences into reusable action chunks/tokens, achieving decoupling of data format from the robot hardware platform and reducing transfer costs.

**Experiments/Validation and Application Value**: Although the paper abstract does not disclose specific experimental data, it can be inferred from the framework design that Open-TeleVision has significant application potential in typical humanoid robot manipulation tasks (e.g., object transportation, precision assembly). By comparing task completion time and success rates with and without active visual feedback, the improvement in operational efficiency due to immersive vision can be validated; by evaluating differences in action diversity and trajectory smoothness between diffusion policies and behavioral cloning, the advantages of generative methods in handling multimodal action distributions can be demonstrated. The open architecture of this system enables it to serve as a universal platform for data collection and policy training, supporting dataset construction, algorithm benchmarking, and cross-platform transfer research in the field of humanoid robotics, holding substantial engineering and academic value.
