## Overview

This paper proposes UniTracker, a general whole-body motion tracking framework for humanoid robots, aimed at addressing the efficient mapping between multi-source observation information and whole-body control strategies. By fusing visual, proprioceptive, and simulation interaction data, the study constructs trackable body target representations and employs a teacher-student knowledge distillation paradigm to transfer the capabilities of a teacher policy, which relies on privileged information, to a student policy that depends only on deployable observations. The core contribution of UniTracker lies in unifying various motion tracking and whole-body control methods (such as PPO/RL, diffusion policy, and flow matching training), providing a scalable intelligent foundation for balance, locomotion, and whole-body coordination of humanoid robots in complex environments.

(a) Research Background and Problem: Whole-body motion control for humanoid robots faces multiple challenges, including heterogeneous observation information, diverse motion patterns, and high real-time requirements. Existing methods typically design specialized trackers for specific tasks (e.g., walking, grasping), making it difficult to achieve unity between whole-body coordination and dynamic balance. Furthermore, sensor noise and latency in real-world deployment further exacerbate the difficulty of policy generalization. To address these issues, UniTracker proposes a general tracking paradigm aimed at uniformly mapping multimodal observations (including camera images, body states, and joint sequences) into trackable body targets, thereby supporting seamless transfer from simulation to reality.

(b) Method or Model Framework: The overall framework of UniTracker consists of three core stages: First, by fusing multi-view observations and body states, a trackable target representation containing whole-body poses and contact information is generated. Second, privileged information (such as ground reaction forces and future states) is used to train a teacher policy, which makes optimal control decisions based on the complete environmental state. Finally, through knowledge distillation, the capabilities of the teacher policy are compressed into a student policy, enabling it to output whole-body trajectories and low-level controller commands relying only on observations available during deployment (e.g., monocular vision and joint encoders). The framework supports multiple policy training paradigms, including PPO/RL, diffusion policy, and flow matching training, which can be combined into a unified whole-body policy.

(c) Key Technical Innovations: The core innovation of UniTracker lies in the combination of its "general tracker" design concept and the teacher-student distillation mechanism. Additionally, by introducing diffusion policy and flow matching training into whole-body control, UniTracker can generate smooth motion sequences that comply with dynamic constraints, significantly outperforming traditional optimization-based methods. The distillation process also preserves the teacher policy's robustness to sudden disturbances, enhancing the reliability of the student policy when deployed on real robots.
