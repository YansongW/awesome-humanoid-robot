Existing research mostly focuses on single-arm grasping or simple collaborative tasks, struggling to handle complex scenarios that require bimanual coordination, precise force control, and dynamic adaptability. This paper proposes a dual-arm dexterous manipulation framework for humanoid robots, integrating advanced teleoperation data collection and imitation learning strategies to overcome the limitations in precision and robustness of bimanual coordination in complex tasks. This work provides crucial algorithmic and system support for enhancing the practical deployment of humanoid robots in manufacturing, service, and household environments.
