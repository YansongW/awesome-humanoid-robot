## 概述

EgoHumanoid 提出了一种无需机器人本体参与的、基于第一视角人类示范的野外移动操作学习框架，旨在解决人形机器人在复杂非结构化环境中执行全身协调操作任务的数据获取与泛化难题。该工作的核心贡献在于构建了一条从多模态人类示范数据到机器人动作预测的端到端通路，通过保留视觉语言模型的语义理解能力并融合机器人状态与动作头，突破了传统方法仅停留在语言规划层面的局限，为野外场景下的全身移动操作提供了可扩展的解决方案。

研究背景与问题：当前人形机器人的移动操作能力在实验室受控环境中已取得显著进展，但面对野外非结构化场景时，仍面临数据获取成本高、示范多样性不足、以及视觉-动作映射泛化性差等关键挑战。传统方法依赖遥操作或外骨骼设备收集机器人本体数据，不仅限制了数据规模，也使得示范行为与机器人运动学特性深度绑定，难以迁移至不同形态的机器人平台。EgoHumanoid 针对这一问题，提出利用人类第一视角视频和动捕轨迹作为示范源，从根本上解耦了数据收集与机器人硬件，从而大幅降低了野外场景数据采集的门槛。

方法或模型框架：EgoHumanoid 的整体框架采用多模态编码-预测范式。首先，系统将相机图像、多视角观测、人类视频与动捕轨迹、以及遥操作/外骨骼数据统一编码为多模态表征。在此基础上，分别采用 ACT（Action Chunking with Transformers）行为克隆模仿学习与 VLA（Vision-Language-Action）多模态动作模型，对地形与场景表征进行预测。该框架的关键设计在于不直接丢弃视觉语言模型（VLM）的语义理解能力，而是将其作为高层次场景推理的骨干网络，同时在其基础上新增机器人状态编码器和动作预测头，使得模型既能理解“场景是什么”，又能输出“机器人应如何运动”。

## Overview

EgoHumanoid proposes a learning framework for in-the-wild mobile manipulation based on first-person human demonstrations, without requiring robot hardware involvement. It aims to address the challenges of data acquisition and generalization for humanoid robots performing whole-body coordinated manipulation tasks in complex unstructured environments. The core contribution of this work is the construction of an end-to-end pipeline from multimodal human demonstration data to robot action prediction. By preserving the semantic understanding capabilities of vision-language models and integrating robot state encoders with action heads, it breaks through the limitation of traditional methods that only operate at the language planning level, offering a scalable solution for whole-body mobile manipulation in in-the-wild scenarios.

## Content

Research Background and Problem: Current mobile manipulation capabilities of humanoid robots have achieved significant progress in controlled laboratory environments. However, when facing unstructured in-the-wild scenarios, key challenges remain, including high data acquisition costs, insufficient demonstration diversity, and poor generalization of vision-to-action mapping. Traditional methods rely on teleoperation or exoskeleton devices to collect robot-specific data, which not only limits data scale but also deeply binds demonstration behaviors to robot kinematic characteristics, making it difficult to transfer across different robot platforms. To address this, EgoHumanoid proposes using human first-person videos and motion capture trajectories as demonstration sources, fundamentally decoupling data collection from robot hardware, thereby significantly lowering the barrier to in-the-wild data acquisition.

Method or Model Framework: The overall framework of EgoHumanoid adopts a multimodal encoding-prediction paradigm. First, the system uniformly encodes camera images, multi-view observations, human videos and motion capture trajectories, as well as teleoperation/exoskeleton data into multimodal representations. On this basis, it employs ACT (Action Chunking with Transformers) behavioral cloning imitation learning and VLA (Vision-Language-Action) multimodal action models to predict terrain and scene representations. The key design of this framework is that it does not discard the semantic understanding capability of the vision-language model (VLM); instead, it retains the VLM as a backbone for high-level scene reasoning while adding a robot state encoder and action prediction head on top. This allows the model to both understand "what the scene is" and output "how the robot should move."

Key Technical Innovation: The core innovation of EgoHumanoid lies in its "semantic-action joint learning" strategy. Traditional VLA methods often use vision-language models only to generate language instructions or planning sequences. In contrast, EgoHumanoid preserves the complete semantic understanding branch of the VLM and introduces a learnable action head, achieving direct mapping from scene semantics to continuous action space. Additionally, this work designs a cross-modal alignment mechanism that aligns first-person visual features from human demonstrations with robot proprioceptive features in a latent space, effectively bridging the morphological and kinematic differences between humans and robots. This design enables the model to make reasonable manipulation decisions for unseen terrains and object layouts under zero-shot or few-shot conditions.

Experimental Validation and Application Value: Although the paper does not disclose specific quantitative experimental data, based on its technical approach and domain positioning, it can be inferred that EgoHumanoid has significant application potential in in-the-wild mobile manipulation tasks. By leveraging large amounts of human daily activity videos as demonstration sources, this method is expected to achieve efficient policy learning in typical scenarios such as carrying objects, opening doors, and crossing obstacles. Moreover, since the data collection process does not require robot hardware involvement, the framework naturally supports cross-platform transfer and can be applied to humanoid robots of different sizes and configurations. At the engineering level, EgoHumanoid provides a feasible path for constructing large-scale, low-cost, and high-diversity in-the-wild manipulation datasets, which is of great significance for advancing the deployment of humanoid robots in real-world scenarios such as service, rescue, and exploration.
