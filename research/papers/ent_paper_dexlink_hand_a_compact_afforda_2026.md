## Overview

The design of dexterous hands for humanoid robots has long faced a trade-off between high degrees of freedom, compact structure, and manufacturing cost. Existing solutions mostly adopt direct-drive motors or tendon-driven mechanisms. The former leads to bulky hand sizes, while the latter suffers from issues such as low transmission efficiency and complex maintenance. DexLink Hand proposes a compact 16-degree-of-freedom dexterous hand design based on linkage transmission, aiming to achieve human-like dexterous manipulation capabilities at a lower cost. Through innovative mechanical structure and transmission layout, this research provides a feasible hardware solution for the large-scale application of robot dexterous hands in service and collaboration scenarios.

The linkage mechanism design is based on kinematic optimization to ensure that the range of motion for each joint approaches the physiological limits of the human hand.

(c) Key Technical Innovations  
The core innovation of this research lies in the compact and modular design of the linkage transmission system. Additionally, linkage transmission offers advantages such as zero backlash, high stiffness, and long lifespan, avoiding common issues like slack and wear found in tendon-driven systems. The design also incorporates an adjustable pre-tensioning mechanism, facilitating calibration of joint motion accuracy during assembly and maintenance. These innovations enable the dexterous hand to maintain high performance with 16 DOFs while achieving significantly lower manufacturing costs compared to similar direct-drive motor solutions.
