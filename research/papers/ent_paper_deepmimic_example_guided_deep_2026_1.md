## 概述

本文提出了一种基于示例引导的深度强化学习框架DeepMimic，旨在解决人形机器人在物理仿真环境中学习复杂运动技能的关键难题。传统强化学习方法难以直接生成逼真且鲁棒的角色动作，而基于运动捕捉数据的模仿学习又面临物理一致性与多样性不足的挑战。该研究通过将动作示例作为引导信号融入深度强化学习训练过程，使物理角色能够高效习得包括后空翻、侧手翻等高动态技能，为人形机器人的运动控制与技能迁移提供了新的范式。

## 核心内容

（a）研究背景与问题：人形机器人及物理仿真角色的运动控制一直是机器人学与计算机图形学交叉领域的研究热点。尽管深度强化学习在游戏、导航等任务中取得了显著成功，但在高维连续动作空间下的物理角色控制中，直接使用稀疏奖励信号往往导致训练效率低下，且难以生成自然流畅的运动模式。现有基于运动捕捉数据的模仿学习方法虽能生成逼真动作，但通常缺乏对物理环境的适应能力，在遇到扰动或新场景时容易失效。因此，如何将示例数据的先验知识与强化学习的探索能力有机结合，成为提升物理角色技能学习效果的关键问题。

（b）方法或模型框架：DeepMimic框架的核心是一个结合了模仿奖励与任务奖励的深度强化学习算法。该算法采用近端策略优化（PPO）作为基础优化器，并引入一个由运动示例数据驱动的奖励函数。具体而言，模仿奖励通过计算当前角色姿态与参考示例姿态之间的加权距离来量化动作相似度，从而引导策略网络生成与示例接近的运动序列。同时，任务奖励（如保持平衡、达到目标高度）确保角色在模仿基础上完成具体任务目标。整个框架在MuJoCo物理引擎上实现，支持多种人形角色模型与复杂地形环境。

（c）关键技术创新：本文的主要创新体现在三个方面。其一，提出了一种基于参考状态初始化（reference state initialization）的训练策略，通过在每次训练回合开始时将角色置于示例动作的某个中间状态，有效缓解了强化学习在长周期动作序列中的探索困难问题。其二，设计了一种自适应奖励权重调节机制，能够根据训练阶段动态平衡模仿奖励与任务奖励的贡献，避免早期过度依赖示例而导致泛化能力不足。其三，引入了一种基于相位变量的时间对齐方法，使策略网络能够学习到与示例动作节奏相匹配的控制策略，显著提升了高动态技能（如空翻）的学习成功率。

（d）实验/验证或应用价值：作者在多种复杂运动技能上进行了验证，包括行走、跑步、跳跃、后空翻及侧手翻等。实验结果表明，DeepMimic能够在数小时内训练出能够稳定执行这些技能的物理角色，且对地面摩擦系数变化、外力扰动等环境干扰表现出较强的鲁棒性。与纯强化学习方法相比，该框架将高动态技能的学习成功率提升了数倍；与纯模仿学习方法相比，生成的动作在物理真实性与任务完成率上均有显著优势。该工作为人形机器人的运动技能学习提供了可复现的技术路线，也为后续研究如技能迁移、多任务学习及真实机器人部署奠定了基础。

## Overview

This paper proposes an example-guided deep reinforcement learning framework, DeepMimic, aimed at addressing the key challenge of humanoid robots learning complex motor skills in physical simulation environments. Traditional reinforcement learning methods struggle to directly generate realistic and robust character motions, while imitation learning based on motion capture data faces challenges of insufficient physical consistency and diversity. By integrating motion examples as guiding signals into the deep reinforcement learning training process, this research enables physical characters to efficiently acquire highly dynamic skills such as backflips and cartwheels, providing a new paradigm for motion control and skill transfer in humanoid robots.

## Content

(a) Research Background and Problem: Motion control of humanoid robots and physically simulated characters has been a research hotspot at the intersection of robotics and computer graphics. Although deep reinforcement learning has achieved significant success in tasks such as games and navigation, directly using sparse reward signals in high-dimensional continuous action spaces for physical character control often leads to low training efficiency and difficulty in generating natural and smooth motion patterns. Existing imitation learning methods based on motion capture data can produce realistic motions but typically lack adaptability to physical environments, failing easily when encountering perturbations or new scenarios. Therefore, how to organically combine the prior knowledge of example data with the exploration capability of reinforcement learning has become a key issue in improving the skill learning effectiveness of physical characters.

(b) Method or Model Framework: The core of the DeepMimic framework is a deep reinforcement learning algorithm that combines imitation rewards with task rewards. This algorithm uses Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) as the base optimizer and introduces a reward function driven by motion example data. Specifically, the imitation reward quantifies motion similarity by calculating the weighted distance between the current character pose and the reference example pose, thereby guiding the policy network to generate motion sequences close to the examples. Meanwhile, task rewards (e.g., maintaining balance, reaching a target height) ensure that the character completes specific task objectives based on imitation. The entire framework is implemented on the MuJoCo physics engine, supporting various humanoid character models and complex terrain environments.

(c) Key Technical Innovations: The main innovations of this paper are reflected in three aspects. First, a training strategy based on reference state initialization is proposed, which places the character at an intermediate state of the example motion at the beginning of each training episode, effectively alleviating the exploration difficulty of reinforcement learning in long-duration motion sequences. Second, an adaptive reward weight adjustment mechanism is designed to dynamically balance the contributions of imitation rewards and task rewards according to the training stage, preventing early over-reliance on examples that could lead to insufficient generalization. Third, a phase variable-based temporal alignment method is introduced, enabling the policy network to learn control strategies that match the rhythm of the example motions, significantly improving the learning success rate of highly dynamic skills (e.g., flips).

(d) Experiments/Validation or Application Value: The authors validated the framework on various complex motor skills, including walking, running, jumping, backflips, and cartwheels. Experimental results show that DeepMimic can train physical characters to stably perform these skills within hours, demonstrating strong robustness to environmental disturbances such as changes in ground friction coefficients and external forces. Compared to pure reinforcement learning methods, this framework improves the learning success rate of highly dynamic skills by several times; compared to pure imitation learning methods, the generated motions exhibit significant advantages in both physical realism and task completion rate. This work provides a reproducible technical pathway for motor skill learning in humanoid robots and lays the foundation for subsequent research such as skill transfer, multi-task learning, and deployment on real robots.
