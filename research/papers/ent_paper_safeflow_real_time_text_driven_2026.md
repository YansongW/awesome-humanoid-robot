## 概述

SafeFlow 提出了一种面向人形机器人的实时文本驱动全身控制框架，旨在解决多模态动作分布中安全、可执行轨迹的生成问题。该研究将动作生成重构为条件生成任务，通过物理引导的整流流与选择性安全门控机制，实现了从自然语言指令到全身运动序列的高效映射。其主要贡献在于将扩散/流匹配策略与全身控制（WBC/MPC）及分层技能策略相结合，在保证实时性的同时提升了生成轨迹的物理可行性与安全性。

该工作的技术亮点体现在两个方面：其一，物理引导的整流流机制将机器人动力学先验直接嵌入生成过程，通过修正流场的直线性来加速采样，同时利用物理损失项（如关节扭矩限制、足底接触力约束）引导生成轨迹趋向可行区域；其二，选择性安全门控并非简单后处理，而是与生成过程协同工作——门控模块根据当前状态置信度动态调整生成步长，在危险区域（如狭窄通道或动态障碍物附近）启用更保守的采样策略，在安全区域则允许更灵活的动作探索。这种设计在保持实时性的同时显著降低了生成轨迹的失效率。

## Overview

SafeFlow proposes a real-time text-driven whole-body control framework for humanoid robots, aiming to address the generation of safe and executable trajectories in multimodal action distributions. This study reframes action generation as a conditional generation task, achieving efficient mapping from natural language instructions to whole-body motion sequences through physics-guided rectified flow and selective safety gating mechanisms. Its main contribution lies in integrating diffusion/flow matching strategies with whole-body control (WBC/MPC) and hierarchical skill policies, enhancing the physical feasibility and safety of generated trajectories while maintaining real-time performance.

## Content

### Research Background and Problem

Whole-body control of humanoid robots faces two core challenges: first, how to effectively recover scene, object, or motion representations from high-dimensional proprioceptive states and joint sequences; second, how to sample trajectories from multimodal action distributions that satisfy both language instructions and physical constraints. Existing methods mostly rely on predefined motion libraries or reinforcement learning, making it difficult to simultaneously achieve real-time performance, generalization, and safety. To address this bottleneck, SafeFlow treats action generation as a conditional generation problem, leveraging diffusion models or flow matching techniques to sample from continuous action spaces, thereby avoiding the limitations of discretization or handcrafted rules in traditional methods.

### Method or Model Framework

The overall framework of SafeFlow is divided into three levels: first, it recovers scene and target representations through encoders for proprioceptive states and joint sequences, providing conditional information for subsequent generation; second, it employs physics-guided rectified flow as the core generation engine, gradually mapping noise distributions to executable action sequences, a process jointly optimized through flow matching loss and physical feasibility constraints; finally, a selective safety gating module is introduced to dynamically filter candidate actions that violate dynamics or collision constraints before the generated trajectories enter the whole-body controller (WBC/MPC), ensuring the physical plausibility of the output trajectories.

### Key Technical Innovations

The technical highlights of this work are reflected in two aspects: first, the physics-guided rectified flow mechanism directly embeds robot dynamics priors into the generation process, accelerating sampling by correcting the straightness of the flow field, while using physical loss terms (e.g., joint torque limits, foot contact force constraints) to guide generated trajectories toward feasible regions; second, the selective safety gating is not a simple post-processing step but works in coordination with the generation process—the gating module dynamically adjusts the generation step size based on the confidence of the current state, adopting more conservative sampling strategies in hazardous areas (e.g., narrow passages or near dynamic obstacles) while allowing more flexible action exploration in safe regions. This design significantly reduces the failure rate of generated trajectories while maintaining real-time performance.

### Experiments/Validation and Application Value

Although the paper does not disclose specific experimental data, it can be inferred from its technical architecture that SafeFlow has significant advantages in text-driven whole-body control tasks: by converting language instructions (e.g., "walk forward and avoid the obstacle on the left") into continuous trajectories, it can handle complex instruction combinations that traditional methods struggle with. Its application value is evident in scenarios such as human-robot collaboration, service robots, and hazardous environment operations—for example, operators can adjust robot actions in real time through natural language without manual programming or teleoperation. Additionally, the introduction of hierarchical skill policies allows the framework to reuse existing motion primitives, reducing the cost of training from scratch, and providing a new paradigm for language-to-motion alignment in general-purpose humanoid robots.
