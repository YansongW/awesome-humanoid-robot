## 概述

人形机器人在复杂环境中执行自然语言指令时，面临高层任务规划与低层运动控制之间难以无缝衔接的挑战。现有方法通常将语言理解、运动生成与全身控制视为独立模块，导致系统存在明显的“断点”，影响执行效率与鲁棒性。UniAct 提出一种统一的运动生成与动作流框架，通过将语言指令转化为可跟踪的身体目标，并将策略网络与控制模块整合至同一训练与部署链路，从而减少高层目标到低层动作之间的转换损失，实现端到端的全身轨迹输出与低层控制器目标生成。

UniAct 的核心框架包含两个关键组件：语言到身体目标的映射模块与全身策略网络。首先，系统将自然语言指令解析为可量化的身体目标，例如末端执行器位姿、重心位置或关节角度约束。随后，策略网络基于这些目标生成全身轨迹或动作序列，而控制模块则进一步将其转化为低层控制器（如 PD 控制器或力矩控制）可直接执行的参考信号。这一设计的关键创新在于，策略网络与控制模块被置于同一训练与部署链中，使得网络在训练阶段即可感知控制器的动态特性与物理约束，从而在推理时生成更符合实际执行条件的动作。

在技术创新层面，UniAct 特别强调了“平衡”与“运动跟踪”两大能力。通过引入行为基础模型（Behavioral Foundation Model）的思想，系统能够在大量示范数据上预训练一个通用的全身控制先验，再针对具体任务进行微调。这使得 UniAct 不仅能够处理静态姿态生成，还能在动态行走、抓取与避障等任务中保持全身协调与稳定性。此外，框架支持动作流式生成，即根据实时传感器反馈逐步调整输出轨迹，从而增强对外部扰动的鲁棒性。

实验验证方面，UniAct 在多种仿真与真实人形机器人平台上进行了评估，涵盖语言引导的行走、物体操作与全身平衡恢复等任务。结果表明，相较于将高层规划与低层控制分离的基线方法，UniAct 在任务成功率、运动平滑度与执行延迟上均有显著提升。特别是在需要快速响应环境变化的场景中，统一训练链路带来的端到端优化优势尤为明显。该工作为人形机器人从“语言理解”到“物理执行”的全链路自动化提供了可行的技术路径，具有重要的工程应用价值。

## Overview

When humanoid robots execute natural language instructions in complex environments, they face the challenge of seamlessly bridging high-level task planning and low-level motion control. Existing methods typically treat language understanding, motion generation, and whole-body control as independent modules, leading to obvious "gaps" in the system that affect execution efficiency and robustness. UniAct proposes a unified motion generation and action flow framework. By converting language instructions into trackable body targets and integrating the policy network with the control module into a single training and deployment pipeline, it reduces the conversion loss from high-level goals to low-level actions, enabling end-to-end whole-body trajectory output and low-level controller target generation.

## Content

Whole-body control of humanoid robots has long suffered from the problem of module fragmentation in the "perception-planning-execution" pipeline. In traditional methods, language instructions are first parsed into high-level task descriptions, then a motion planner generates joint trajectories, which are finally executed by a low-level controller. This process can introduce error accumulation and delays at each stage. Especially in scenarios requiring dynamic balance and multi-task coordination, the mapping gap between high-level semantics and low-level dynamics is particularly prominent. UniAct is proposed precisely to bridge this gap, integrating language understanding, motion generation, and whole-body control into a single end-to-end optimizable system through unified modeling.

The core framework of UniAct comprises two key components: a language-to-body-target mapping module and a whole-body policy network. First, the system parses natural language instructions into quantifiable body targets, such as end-effector poses, center-of-mass positions, or joint angle constraints. Subsequently, the policy network generates whole-body trajectories or action sequences based on these targets, while the control module further converts them into reference signals that can be directly executed by low-level controllers (e.g., PD controllers or torque control). The key innovation of this design is that the policy network and the control module are placed within the same training and deployment pipeline, allowing the network to perceive the dynamic characteristics and physical constraints of the controller during training, thereby generating actions that better conform to actual execution conditions during inference.

In terms of technological innovation, UniAct particularly emphasizes two capabilities: "balance" and "motion tracking." By incorporating the concept of a Behavioral Foundation Model, the system can pre-train a general whole-body control prior on a large amount of demonstration data and then fine-tune it for specific tasks. This enables UniAct not only to handle static pose generation but also to maintain whole-body coordination and stability in tasks such as dynamic walking, grasping, and obstacle avoidance. Furthermore, the framework supports streaming action generation, meaning it can gradually adjust the output trajectory based on real-time sensor feedback, thereby enhancing robustness against external disturbances.

In terms of experimental validation, UniAct has been evaluated on various simulated and real humanoid robot platforms, covering tasks such as language-guided walking, object manipulation, and whole-body balance recovery. Results show that compared to baseline methods that separate high-level planning from low-level control, UniAct achieves significant improvements in task success rate, motion smoothness, and execution latency. Particularly in scenarios requiring rapid response to environmental changes, the end-to-end optimization advantage brought by the unified training pipeline is especially evident. This work provides a feasible technical pathway for the full-chain automation of humanoid robots from "language understanding" to "physical execution," holding significant engineering application value.
