## 概述

针对人形机器人在全身移动操作任务中面临的感知与运动控制割裂问题，本文提出WholeBodyVLA框架，旨在通过统一的潜在空间表征实现视觉-语言-动作（VLA）的端到端融合。该工作的核心贡献在于设计了一种多模态编码与动作预测架构，在保留视觉语言模型（VLM）高层语义理解能力的同时，引入机器人状态编码与动作头，使得模型能够直接输出全身轨迹与低层控制器目标，从而突破传统方法仅停留于语言规划层面的局限。

## 核心内容

人形机器人的全身移动操作（whole-body loco-manipulation）要求同时协调上肢操作与下肢移动，这对感知、规划与控制的一体化提出了极高挑战。现有方法通常将视觉感知、语言指令与运动控制分离处理，导致语义理解与物理执行之间存在语义鸿沟。尤其是当机器人需要根据自然语言指令完成复杂操作时，传统流水线方法难以在保持高层语义一致性的同时生成精细的全身运动序列。因此，如何构建一个能够统一处理多模态输入并直接输出可执行动作的端到端模型，成为该领域的关键问题。

WholeBodyVLA提出了一种基于潜在空间统一的VLA框架。其实现路径首先将相机图像/多视角观测、遥操作与外骨骼数据编码为多模态表征，随后通过VLA多模态动作模型预测全身轨迹/动作序列以及低层控制器目标。该框架的核心在于设计了一个共享的潜在空间，使得视觉特征、语言嵌入与机器人状态能够在同一表征空间中交互融合，从而支持从高层语义到底层运动的连续映射。

在技术创新方面，WholeBodyVLA的关键在于对VLM的扩展与适配。传统VLM擅长语义理解与语言规划，但缺乏对机器人运动学与动力学约束的建模能力。为此，该框架在VLM基础上增加了机器人状态编码器与动作头模块，前者负责将关节角度、力矩等状态信息融入潜在表征，后者则直接输出全身轨迹点与控制器目标值。这种设计既保留了VLM对场景与指令的语义理解能力，又赋予了模型生成精确运动指令的能力，避免了仅停留在语言规划层面的局限性。

实验验证方面，WholeBodyVLA在多种全身移动操作任务上进行了评估，包括物体搬运、环境导航与操作协同等场景。通过对比纯语言规划方法与分离式运动控制基线，该框架在任务成功率、动作平滑度与语义一致性等指标上均表现出显著优势。此外，模型对遥操作数据的利用有效降低了真实机器人部署中对大量标注数据的依赖，展现了其在数据效率与泛化能力上的潜力。该工作为人形机器人在复杂动态环境中实现自主操作提供了可行的技术路径，也为后续VLA模型在全身控制领域的应用奠定了方法论基础。

## Overview

To address the issue of the disconnect between perception and motion control in whole-body mobile manipulation tasks for humanoid robots, this paper proposes the WholeBodyVLA framework, aiming to achieve end-to-end integration of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) through a unified latent space representation. The core contribution of this work lies in designing a multimodal encoding and action prediction architecture that preserves the high-level semantic understanding capabilities of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) while introducing robot state encoding and action heads. This enables the model to directly output whole-body trajectories and low-level controller targets, thereby overcoming the limitation of traditional methods that remain confined to language planning.

## Content

Whole-body loco-manipulation for humanoid robots requires the simultaneous coordination of upper-body manipulation and lower-body locomotion, posing significant challenges for the integration of perception, planning, and control. Existing methods typically separate visual perception, language instructions, and motion control, leading to a semantic gap between semantic understanding and physical execution. Particularly when robots need to perform complex operations based on natural language instructions, traditional pipeline approaches struggle to generate precise whole-body motion sequences while maintaining high-level semantic consistency. Therefore, constructing an end-to-end model that can uniformly process multimodal inputs and directly output executable actions has become a key issue in this field.

WholeBodyVLA proposes a VLA framework based on latent space unification. Its implementation first encodes camera images/multi-view observations, teleoperation, and exoskeleton data into multimodal representations, and then predicts whole-body trajectories/action sequences and low-level controller targets through a VLA multimodal action model. The core of this framework lies in designing a shared latent space, where visual features, language embeddings, and robot states can interact and fuse within the same representation space, thereby supporting continuous mapping from high-level semantics to low-level motions.

In terms of technical innovation, the key to WholeBodyVLA lies in the extension and adaptation of VLMs. Traditional VLMs excel at semantic understanding and language planning but lack the ability to model robot kinematic and dynamic constraints. To address this, the framework adds a robot state encoder and action head module on top of the VLM. The former integrates state information such as joint angles and torques into the latent representation, while the latter directly outputs whole-body trajectory points and controller target values. This design preserves the VLM's semantic understanding of scenes and instructions while endowing the model with the ability to generate precise motion commands, avoiding the limitation of remaining solely at the language planning level.

In terms of experimental validation, WholeBodyVLA was evaluated on various whole-body mobile manipulation tasks, including object transportation, environmental navigation, and manipulation coordination. Compared to pure language planning methods and separated motion control baselines, the framework demonstrated significant advantages in metrics such as task success rate, motion smoothness, and semantic consistency. Additionally, the model's use of teleoperation data effectively reduces the reliance on large amounts of labeled data in real robot deployment, showcasing its potential in data efficiency and generalization capabilities. This work provides a feasible technical pathway for humanoid robots to achieve autonomous operation in complex dynamic environments and lays a methodological foundation for the subsequent application of VLA models in whole-body control.
