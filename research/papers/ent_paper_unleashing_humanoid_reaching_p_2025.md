当前人形机器人研究面临的核心挑战之一，是如何在真实世界中实现高效、鲁棒的操作行为。传统方法通常依赖手工设计的运动规划器或强化学习，但这些方法在应对非结构化环境、多模态动作分布以及实时性要求时往往力不从心。尤其关键的是，从数据采集到策略训练再到实际部署的完整闭环难以打通：人类操作数据（如遥操作）与仿真数据之间存在分布偏移，而低层控制器的泛化能力又受限于训练数据的多样性与质量。因此，如何构建一个既能充分利用多源数据，又能生成真实世界可执行轨迹的统一框架，成为该领域亟待解决的关键问题。

本文提出的R2S2框架以“技能空间”为核心概念，将动作生成建模为条件生成问题。具体而言，系统首先通过相机图像、多视角观测、遥操作/外骨骼设备以及仿真交互环境，采集包含人类操作意图与机器人状态的高维数据。随后，利用扩散策略（Diffusion Policy）或流匹配（Flow Matching）技术，在由这些数据构成的多模态动作分布中采样出平滑且物理可行的轨迹。这一过程不仅保留了动作的多样性，还通过条件生成机制（如以视觉观测为条件）实现了对场景语义的深度理解，从而避免了传统方法中“模式坍塌”或“动作抖动”的问题。

在技术创新层面，R2S2的关键在于其分层架构与全身控制（WBC/MPC）的深度融合。框架将高层技能规划与低层运动控制解耦：高层模块负责根据视觉感知结果（如目标物体位置、障碍物分布）选择技能原语（如“抓取”、“放置”），而低层模块则通过扩散模型生成连续关节轨迹，并经由全身控制器（Whole-Body Controller）或模型预测控制（MPC）进行实时优化，确保运动符合动力学约束与安全性要求。这种分层设计使得系统既能应对复杂任务的长时域规划，又能保证底层执行的精确性与鲁棒性。

实验验证方面，尽管本文未公开具体数值结果，但从其框架设计可合理推断，R2S2在移动操作（mobile manipulation）与场景理解（scene understanding）任务中具有显著优势。例如，在需要同时协调双臂与移动底盘的复杂操作场景中，该框架能够利用视觉引导控制（vision-guided control）实时调整动作策略，而扩散模型的随机采样特性则赋予了系统应对环境扰动（如物体位置偏移）的适应性。此外，由于训练数据同时包含真实遥操作与仿真交互数据，模型在零样本迁移至新场景时有望展现出较强的泛化能力。

从应用价值来看，R2S2为人形机器人在家庭服务、工业装配等真实场景中的部署提供了可行的技术路径。其“数据闭环”思想不仅降低了数据采集成本（通过融合低成本仿真数据），还通过生成式模型提升了策略的鲁棒性。未来，该框架可进一步扩展至多机器人协作或人机共融场景，成为连接感知、规划与控制的核心中间件。

## Overview

This paper addresses the data closed-loop challenge faced by humanoid robots in achieving dexterous manipulation in the real world, proposing a framework named "Real-world Ready Skill Space" (R2S2). The core contribution of this research lies in integrating multimodal observation data (camera images, teleoperation and exoskeleton data, simulation interaction data) and leveraging generative models such as Diffusion Policy and Flow Matching to transform complex action generation into a conditional generation problem, thereby constructing trainable and reusable low-level controllers. This approach effectively bridges the gap between simulation and real-world environments, providing key technical support for humanoid robots to transition from the laboratory to practical deployment.

## Content

One of the core challenges currently facing humanoid robot research is how to achieve efficient and robust manipulation behaviors in the real world. Traditional methods typically rely on handcrafted motion planners or reinforcement learning, but these approaches often struggle with unstructured environments, multimodal action distributions, and real-time requirements. Critically, the complete closed loop from data collection to policy training to actual deployment is difficult to achieve: there is a distribution shift between human operation data (e.g., teleoperation) and simulation data, while the generalization ability of low-level controllers is limited by the diversity and quality of training data. Therefore, how to construct a unified framework that can fully utilize multi-source data while generating executable trajectories in the real world has become a key issue to be addressed in this field.

The R2S2 framework proposed in this paper takes "skill space" as its core concept, modeling action generation as a conditional generation problem. Specifically, the system first collects high-dimensional data containing human operation intentions and robot states through camera images, multi-view observations, teleoperation/exoskeleton devices, and simulation interaction environments. Subsequently, using Diffusion Policy or Flow Matching techniques, it samples smooth and physically feasible trajectories from the multimodal action distribution composed of this data. This process not only preserves action diversity but also achieves deep understanding of scene semantics through conditional generation mechanisms (e.g., conditioned on visual observations), thereby avoiding issues like "mode collapse" or "action jitter" found in traditional methods.

In terms of technical innovation, the key to R2S2 lies in its hierarchical architecture and deep integration with Whole-Body Control (WBC/MPC). The framework decouples high-level skill planning from low-level motion control: the high-level module is responsible for selecting skill primitives (e.g., "grasp", "place") based on visual perception results (e.g., target object position, obstacle distribution), while the low-level module generates continuous joint trajectories through diffusion models and performs real-time optimization via a Whole-Body Controller or Model Predictive Control (MPC) to ensure motion adheres to dynamic constraints and safety requirements. This hierarchical design enables the system to handle long-horizon planning for complex tasks while ensuring precision and robustness in low-level execution.

Regarding experimental validation, although this paper does not disclose specific numerical results, it can be reasonably inferred from the framework design that R2S2 has significant advantages in mobile manipulation and scene understanding tasks. For example, in complex manipulation scenarios requiring simultaneous coordination of dual arms and a mobile base, the framework can use vision-guided control to adjust action strategies in real time, while the stochastic sampling characteristics of diffusion models endow the system with adaptability to environmental disturbances (e.g., object position shifts). Additionally, since the training data includes both real teleoperation and simulation interaction data, the model is expected to exhibit strong generalization capabilities when zero-shot transferred to new scenarios.

From an application perspective, R2S2 provides a feasible technical pathway for deploying humanoid robots in real-world scenarios such as home services and industrial assembly. Its "data closed-loop" concept not only reduces data collection costs (by incorporating low-cost simulation data) but also enhances policy robustness through generative models. In the future, this framework can be further extended to multi-robot collaboration or human-robot coexistence scenarios, serving as a core middleware connecting perception, planning, and control.
