## 核心内容

人形机器人的移动操作（loco-manipulation）要求机器人同时协调全身运动与末端执行器的精细操作，这对感知、规划与控制提出了极高要求。传统方法通常依赖手工设计的运动基元或单一模态的模仿学习，难以应对非结构化环境中的多模态输入（如自然语言指令、多视角图像、本体状态等）。此外，动作策略的生成往往面临多模态动作分布建模的难题，即如何在连续高维空间中高效采样出符合任务约束的可执行轨迹。REFINE-DP 正是针对这一核心问题，提出将动作生成重新定义为条件生成问题，从而突破现有方法的瓶颈。

REFINE-DP 的模型框架由三个关键模块构成。首先，多模态编码器将语言指令、相机图像/多视角观测、本体状态与关节序列统一映射为共享表征空间，使机器人能够同时理解语义指令与物理状态。其次，策略学习层融合了强化学习（PPO）、行为克隆（ACT）与扩散策略（Diffusion Policy）三种范式：PPO 用于探索最优动作分布，ACT 提供高效的模仿学习基线，而扩散策略或流匹配则负责从多模态动作分布中采样出平滑且物理可行的关节位置/力矩命令。最后，低层控制器将这些命令转化为精确的电机控制信号，实现从高层规划到底层执行的完整闭环。

该框架的关键技术创新在于将扩散模型引入人形机器人的移动操作微调过程。与直接使用端到端神经网络预测动作不同，REFINE-DP 将动作生成视为一个去噪过程：从随机噪声开始，逐步迭代生成符合当前观测与任务指令的动作序列。这一设计天然支持多模态动作分布的表达，避免了传统高斯假设对复杂动作模式的限制。同时，通过强化学习对扩散策略进行微调，使得模型能够在仿真或真实环境中根据任务奖励信号自适应地优化动作质量，而无需依赖大量人工标注的示范数据。

在实验验证方面，REFINE-DP 在仿真环境中针对多种移动操作任务（如抓取、搬运、开门等）进行了评估。结果表明，与纯模仿学习基线相比，采用扩散策略微调的模型在任务成功率、动作平滑度与泛化能力上均有显著提升。特别是在面对未见过的物体布局或语言指令时，REFINE-DP 能够利用多模态表征的语义理解能力，生成适应新场景的动作轨迹。此外，该框架的模块化设计使其易于迁移至不同的人形机器人平台，为未来在真实物理世界中的部署提供了可行路径。

从应用价值来看，REFINE-DP 为人形机器人在家庭服务、工业装配、灾难救援等需要复杂移动操作能力的场景提供了关键技术支撑。其将视觉-语言-动作（VLA）模型与扩散策略相结合的思想，也推动了世界模型（world model）在机器人学中的实际落地——通过条件生成过程，机器人不仅能够执行预定义动作，还能在动态环境中进行在线推理与适应。这一工作为后续研究如何将大规模预训练模型与强化学习微调深度整合，以解决更通用的机器人操作问题，奠定了重要基础。

## Overview

Humanoid robots face dual challenges in multimodal perception fusion and action generation when performing mobile manipulation tasks in complex environments. Existing methods often struggle to simultaneously handle language instruction comprehension, visual observation processing, and proprioceptive state modeling, resulting in limited generalization of action policies. REFINE-DP proposes a reinforcement learning-based diffusion policy fine-tuning framework that encodes language, vision, and joint sequences into a unified multimodal representation, leveraging diffusion models or flow matching techniques for conditional action generation, thereby significantly improving the execution quality and robustness of humanoid robot mobile manipulation tasks.

## Content

Mobile manipulation (loco-manipulation) for humanoid robots requires the simultaneous coordination of whole-body motion and fine-grained end-effector operations, imposing extremely high demands on perception, planning, and control. Traditional methods typically rely on handcrafted motion primitives or single-modality imitation learning, which struggle to handle multimodal inputs (e.g., natural language instructions, multi-view images, proprioceptive states) in unstructured environments. Moreover, action policy generation often faces the challenge of modeling multimodal action distributions—specifically, how to efficiently sample executable trajectories that satisfy task constraints in a continuous high-dimensional space. REFINE-DP directly addresses this core issue by redefining action generation as a conditional generation problem, thereby breaking through the bottlenecks of existing methods.

The model framework of REFINE-DP consists of three key modules. First, a multimodal encoder uniformly maps language instructions, camera images/multi-view observations, proprioceptive states, and joint sequences into a shared representation space, enabling the robot to simultaneously understand semantic instructions and physical states. Second, the policy learning layer integrates three paradigms: reinforcement learning (PPO), behavior cloning (ACT), and diffusion policy. PPO explores optimal action distributions, ACT provides an efficient imitation learning baseline, and diffusion policy or flow matching samples smooth and physically feasible joint position/torque commands from multimodal action distributions. Finally, a low-level controller converts these commands into precise motor control signals, achieving a complete closed loop from high-level planning to low-level execution.

The key technical innovation of this framework lies in introducing diffusion models into the fine-tuning process of humanoid robot mobile manipulation. Unlike directly using end-to-end neural networks to predict actions, REFINE-DP treats action generation as a denoising process: starting from random noise, it iteratively generates action sequences that align with current observations and task instructions. This design naturally supports the expression of multimodal action distributions, avoiding the limitations of traditional Gaussian assumptions on complex action patterns. Meanwhile, by fine-tuning the diffusion policy through reinforcement learning, the model can adaptively optimize action quality based on task reward signals in simulation or real environments, without relying on large amounts of manually annotated demonstration data.

In terms of experimental validation, REFINE-DP was evaluated in simulation environments on various mobile manipulation tasks (e.g., grasping, carrying, door opening). Results show that, compared to pure imitation learning baselines, the model fine-tuned with diffusion policy achieves significant improvements in task success rate, action smoothness, and generalization ability. Particularly when facing unseen object layouts or language instructions, REFINE-DP leverages the semantic understanding capability of multimodal representations to generate action trajectories adapted to new scenarios. Furthermore, the modular design of this framework facilitates its transfer to different humanoid robot platforms, providing a feasible path for future deployment in real physical environments.

From an application perspective, REFINE-DP provides key technical support for humanoid robots in scenarios requiring complex mobile manipulation capabilities, such as home services, industrial assembly, and disaster rescue. Its integration of vision-language-action (VLA) models with diffusion policy also advances the practical implementation of world models in robotics—through the conditional generation process, robots can not only execute predefined actions but also perform online reasoning and adaptation in dynamic environments. This work lays an important foundation for future research on deeply integrating large-scale pre-trained models with reinforcement learning fine-tuning to solve more general robot manipulation problems.
