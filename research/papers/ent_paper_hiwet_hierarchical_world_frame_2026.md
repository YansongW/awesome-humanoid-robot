## 概述

HiWET（Hierarchical World-Frame End-Effector Tracking）提出了一种面向长时程人形机器人移动操作任务的分层控制框架。该研究的核心动机在于解决人形机器人在复杂环境中执行长时间、多步骤操作任务时，全身协调与末端执行器精确跟踪之间的耦合难题。其主要贡献在于构建了一个将本体状态、关节序列、仿真交互数据以及接触力/触觉信号统一转化为可跟踪身体目标的转换机制，并通过分层策略实现任务拆解与执行组合，从而显著提升了人形机器人在非结构化环境中的操作鲁棒性与任务完成能力。

方法或模型框架：HiWET 采用分层架构，将任务自上而下分解为语义规划层、技能路由层与执行控制层。高层模块利用视觉语言模型（VLM）进行语义规划与路由，将长时程任务拆解为一系列可路由的技能或专家策略。中层通过分层技能/专家策略训练，将每个技能模块化为可复用的控制原语。低层则结合 PPO/RL 策略训练与组合全身策略，将本体状态、关节序列、仿真交互数据及接触力/触觉信号转化为可跟踪的身体目标，最终输出全身轨迹、末端执行器/腕手目标以及低层控制器目标。

## Overview

HiWET (Hierarchical World-Frame End-Effector Tracking) proposes a hierarchical control framework for long-horizon mobile manipulation tasks of humanoid robots. The core motivation of this research is to address the coupling challenge between whole-body coordination and precise end-effector tracking when humanoid robots perform long-duration, multi-step manipulation tasks in complex environments. Its main contribution lies in constructing a transformation mechanism that uniformly converts proprioceptive states, joint sequences, simulation interaction data, and contact force/tactile signals into trackable body targets. Through a hierarchical strategy for task decomposition and execution composition, it significantly enhances the operational robustness and task completion capability of humanoid robots in unstructured environments.

## Content

Research Background and Problem: Humanoid robot loco-manipulation is a frontier challenge in robotics, with the difficulty lying in the need to simultaneously coordinate the stability of lower-limb locomotion and the precision of upper-limb manipulation. Existing methods often treat locomotion and manipulation separately or rely on global motion planning, making it difficult to handle frequent contact state changes and environmental uncertainties in long-horizon tasks. HiWET addresses this bottleneck by focusing on how to map high-level task semantics into low-level executable whole-body trajectories and solving the problem of continuous end-effector tracking in the world coordinate system, which is a key prerequisite for achieving long-duration autonomous operation.

Method or Model Framework: HiWET adopts a hierarchical architecture that decomposes tasks from top to bottom into a semantic planning layer, a skill routing layer, and an execution control layer. The high-level module uses a Vision-Language Model (VLM) for semantic planning and routing, breaking down long-horizon tasks into a series of routable skills or expert policies. The middle layer modularizes each skill into reusable control primitives through hierarchical skill/expert policy training. The low-level layer combines PPO/RL policy training with composite whole-body policies, transforming proprioceptive states, joint sequences, simulation interaction data, and contact force/tactile signals into trackable body targets, ultimately outputting whole-body trajectories, end-effector/wrist-hand targets, and low-level controller targets.

Key Technical Innovations: The core innovation of HiWET lies in the "world-frame end-effector tracking" mechanism, which breaks away from the traditional ego-centric control paradigm, allowing end-effector motion planning and execution to directly align with global reference points in the environment. Additionally, by explicitly incorporating contact force/tactile signals into trackable targets, the framework achieves real-time perception and compensation of physical interaction states, significantly improving force control accuracy and disturbance rejection during operation. The hierarchical routing mechanism allows the system to dynamically select and combine expert policies during execution, thereby adapting to changes and uncertainties in the task sequence.
