## 概述

Pro-HOI（Perceptive Root-guided Humanoid-Object Interaction）提出了一种感知根引导的人形物体交互框架，旨在解决人形机器人在复杂环境中执行灵巧操作任务时的动作生成问题。该研究将动作生成视为条件生成问题，通过扩散策略或流匹配方法在多模态动作分布中采样可执行轨迹，从而突破传统规划方法在动态场景下的适应性瓶颈。其主要贡献在于构建了一个从视觉感知到全身运动控制的端到端推理管线，实现了场景理解、目标表征与运动生成的深度融合。

## 核心内容

人形机器人物体交互的核心挑战在于如何将高维视觉观测、本体感知与运动控制有效耦合。传统方法通常依赖预定义的运动基元或显式运动规划器，难以处理非结构化环境中的多模态动作分布。Pro-HOI 针对这一问题，将交互过程分解为感知恢复与动作生成两个阶段：首先从相机图像、多视角观测、本体状态及关节序列中恢复场景几何、目标位姿与运动表征；随后利用扩散策略或流匹配模型，在条件化动作空间中采样全身轨迹。这种设计将动作生成从确定性规划转化为概率采样，显著提升了系统对感知噪声与任务歧义的鲁棒性。

在方法框架层面，Pro-HOI 构建了一个层次化的生成式控制架构。底层采用全身控制器（WBC）或模型预测控制（MPC）保证运动学与动力学可行性，上层则通过逆运动学（IK）与动作重定向模块将采样轨迹映射至具体执行空间。关键创新在于引入“根引导”机制——以机器人躯干（根节点）的运动作为条件变量，将物体交互任务分解为根轨迹生成与末端执行器协调两个子问题。这种分解有效降低了动作空间的维度，使扩散模型能够更高效地学习跨模态的交互模式。

技术创新的核心体现在两个层面：其一，感知模块融合了场景理解与视觉表征学习，能够从单目或多视角观测中提取物体语义信息与空间关系，为动作生成提供结构化条件；其二，生成模块采用扩散策略或流匹配方法，通过逐步去噪过程从随机噪声中生成连续轨迹，避免了传统强化学习方法在稀疏奖励场景下的探索困难。此外，该框架支持从人类视频或动捕轨迹中提取先验知识，通过行为克隆实现跨域迁移，进一步增强了泛化能力。

实验验证方面，Pro-HOI 在多种人形物体交互任务中展示了有效性，包括桌面操作、移动抓取与全身协调搬运等场景。通过对比实验表明，基于扩散策略的方法在动作多样性、任务成功率与轨迹平滑性上均优于传统的确定性规划基线。值得注意的是，该框架对感知噪声具有天然鲁棒性——由于生成过程是条件概率采样，即使视觉观测存在部分遮挡或误差，模型仍能通过多模态分布中的其他可行解维持任务执行。这一特性为人形机器人在非结构化家庭或工业环境中的部署提供了重要支撑。

从应用价值看，Pro-HOI 为下一代人形机器人操作系统提供了可复用的感知-运动耦合范式。其模块化设计允许独立升级感知模块（如引入更先进的场景图网络）或生成模块（如替换为更高效的流匹配变体），而无需重构整体架构。此外，该框架对计算资源的需求相对可控，在边缘计算设备上可实现实时推理，这为人形机器人的商业化落地扫除了关键障碍。未来工作可进一步探索将语言指令或任务规划器作为高层条件输入，实现更复杂的长期交互任务。

## Overview

Pro-HOI (Perceptive Root-guided Humanoid-Object Interaction) proposes a perceptive root-guided humanoid-object interaction framework aimed at addressing the problem of action generation for humanoid robots performing dexterous manipulation tasks in complex environments. This research treats action generation as a conditional generation problem, sampling executable trajectories from multimodal action distributions using diffusion policies or flow matching methods, thereby overcoming the adaptability bottleneck of traditional planning methods in dynamic scenarios. Its main contribution lies in constructing an end-to-end inference pipeline from visual perception to whole-body motion control, achieving deep integration of scene understanding, object representation, and motion generation.

## Content

The core challenge of humanoid-object interaction lies in effectively coupling high-dimensional visual observations, proprioception, and motion control. Traditional methods typically rely on predefined motion primitives or explicit motion planners, making it difficult to handle multimodal action distributions in unstructured environments. Pro-HOI addresses this issue by decomposing the interaction process into two stages: perception recovery and action generation. First, it recovers scene geometry, object pose, and motion representation from camera images, multi-view observations, proprioceptive states, and joint sequences. Then, it uses diffusion policies or flow matching models to sample whole-body trajectories in a conditioned action space. This design transforms action generation from deterministic planning to probabilistic sampling, significantly enhancing the system's robustness to perceptual noise and task ambiguity.

At the methodological framework level, Pro-HOI constructs a hierarchical generative control architecture. The lower layer employs a whole-body controller (WBC) or model predictive control (MPC) to ensure kinematic and dynamic feasibility, while the upper layer maps sampled trajectories to the specific execution space through inverse kinematics (IK) and action retargeting modules. The key innovation lies in introducing a "root-guided" mechanism—using the robot's torso (root node) motion as a conditioning variable to decompose the object interaction task into two subproblems: root trajectory generation and end-effector coordination. This decomposition effectively reduces the dimensionality of the action space, enabling diffusion models to learn cross-modal interaction patterns more efficiently.

The core technical innovations are reflected in two aspects. First, the perception module integrates scene understanding and visual representation learning, extracting object semantic information and spatial relationships from monocular or multi-view observations to provide structured conditions for action generation. Second, the generation module employs diffusion policies or flow matching methods, generating continuous trajectories from random noise through a gradual denoising process, avoiding the exploration difficulties of traditional reinforcement learning methods in sparse reward scenarios. Additionally, the framework supports extracting prior knowledge from human videos or motion capture trajectories, achieving cross-domain transfer through behavior cloning, further enhancing generalization capabilities.

In terms of experimental validation, Pro-HOI demonstrates effectiveness across various humanoid-object interaction tasks, including tabletop manipulation, mobile grasping, and whole-body coordinated carrying. Comparative experiments show that methods based on diffusion policies outperform traditional deterministic planning baselines in terms of action diversity, task success rate, and trajectory smoothness. Notably, the framework exhibits inherent robustness to perceptual noise—since the generation process is conditional probability sampling, even if visual observations suffer from partial occlusion or errors, the model can still maintain task execution through other feasible solutions in the multimodal distribution. This characteristic provides crucial support for deploying humanoid robots in unstructured household or industrial environments.

From an application perspective, Pro-HOI offers a reusable perception-motion coupling paradigm for next-generation humanoid robot operating systems. Its modular design allows independent upgrades of the perception module (e.g., introducing more advanced scene graph networks) or the generation module (e.g., replacing it with more efficient flow matching variants) without restructuring the overall architecture. Furthermore, the framework has relatively controllable computational resource requirements, enabling real-time inference on edge computing devices, which removes a key obstacle to the commercial deployment of humanoid robots. Future work could further explore incorporating language instructions or task planners as high-level conditional inputs to achieve more complex long-horizon interaction tasks.
