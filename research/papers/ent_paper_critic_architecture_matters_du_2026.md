## 概述

本研究聚焦于人形机器人移动操作任务中价值网络架构的关键作用，系统对比了双评论家与统一评论家两种设计范式对策略学习效果的影响。通过将多模态观测（相机图像、本体状态、关节序列）与仿真交互数据转化为可跟踪的身体目标，并融合PPO强化学习与扩散策略/流匹配生成方法，本文提出了一套面向全身轨迹规划的端到端框架。其核心贡献在于揭示了评论家结构在条件生成式动作采样中的重要性，为提升人形机器人复杂操作任务的鲁棒性与多模态适应性提供了新思路。

## 核心内容

人形机器人的移动操作任务面临高维状态空间与多模态动作分布的双重挑战。传统强化学习方法通常采用单一价值网络（统一评论家）评估状态-动作对，但在处理全身协调控制时，单一评价器难以同时捕捉上肢精细操作与下肢动态平衡的差异化特征。现有工作多将动作生成简化为确定性映射，忽视了真实环境中多模态轨迹分布的复杂性。本文针对这一瓶颈，提出将动作生成重新定义为条件生成问题，通过扩散模型或流匹配在概率空间中对可执行轨迹进行采样，从而在保持策略多样性的同时提升控制鲁棒性。

在方法框架层面，研究构建了包含感知编码、状态融合与策略生成的三阶段流水线。首先，相机图像与多视角观测通过视觉编码器提取空间特征，与本体状态和关节序列进行时序对齐；其次，利用仿真交互数据构建可跟踪的身体目标表征，将抽象任务指令转化为具体的运动学约束；最后，采用PPO强化学习与扩散策略/流匹配的混合训练范式，其中价值网络作为条件生成的关键引导信号。双评论家架构在此处体现为分别对上半身操作与全身平衡进行独立价值估计，而统一评论家则共享同一网络参数。

关键技术创新的核心在于评论家结构与条件生成机制的协同设计。双评论家通过解耦上肢与下肢的价值函数，使得扩散模型在采样时能分别接收操作精度与稳定性的梯度引导，有效缓解了多目标优化中的冲突问题。此外，研究引入了组合全身策略（Combined Whole-Body Policy），将强化学习的高效探索与生成模型的分布建模能力结合：PPO负责粗粒度策略初始化，扩散/流匹配则对动作轨迹进行细粒度去噪与平滑。这种混合架构在保持实时控制性能的同时，显著扩展了策略对多模态动作分布的覆盖范围。

实验验证方面，研究在仿真环境中设计了多类移动操作基准任务，包括动态抓取、物体搬运与复杂地形行走。结果表明，双评论家架构在任务成功率与轨迹平滑度上均优于统一评论家，尤其在需要同时协调手臂运动与步态调整的场景中，双评论家策略的失败率降低约30%。消融实验进一步证实，扩散策略与流匹配在双评论家引导下能生成更符合人体运动学约束的全身轨迹，而统一评论家则容易陷入局部最优。这些发现表明，价值网络的结构设计直接影响条件生成模型对多模态动作分布的拟合能力。

从应用价值看，该研究为人形机器人在非结构化环境中的自主操作提供了可扩展的框架。通过将动作生成转化为条件采样问题，系统能够自然适应传感器噪声与任务变化，无需重新训练策略。双评论家架构的引入降低了全身控制中不同身体部位间的干扰，为后续在真实机器人平台上的部署奠定了理论基础。此外，该工作对强化学习与生成模型融合范式的探索，也为机器人学中策略学习与轨迹规划的统一提供了重要参考。

## Overview

This study focuses on the critical role of value network architecture in mobile manipulation tasks for humanoid robots, systematically comparing the impact of two design paradigms—dual critic and unified critic—on policy learning effectiveness. By transforming multimodal observations (camera images, proprioceptive states, joint sequences) and simulation interaction data into trackable body targets, and integrating PPO reinforcement learning with diffusion policy/flow matching generation methods, this paper proposes an end-to-end framework for full-body trajectory planning. Its core contribution lies in revealing the importance of critic structure in conditional generative action sampling, offering new insights for enhancing the robustness and multimodal adaptability of humanoid robots in complex manipulation tasks.

## Content

Mobile manipulation tasks for humanoid robots face dual challenges of high-dimensional state spaces and multimodal action distributions. Traditional reinforcement learning methods typically employ a single value network (unified critic) to evaluate state-action pairs, but when handling full-body coordinated control, a single evaluator struggles to simultaneously capture the differentiated characteristics of fine-grained upper-limb manipulation and dynamic lower-limb balance. Existing work often simplifies action generation into deterministic mappings, neglecting the complexity of multimodal trajectory distributions in real-world environments. Addressing this bottleneck, this study redefines action generation as a conditional generation problem, sampling executable trajectories in probability space via diffusion models or flow matching, thereby enhancing control robustness while maintaining policy diversity.

At the methodological framework level, the research constructs a three-stage pipeline comprising perception encoding, state fusion, and policy generation. First, camera images and multi-view observations are processed by a visual encoder to extract spatial features, which are temporally aligned with proprioceptive states and joint sequences. Second, simulation interaction data are used to build trackable body target representations, converting abstract task instructions into specific kinematic constraints. Finally, a hybrid training paradigm combining PPO reinforcement learning with diffusion policy/flow matching is adopted, where the value network serves as a key guiding signal for conditional generation. The dual critic architecture here manifests as independent value estimation for upper-body manipulation and full-body balance, while the unified critic shares the same network parameters.

The core of the technical innovation lies in the synergistic design of the critic structure and conditional generation mechanism. By decoupling the value functions for upper and lower limbs, the dual critic enables the diffusion model to receive gradient guidance for manipulation precision and stability separately during sampling, effectively alleviating conflicts in multi-objective optimization. Additionally, the study introduces a Combined Whole-Body Policy, combining the efficient exploration of reinforcement learning with the distribution modeling capability of generative models: PPO handles coarse-grained policy initialization, while diffusion/flow matching performs fine-grained denoising and smoothing of action trajectories. This hybrid architecture significantly expands the policy's coverage of multimodal action distributions while maintaining real-time control performance.

In terms of experimental validation, the study designs multiple mobile manipulation benchmark tasks in simulation environments, including dynamic grasping, object transportation, and complex terrain walking. Results show that the dual critic architecture outperforms the unified critic in task success rate and trajectory smoothness, particularly in scenarios requiring simultaneous coordination of arm movements and gait adjustments, where the failure rate of the dual critic policy is reduced by approximately 30%. Ablation studies further confirm that diffusion policies and flow matching, guided by the dual critic, generate full-body trajectories that better conform to human kinematic constraints, while the unified critic tends to fall into local optima. These findings indicate that the structural design of the value network directly influences the conditional generative model's ability to fit multimodal action distributions.

From an application perspective, this research provides a scalable framework for autonomous operation of humanoid robots in unstructured environments. By transforming action generation into a conditional sampling problem, the system can naturally adapt to sensor noise and task variations without retraining the policy. The introduction of the dual critic architecture reduces interference between different body parts in full-body control, laying a theoretical foundation for subsequent deployment on real robotic platforms. Furthermore, this work's exploration of the fusion paradigm between reinforcement learning and generative models offers important references for unifying policy learning and trajectory planning in robotics.
