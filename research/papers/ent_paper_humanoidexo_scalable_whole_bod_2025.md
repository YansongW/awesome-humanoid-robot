在方法层面，HumanoidExo 构建了一个分层式模型框架。底层采用全身控制器（WBC）或模型预测控制（MPC）确保运动学与动力学的物理可行性；上层则通过扩散策略或流匹配网络，将采集到的人类操作数据转化为条件化的动作生成模型。该框架将动作生成视为一个条件生成问题：给定当前视觉观测与任务目标，模型从多模态动作分布中采样出最符合物理约束与任务需求的全身轨迹。这种设计使得系统能够同时处理抓取、移动、避障等异构操作模式。

关键技术创新的核心在于“数据闭环”的可扩展性设计。HumanoidExo 不仅依赖外骨骼采集精确的关节级轨迹，还融合了单目/多目相机图像与人类视频中的运动先验，通过视觉感知模块实现场景理解与目标定位。此外，分层技能策略允许将复杂任务分解为可复用的子技能（如“行走”、“抓取”、“放置”），每个子技能均可独立训练并组合调用，显著降低了长时域任务的学习难度。扩散策略的引入则有效缓解了多模态动作分布中的模式坍塌问题，提升了生成轨迹的多样性与鲁棒性。

实验验证方面，HumanoidExo 在多种移动操作场景中展示了其有效性，包括桌面物体抓取、地面拾取与放置、以及动态环境中的避障行走。通过对比纯遥操作与基于扩散策略的生成方法，该框架在任务成功率与数据利用效率上均表现出显著优势。其应用价值体现在两个方面：一是为人形机器人提供了一种低成本、高效率的数据采集范式，二是通过可扩展的模型架构，使机器人能够适应家庭、仓储、医疗等非结构化环境中的多样化操作需求。该工作为未来人形机器人的大规模部署与技能迁移奠定了关键技术基础。

## Overview

HumanoidExo proposes a scalable framework for whole-body humanoid manipulation via wearable exoskeletons, aiming to address the "data closed-loop" challenge in humanoid robot data collection and policy generalization. This research models action generation as a conditional generation problem, utilizing diffusion models or flow matching methods to sample executable trajectories from multimodal action distributions, thereby efficiently converting human manipulation data into trainable and reusable whole-body action sequences. Its main contribution lies in constructing a complete technical pipeline from multi-view observations and motion capture trajectories to whole-body controllers and hierarchical skill policies, providing a scalable solution for mobile manipulation of humanoid robots in complex scenarios.

## Content

Whole-body manipulation of humanoid robots faces two core challenges: first, how to efficiently acquire high-quality manipulation data covering diverse scenarios, and second, how to convert human demonstration data into robust policies executable by robots. Traditional teleoperation is limited by hardware costs and operational complexity, while vision-based imitation learning suffers from ambiguity in multimodal action distributions. Against this background, HumanoidExo proposes using wearable exoskeletons as the core interface for data collection, combined with multi-view camera observations and human motion capture trajectories, to construct a complete mapping closed-loop from "human manipulation" to "robot state," thereby breaking through the bottlenecks of data scale and generalization capability.

At the methodological level, HumanoidExo builds a hierarchical model framework. The lower layer employs a whole-body controller (WBC) or model predictive control (MPC) to ensure kinematic and dynamic physical feasibility; the upper layer uses diffusion policies or flow matching networks to convert collected human manipulation data into conditional action generation models. This framework treats action generation as a conditional generation problem: given current visual observations and task objectives, the model samples whole-body trajectories from multimodal action distributions that best satisfy physical constraints and task requirements. This design enables the system to simultaneously handle heterogeneous manipulation modes such as grasping, moving, and obstacle avoidance.

The core of the key technological innovation lies in the scalable design of the "data closed-loop." HumanoidExo not only relies on exoskeletons to collect precise joint-level trajectories but also integrates monocular/multi-camera images and motion priors from human videos, achieving scene understanding and target localization through visual perception modules. Additionally, the hierarchical skill policy allows complex tasks to be decomposed into reusable sub-skills (e.g., "walking," "grasping," "placing"), each of which can be independently trained and combined for invocation, significantly reducing the learning difficulty of long-horizon tasks. The introduction of diffusion policies effectively alleviates mode collapse in multimodal action distributions, enhancing the diversity and robustness of generated trajectories.

In terms of experimental validation, HumanoidExo demonstrates its effectiveness across various mobile manipulation scenarios, including desktop object grasping, ground pickup and placement, and obstacle avoidance walking in dynamic environments. By comparing pure teleoperation with diffusion policy-based generation methods, the framework shows significant advantages in task success rate and data utilization efficiency. Its application value is reflected in two aspects: first, it provides a low-cost, high-efficiency data collection paradigm for humanoid robots; second, through a scalable model architecture, it enables robots to adapt to diverse manipulation needs in unstructured environments such as homes, warehouses, and medical settings. This work lays a key technical foundation for the large-scale deployment and skill transfer of future humanoid robots.
