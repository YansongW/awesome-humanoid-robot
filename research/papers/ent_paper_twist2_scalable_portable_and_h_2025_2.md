## Overview

The generalization capability of humanoid robots heavily relies on large-scale, high-quality motion and manipulation data. However, existing data collection systems often struggle to balance scalability, portability, and data integrity. TWIST2 proposes a scalable, portable, and holistic humanoid data collection system. By uniformly encoding multimodal observations (camera images, human videos, motion capture trajectories, teleoperation, and exoskeleton data) into a multimodal representation, and integrating behavioral cloning, VLA multimodal action models, and whole-body controllers (WBC/MPC), it achieves an end-to-end closed loop from perception to action. While retaining the semantic understanding capabilities of Vision-Language Models (VLMs), this system introduces robot states and action heads, breaking through the limitations of traditional methods that remain at the level of linguistic planning, thereby providing a system-level solution for deploying humanoid robots in the real world.

**Research Background and Problem**  
Humanoid robots have inherent advantages in complex environments due to their human-like morphology, but the high cost and low efficiency of data acquisition severely constrain the generalization of their motion and manipulation skills. Existing data collection methods mostly rely on a single modality (e.g., teleoperation or motion capture), making it difficult to simultaneously capture visual, force, and kinematic information. Moreover, the systems are often bulky and hard to transfer to different robot platforms. Additionally, traditional approaches frequently decouple linguistic planning from action execution, preventing robots from directly converting high-level semantic instructions into fine-grained whole-body coordinated actions. TWIST2 addresses these bottlenecks by aiming to construct a unified data collection framework that is cross-platform deployable, supports multi-source data fusion, and can output executable action commands end-to-end.

**Method or Model Framework**  
The core framework of TWIST2 is divided into three layers: First, the system uniformly encodes multimodal data from cameras, motion capture devices, teleoperation controllers, and exoskeletons into a structured representation, ensuring alignment of information from different sources in the feature space. Second, it uses ACT (Action Chunking with Transformers) and behavioral cloning imitation learning to extract action priors from human demonstration data, while introducing a VLA (Vision-Language-Action) multimodal action model that fuses visual-linguistic understanding with robot states (e.g., joint angles, torques). Finally, through a whole-body controller (WBC) and model predictive control (MPC), the learned actions are mapped into executable joint-level commands, achieving smooth and stable whole-body motion.

**Key Technological Innovations**  
The key innovation of TWIST2 lies in its dual design of "retaining VLM semantic understanding" and "adding robot states and action heads." Traditional VLMs only output language or symbolic plans, whereas TWIST2 appends an action head to the VLM, enabling it to directly output continuous action sequences, thereby avoiding conversion loss from semantics to actions. Furthermore, the system employs a "multimodal representation encoder" to uniformly process heterogeneous data sources, allowing the same model to simultaneously utilize human videos, motion capture trajectories, and teleoperation data, significantly improving data utilization efficiency. The portable hardware design (e.g., lightweight exoskeletons and modular sensor suites) enables rapid deployment on different robot platforms, enhancing scalability in practical applications.
