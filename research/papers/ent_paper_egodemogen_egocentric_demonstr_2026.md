This paper proposes the EgoDemoGen framework, aimed at enhancing the robustness of robot manipulation policies to viewpoint variations by generating first-person perspective demonstration data. This issue is particularly pronounced in dynamic environments such as home services and medical assistance, where robots often need to perceive the environment and make decisions from their own "first-person" perspective.
