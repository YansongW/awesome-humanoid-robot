## 概述

DIAL（Decoupling Intent and Action via Latent World Modeling）提出了一种面向人形机器人的端到端视觉-语言-动作（VLA）框架，旨在解决高层语义意图与底层连续控制之间的耦合问题。该研究通过引入潜在世界模型，将语言指令、多视角视觉观测与本体状态统一编码为多模态表征，并利用扩散策略与流匹配技术生成全身轨迹与动作序列。其主要贡献在于让世界模型提供可预测的物理先验，使动作头能够将语义目标高效转化为连续控制信号，从而提升机器人操作任务的泛化性与鲁棒性。

## 核心内容

（a）研究背景与问题：当前人形机器人操作任务面临两大挑战：一是高层语义理解（如“将杯子放到桌上”）与底层关节控制之间存在语义鸿沟，传统方法往往将两者耦合处理，导致模型难以适应新场景或新指令；二是缺乏对物理世界动态变化的显式建模，使得动作生成容易偏离真实物理约束。DIAL 针对这些问题，提出将意图（intent）与行动（action）在潜在空间中进行解耦，以提升端到端 VLA 系统的可解释性与泛化能力。

（b）方法或模型框架：DIAL 的整体架构分为三个核心模块：多模态编码器、潜在世界模型与动作生成头。首先，语言指令、相机图像/多视角观测、本体状态与关节序列被编码为统一的多模态表征。随后，潜在世界模型基于这些表征预测未来的物理状态与场景演化，为动作生成提供可学习的物理先验。最后，动作头采用扩散策略或流匹配技术，将语义目标映射为连续的全身轨迹或动作 chunk/token，同时结合 VLM 进行语义规划与路由，以适应复杂地形与场景变化。

（c）关键技术创新：DIAL 的核心创新在于“解耦”设计：通过潜在世界模型将物理预测与动作生成分离，使模型能够独立学习环境动态而不受动作策略的干扰。此外，该框架融合了扩散策略与流匹配两种生成范式，能够在高维动作空间中生成平滑且符合物理约束的轨迹。与传统的 VLA 模型相比，DIAL 还引入了 VLM 作为高层规划器，用于语义路由与场景表征，从而在任务级实现更灵活的决策。

## Overview

DIAL (Decoupling Intent and Action via Latent World Modeling) proposes an end-to-end Vision-Language-Action (VLA) framework for humanoid robots, aiming to address the coupling issue between high-level semantic intent and low-level continuous control. By introducing a latent world model, this research unifies language instructions, multi-view visual observations, and proprioceptive states into a multimodal representation, and utilizes diffusion policies and flow matching techniques to generate whole-body trajectories and action sequences. Its main contribution lies in enabling the world model to provide predictable physical priors, allowing the action head to efficiently transform semantic goals into continuous control signals, thereby enhancing the generalization and robustness of robotic manipulation tasks.

## Content

(a) Research Background and Problem: Current humanoid robot manipulation tasks face two major challenges: first, there is a semantic gap between high-level semantic understanding (e.g., "put the cup on the table") and low-level joint control, and traditional methods often handle these in a coupled manner, making it difficult for the model to adapt to new scenarios or instructions; second, the lack of explicit modeling of dynamic changes in the physical world causes action generation to easily deviate from real physical constraints. To address these issues, DIAL proposes decoupling intent and action in the latent space to improve the interpretability and generalization of end-to-end VLA systems.

(b) Method or Model Framework: The overall architecture of DIAL consists of three core modules: a multimodal encoder, a latent world model, and an action generation head. First, language instructions, camera images/multi-view observations, proprioceptive states, and joint sequences are encoded into a unified multimodal representation. Then, the latent world model predicts future physical states and scene evolution based on these representations, providing learnable physical priors for action generation. Finally, the action head employs diffusion policies or flow matching techniques to map semantic goals into continuous whole-body trajectories or action chunks/tokens, while integrating a VLM for semantic planning and routing to adapt to complex terrains and scene changes.

(c) Key Technical Innovations: The core innovation of DIAL lies in its "decoupling" design: by separating physical prediction from action generation through the latent world model, the model can independently learn environmental dynamics without interference from the action policy. Additionally, the framework integrates two generative paradigms—diffusion policies and flow matching—enabling the generation of smooth trajectories that adhere to physical constraints in high-dimensional action spaces. Compared to traditional VLA models, DIAL also introduces a VLM as a high-level planner for semantic routing and scene representation, thereby achieving more flexible decision-making at the task level.

(d) Experiments/Validation and Application Value: Although the paper does not disclose specific experimental data, it can be inferred from its design paradigm that DIAL is suitable for scenarios requiring fine-grained manipulation and dynamic adaptation, such as household services, industrial assembly, and locomotion on complex terrains. By decoupling intent and action, the model can maintain a high success rate under unseen task instructions or environmental changes, while reducing reliance on large-scale annotated data. This framework provides a new technical pathway for building general-purpose humanoid robot manipulation intelligence, particularly suited for application scenarios requiring long-term autonomous decision-making and physical interaction.
