## 概述

SkillBlender 提出了一种面向人形机器人全身移动操作任务的通用框架，旨在解决传统方法在复杂环境中动作生成单一、泛化能力弱的问题。该研究将动作生成建模为条件生成问题，通过扩散模型与流匹配技术从多模态动作分布中采样可执行轨迹，从而显著提升机器人对多样化场景与任务的适应能力。其主要贡献在于构建了一个融合视觉感知、本体状态与触觉信号的统一表征与生成管线，为人形机器人实现灵巧、鲁棒的全身协调操作提供了新范式。

## 核心内容

人形机器人全身移动操作（whole-body loco-manipulation）面临的核心挑战在于：机器人需要在动态、非结构化环境中同时协调行走、抓取、推拉等复合动作，而传统基于规则或单一强化学习策略的方法往往难以处理多模态感知输入与高维动作空间之间的复杂映射。现有研究多聚焦于特定任务（如抓取或行走）的独立优化，缺乏对场景、目标与运动表征的统一建模，导致模型在任务切换或环境变化时性能急剧下降。SkillBlender 正是针对这一空白，提出将动作生成视为条件生成问题，以应对人形机器人操作中固有的多模态动作分布特性。

在方法层面，SkillBlender 构建了一个端到端的感知-生成-控制管线。首先，系统从相机图像、多视角观测、本体状态与关节序列、接触力与触觉信号中恢复场景、目标或运动的高层表征。这些表征作为条件输入，引导后续的动作生成过程。核心生成模块采用扩散策略（diffusion policy）或流匹配（flow matching）技术，在预定义的动作分布中通过迭代去噪或概率流采样，输出符合当前任务与物理约束的全身轨迹。生成的轨迹进一步由全身控制器（WBC）或模型预测控制（MPC）进行底层执行，确保动作的平滑性与安全性。

关键技术创新的核心在于“技能混合”（skill blending）机制。不同于以往将不同技能（如行走、抓取、推拉）作为独立模块拼接的方法，SkillBlender 通过条件生成框架将多种技能隐式地编码在同一个扩散模型中。当输入条件（如目标物体位置、地形信息、接触力阈值）变化时，模型能够自动在动作分布中插值或切换，生成适应新场景的混合动作。例如，在需要同时保持平衡与抓取物体的任务中，模型可动态调整腿部与手臂的协调策略，而无需显式设计切换规则。这种设计大幅提升了模型在零样本迁移场景下的泛化能力。

实验验证方面，项目页面展示了 SkillBlender 在仿真环境与真实人形机器人平台上的多项测试。在仿真中，模型成功完成了包括动态行走中抓取移动物体、在狭窄通道中推拉障碍物、以及从地面拾取物品后恢复站立等复合任务。与基于 PPO 的基线方法相比，SkillBlender 在任务成功率与动作平滑度上均取得显著提升，尤其在面对未见过物体形状或地形变化时表现出更强的鲁棒性。此外，通过消融实验验证了多模态感知输入（特别是触觉信号）对提高操作精度的关键作用。该框架的应用价值在于：为人形机器人在家庭服务、灾难救援、工业装配等需要全身协调操作的场景中提供了一种可扩展、可迁移的解决方案，降低了针对新任务重新设计策略的成本。

## Overview

SkillBlender proposes a general framework for whole-body mobile manipulation tasks of humanoid robots, aiming to address the issues of single action generation and weak generalization ability of traditional methods in complex environments. This study models action generation as a conditional generation problem, sampling executable trajectories from multimodal action distributions using diffusion models and flow matching techniques, thereby significantly enhancing the robot's adaptability to diverse scenarios and tasks. Its main contribution lies in constructing a unified representation and generation pipeline that integrates visual perception, proprioceptive states, and tactile signals, providing a new paradigm for achieving dexterous and robust whole-body coordination in humanoid robots.

## Content

The core challenge of whole-body loco-manipulation for humanoid robots lies in the need to simultaneously coordinate compound actions such as walking, grasping, pushing, and pulling in dynamic, unstructured environments. Traditional methods based on rules or single reinforcement learning strategies often struggle to handle the complex mapping between multimodal perceptual inputs and high-dimensional action spaces. Existing research mostly focuses on independent optimization for specific tasks (e.g., grasping or walking), lacking a unified modeling of scenes, goals, and motion representations, leading to a sharp decline in performance when tasks switch or environments change. SkillBlender addresses this gap by treating action generation as a conditional generation problem to cope with the inherent multimodal action distribution characteristics of humanoid robot manipulation.

At the methodological level, SkillBlender constructs an end-to-end perception-generation-control pipeline. First, the system recovers high-level representations of scenes, goals, or motions from camera images, multi-view observations, proprioceptive states and joint sequences, contact forces, and tactile signals. These representations serve as conditional inputs to guide the subsequent action generation process. The core generation module employs diffusion policy or flow matching techniques to sample whole-body trajectories that meet current task and physical constraints through iterative denoising or probabilistic flow sampling within a predefined action distribution. The generated trajectories are then executed at the low level by a whole-body controller (WBC) or model predictive control (MPC), ensuring smoothness and safety of actions.

The core of the key technological innovation lies in the "skill blending" mechanism. Unlike previous approaches that concatenate different skills (e.g., walking, grasping, pushing, pulling) as independent modules, SkillBlender implicitly encodes multiple skills within a single diffusion model through a conditional generation framework. When input conditions (e.g., target object position, terrain information, contact force thresholds) change, the model can automatically interpolate or switch within the action distribution to generate blended actions adapted to new scenarios. For example, in tasks requiring simultaneous balance maintenance and object grasping, the model can dynamically adjust the coordination strategy between legs and arms without explicitly designing switching rules. This design significantly enhances the model's generalization ability in zero-shot transfer scenarios.

In terms of experimental validation, the project page demonstrates multiple tests of SkillBlender in simulation environments and on real humanoid robot platforms. In simulations, the model successfully completed compound tasks including grasping moving objects during dynamic walking, pushing and pulling obstacles in narrow passages, and recovering to a standing position after picking up items from the ground. Compared with PPO-based baseline methods, SkillBlender achieved significant improvements in task success rate and action smoothness, particularly showing stronger robustness when facing unseen object shapes or terrain changes. Additionally, ablation experiments verified the critical role of multimodal perceptual inputs (especially tactile signals) in improving manipulation precision. The application value of this framework lies in providing a scalable and transferable solution for humanoid robots in scenarios requiring whole-body coordination, such as home services, disaster rescue, and industrial assembly, thereby reducing the cost of redesigning strategies for new tasks.
