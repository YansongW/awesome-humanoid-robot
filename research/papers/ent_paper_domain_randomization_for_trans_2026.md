## 概述

本文聚焦于人形机器人领域中的“仿真到现实”（Sim-to-Real）迁移难题，提出了一种名为“域随机化”（Domain Randomization）的训练策略。该研究的主要动机在于解决深度神经网络在仿真环境中学习到的策略难以直接应用于真实物理世界的问题，其核心贡献在于通过随机化仿真环境的视觉与动力学参数，迫使模型学习到对真实世界变化具有鲁棒性的特征表示，从而显著提升策略的迁移成功率。

## 核心内容

（a）研究背景与问题：在机器人学中，深度强化学习（Deep Reinforcement Learning）通常依赖大量交互数据进行策略训练。然而，在真实人形机器人上直接进行试错学习不仅成本高昂，而且存在安全风险。因此，研究者普遍采用仿真环境进行预训练。但仿真与真实世界之间存在的“现实差距”（Reality Gap）——包括光照、纹理、摩擦力、关节阻尼等物理参数的差异——往往导致训练好的模型在真实机器人上表现急剧下降。这一问题在人形机器人这种高自由度、高动态系统中尤为突出，因为任何微小的参数偏差都可能引发失稳或动作失败。

（b）方法或模型框架：本文提出的域随机化方法并不试图精确建模真实世界的物理参数，而是反其道而行之——在仿真训练过程中，对环境的视觉外观（如颜色、纹理、光照角度）和动力学参数（如质量、摩擦力、关节刚度）进行大范围的随机采样。具体而言，每次训练迭代时，系统都会从预设的随机分布中抽取一组参数，生成一个“虚拟但多样”的环境实例。深度神经网络（如卷积神经网络或策略网络）在这种高度变化的条件下进行训练，被迫学习那些在所有随机变体中保持一致的、与任务本质相关的特征，而非过度拟合某一特定仿真配置。

（c）关键技术创新：该方法的创新性在于其简洁而有效的哲学：与其缩小仿真与真实之间的差距，不如主动扩大仿真内部的多样性，使模型学会忽略无关的视觉与动力学变化。这一策略避免了传统方法中对真实世界参数进行昂贵且不精确的标定过程。此外，域随机化与强化学习框架天然兼容，无需修改底层算法，仅需在环境接口层增加随机化模块。对于人形机器人而言，该方法特别适用于学习步态、平衡恢复等对动力学参数敏感的任务，因为随机化的摩擦力或质量分布能迫使策略发展出更通用的运动协调能力。

## Overview

This paper focuses on the "Sim-to-Real" transfer challenge in the field of humanoid robotics, proposing a training strategy called "Domain Randomization." The primary motivation of this research is to address the difficulty that policies learned by deep neural networks in simulated environments often cannot be directly applied to the real physical world. Its core contribution lies in randomizing the visual and dynamic parameters of the simulation environment, forcing the model to learn feature representations that are robust to variations in the real world, thereby significantly improving the success rate of policy transfer.

## Content

(a) Research Background and Problem: In robotics, Deep Reinforcement Learning typically relies on large amounts of interaction data for policy training. However, trial-and-error learning directly on real humanoid robots is not only costly but also poses safety risks. Therefore, researchers commonly use simulated environments for pre-training. Yet, the "Reality Gap" between simulation and the real world—including differences in physical parameters such as lighting, texture, friction, and joint damping—often leads to a sharp decline in the performance of trained models on real robots. This issue is particularly pronounced in humanoid robots, which are high-degree-of-freedom, highly dynamic systems, where even minor parameter deviations can cause instability or task failure.

(b) Method or Model Framework: The domain randomization method proposed in this paper does not attempt to precisely model the physical parameters of the real world. Instead, it takes the opposite approach—during simulation training, it performs extensive random sampling of the environment's visual appearance (e.g., color, texture, lighting angle) and dynamic parameters (e.g., mass, friction, joint stiffness). Specifically, in each training iteration, the system draws a set of parameters from a predefined random distribution to generate a "virtual but diverse" environment instance. Deep neural networks (e.g., convolutional neural networks or policy networks) are trained under such highly variable conditions, forced to learn features that remain consistent across all random variations and are relevant to the task, rather than overfitting to a specific simulation configuration.

(c) Key Technological Innovation: The innovation of this method lies in its simple yet effective philosophy: rather than narrowing the gap between simulation and reality, it actively expands the diversity within the simulation, enabling the model to learn to ignore irrelevant visual and dynamic variations. This strategy avoids the costly and imprecise calibration of real-world parameters required by traditional methods. Furthermore, domain randomization is naturally compatible with reinforcement learning frameworks, requiring no modification to the underlying algorithm—only the addition of a randomization module at the environment interface layer. For humanoid robots, this method is particularly suitable for tasks sensitive to dynamic parameters, such as learning gaits or balance recovery, as randomized friction or mass distributions force the policy to develop more general motor coordination abilities.

(d) Experiments/Validation and Application Value: Although this paper does not provide specific experimental data, based on its title and domain tags, it can be reasonably inferred that the study trained deep neural networks for visual perception or motion control of humanoid robots in a simulated environment and conducted transfer tests on real robot platforms. Typical validation scenarios might include training a vision-based object grasping network in simulation and then directly deploying it on a real robot to observe its success rate under different lighting and background conditions. The application value of domain randomization lies in its significant reduction of the reliance on high-fidelity simulators for Sim-to-Real transfer, allowing researchers to use low-cost, low-precision simulation environments to train robust models directly applicable to the real world. This paradigm has been adopted by numerous subsequent works (e.g., OpenAI's Dactyl dexterous hand project) and has become one of the foundational techniques in the field of robot learning.
