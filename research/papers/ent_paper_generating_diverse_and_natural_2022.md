## 概述

（b）方法或模型框架：该研究提出了一种基于条件变分自编码器（Conditional VAE）与Transformer架构相结合的生成框架。模型首先利用预训练的语言模型对输入文本进行编码，提取语义特征向量；随后，通过一个时序生成网络将语义特征映射为连续的运动帧序列。为了增强多样性，模型在潜在空间中引入了随机噪声采样机制，使得同一文本描述可以对应多种合理的运动输出。同时，框架中嵌入了运动先验约束，确保生成的运动符合人体运动学规律，避免出现不自然的关节扭曲或物理违例。

## Overview

This paper addresses the problem of motion generation in the field of humanoid robotics, proposing a method to directly generate diverse and natural 3D human motion sequences from natural language text. The research aims to overcome the shortcomings of existing motion synthesis methods in terms of diversity, naturalness, and text-motion semantic alignment. By designing a novel generation framework, it provides a more flexible and intuitive motion control interface for humanoid robot behavior planning and human-robot interaction. The main contribution lies in constructing a model capable of generating high-quality, multimodal human motion data from text descriptions, significantly enhancing the realism and variability of generated motions.

## Content

(a) Research Background and Problem: In the interdisciplinary field of robotics and computer graphics, generating human motion from text is a challenging task, with the core difficulty being bridging the gap between high-level semantic descriptions and low-level motion parameters. Traditional methods often rely on predefined motion libraries or template matching, making it difficult to generate motion sequences that precisely correspond to text semantics and exhibit rich variation. Additionally, existing generative models frequently suffer from mode collapse, where generated motions tend to be uniform or repetitive, lacking the natural randomness and diversity of real human motion. This issue is particularly prominent in humanoid robot application scenarios, as robots need to produce adaptable and expressive actions based on different commands (e.g., "walk slowly" or "wave goodbye").

(b) Method or Model Framework: This research proposes a generation framework combining Conditional Variational Autoencoder (Conditional VAE) with a Transformer architecture. The model first uses a pre-trained language model to encode the input text and extract semantic feature vectors; subsequently, a temporal generation network maps these semantic features into a continuous sequence of motion frames. To enhance diversity, the model introduces a random noise sampling mechanism in the latent space, allowing the same text description to correspond to multiple reasonable motion outputs. At the same time, the framework incorporates motion prior constraints to ensure that generated motions comply with human kinematic principles, avoiding unnatural joint distortions or physical violations.

(c) Key Technological Innovations: The core innovation of this paper lies in designing a "text-motion bidirectional alignment" mechanism. On one hand, a contrastive learning loss function strengthens the matching degree between text features and motion features in the semantic space; on the other hand, a motion periodicity consistency constraint is introduced to maintain coherence and rhythm in the temporal dimension of generated long-sequence motions. Furthermore, the model adopts a progressive generation strategy, first coarsely generating the global trajectory of the motion skeleton, then finely filling in local joint details, thereby improving motion precision while ensuring generation efficiency. These techniques collectively address the pain points of semantic ambiguity, motion stiffness, and insufficient diversity in existing methods.

(d) Experiments/Validation or Application Value: The research conducted quantitative and qualitative evaluations on public human motion datasets (e.g., HumanML3D). Experimental results show that the proposed method significantly outperforms contemporary baseline models (such as GAN-based or standard VAE-based methods) in motion diversity metrics (e.g., average joint distance variance) and naturalness scores (based on user surveys). In ablation experiments, removing the noise sampling module or contrastive loss reduced the diversity of generated motions by approximately 30%, validating the effectiveness of each module. In terms of application value, this work can directly serve humanoid robot motion generation systems, such as synthesizing walking, grasping, or social gestures in real time based on voice commands, providing robots with more human-like and flexible motion capabilities, thereby enhancing the naturalness and efficiency of human-robot collaboration.
