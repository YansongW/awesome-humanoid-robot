## 概述

本研究聚焦于腿式机械手（legged manipulators）在全身动态投掷任务中的动作生成问题。现有方法多依赖预设的运动模板或单一模态数据，难以应对复杂环境下的多模态动作分布。该工作提出将动作生成视为条件生成问题，通过扩散策略或流匹配（flow matching）从本体状态、关节序列、人类视频/动捕轨迹以及仿真交互数据中恢复场景、目标或运动表征，进而采样出可执行的全身轨迹与动作序列。其主要贡献在于构建了一个统一的数据驱动框架，能够融合多源异构数据，生成兼具动态性与适应性的全身投掷动作。

## Overview

This study focuses on motion generation for legged manipulators in whole-body dynamic throwing tasks. Existing methods often rely on predefined motion templates or single-modal data, making it difficult to handle multi-modal action distributions in complex environments. This work proposes treating motion generation as a conditional generation problem, using diffusion policies or flow matching to recover scene, target, or motion representations from proprioceptive states, joint sequences, human video/motion capture trajectories, and simulation interaction data, thereby sampling executable whole-body trajectories and action sequences. Its main contribution lies in constructing a unified data-driven framework capable of fusing multi-source heterogeneous data to generate whole-body throwing actions that are both dynamic and adaptive.

Additionally, human throwing demonstration data (e.g., videos, motion capture) and simulation data differ in representation space. These representations serve as conditional information input into diffusion policies or flow matching models. Diffusion policies generate action sequences from random noise through a gradual denoising process, while flow matching samples trajectories by learning a continuous probability flow. Both effectively model multi-modal action distributions, enabling the generated throwing actions to satisfy dynamic constraints while exhibiting diversity and naturalness.

(c) Key Technological Innovations: The core innovation of this study lies in redefining the action generation problem as a conditional generation problem and using diffusion models or flow matching to sample feasible trajectories from multi-modal distributions. Furthermore, by introducing a "conditional generation" mechanism, the model can dynamically adjust outputs based on different scenes, targets, or proprioceptive states, achieving flexible control over dimensions such as throwing distance, angle, and force.
