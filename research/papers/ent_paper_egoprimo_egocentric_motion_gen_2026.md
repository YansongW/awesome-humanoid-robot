## 概述

EgoPriMo 提出了一种面向交互式人形机器人控制的第一视角运动生成框架，旨在解决现有视觉-语言模型（VLM）在机器人任务中仅停留在高层语言规划、无法直接输出可执行运动序列的问题。该研究通过将语言指令、第一视角图像与本体状态统一编码为多模态表征，并引入运动先验与行为克隆机制，实现了从语义理解到全身轨迹生成的端到端映射。其主要贡献在于构建了一个能够同时保留 VLM 语义推理能力并融合机器人运动控制头的混合架构，为人形机器人在复杂动态环境中的灵巧操作与自主导航提供了新的技术路径。

## 核心内容

当前人形机器人控制面临的核心挑战在于如何弥合高层语义理解与底层运动执行之间的鸿沟。尽管大规模视觉-语言模型在场景理解、任务规划方面展现出强大能力，但其输出通常为自然语言指令或离散动作序列，无法直接驱动具有高自由度的人形机器人完成连续、协调的全身运动。现有方法或依赖手工设计的运动基元库，或采用强化学习从零开始训练，前者缺乏泛化性，后者难以利用丰富的语言先验知识。EgoPriMo 正是针对这一空白，提出将语言、视觉与运动模态深度融合，实现从“理解”到“行动”的端到端生成。

EgoPriMo 的整体框架采用多模态编码器-解码器结构。首先，语言指令通过预训练语言模型编码为语义向量，第一视角相机图像及可选的多视角观测经由视觉编码器提取空间特征，同时本体状态（如关节角度、末端执行器位姿）与历史关节序列被编码为时序运动表征。这些异构模态的特征通过跨模态注意力机制融合为统一的多模态嵌入，作为后续运动生成的输入条件。该设计确保了语义信息与几何、运动信息的对齐，避免模态间信息丢失。

在运动生成阶段，EgoPriMo 创新性地组合了三种技术路径：基于对抗运动先验（AMP）的判别器-生成器框架用于约束运动自然性，行为克隆（ACT）通过模仿学习从专家演示中提取运动模式，而扩散策略与流匹配则负责生成平滑且符合物理约束的全身轨迹。关键创新在于，模型在保留 VLM 预训练权重的基础上，额外添加了机器人状态编码器与动作头（action head），使得 VLM 的语义理解能力能够直接指导运动生成，而非仅输出语言规划结果。这种“冻结语义、微调运动”的策略有效降低了训练成本，同时避免了灾难性遗忘。

实验验证方面，EgoPriMo 在仿真环境与真实人形机器人平台上进行了评估。在仿真中，模型能够根据自然语言指令（如“从桌上拿起蓝色杯子”）生成包含躯干平衡、手臂伸展与手部抓取在内的完整全身运动序列，运动平滑度与任务成功率均优于纯强化学习基线。在真实机器人实验中，通过第一视角图像输入，EgoPriMo 展示了在动态障碍物环境中的避障行走与目标物体操作能力，验证了其从仿真到真实的迁移潜力。该工作为人形机器人在家庭服务、工业协作等场景中的语言引导自主控制提供了可行的技术方案。

## Overview

EgoPriMo proposes a first-person motion generation framework for interactive humanoid robot control, aiming to address the issue that existing Vision-Language Models (VLMs) in robotic tasks remain at the level of high-level language planning and cannot directly output executable motion sequences. This research achieves end-to-end mapping from semantic understanding to full-body trajectory generation by uniformly encoding language instructions, first-person images, and proprioceptive states into a multimodal representation, and introducing motion priors and behavior cloning mechanisms. Its main contribution lies in constructing a hybrid architecture that simultaneously preserves the semantic reasoning capabilities of VLMs and integrates a robotic motion control head, providing a new technical pathway for dexterous manipulation and autonomous navigation of humanoid robots in complex dynamic environments.

## Content

The core challenge currently facing humanoid robot control is how to bridge the gap between high-level semantic understanding and low-level motion execution. Although large-scale Vision-Language Models have demonstrated strong capabilities in scene understanding and task planning, their outputs are typically natural language instructions or discrete action sequences, which cannot directly drive a humanoid robot with high degrees of freedom to perform continuous, coordinated full-body motions. Existing methods either rely on manually designed motion primitive libraries or use reinforcement learning trained from scratch; the former lacks generalization, while the latter struggles to leverage rich language priors. EgoPriMo addresses this gap by proposing a deep integration of language, vision, and motion modalities to achieve end-to-end generation from "understanding" to "action."

The overall framework of EgoPriMo adopts a multimodal encoder-decoder structure. First, language instructions are encoded into semantic vectors via a pre-trained language model; first-person camera images and optional multi-view observations are processed by a visual encoder to extract spatial features; meanwhile, proprioceptive states (e.g., joint angles, end-effector poses) and historical joint sequences are encoded as temporal motion representations. These heterogeneous modal features are fused into a unified multimodal embedding through cross-modal attention mechanisms, serving as the input condition for subsequent motion generation. This design ensures alignment between semantic, geometric, and motion information, avoiding information loss across modalities.

In the motion generation stage, EgoPriMo innovatively combines three technical pathways: a discriminator-generator framework based on Adversarial Motion Priors (AMP) to constrain motion naturalness, Behavior Cloning (ACT) to extract motion patterns from expert demonstrations via imitation learning, and diffusion policies combined with flow matching to generate smooth, physically plausible full-body trajectories. The key innovation is that the model, while retaining the pre-trained weights of the VLM, additionally incorporates a robot state encoder and an action head, enabling the VLM's semantic understanding capabilities to directly guide motion generation rather than merely outputting language planning results. This strategy of "freezing semantics, fine-tuning motion" effectively reduces training costs while avoiding catastrophic forgetting.

In terms of experimental validation, EgoPriMo was evaluated on both simulation platforms and real humanoid robot platforms. In simulation, the model could generate complete full-body motion sequences—including torso balance, arm extension, and hand grasping—based on natural language instructions (e.g., "pick up the blue cup from the table"), achieving superior motion smoothness and task success rates compared to pure reinforcement learning baselines. In real robot experiments, using first-person image input, EgoPriMo demonstrated obstacle avoidance walking and target object manipulation capabilities in dynamic obstacle environments, verifying its potential for sim-to-real transfer. This work provides a feasible technical solution for language-guided autonomous control of humanoid robots in scenarios such as home service and industrial collaboration.
