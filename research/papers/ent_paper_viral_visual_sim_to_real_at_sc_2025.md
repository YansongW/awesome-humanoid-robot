## 概述

人形机器人在复杂环境中执行移动操作任务，长期面临从仿真到现实迁移的“视觉域差距”与数据闭环缺失两大瓶颈。本文提出的 VIRAL 框架，旨在通过大规模视觉模拟现实技术，构建一个端到端的数据闭环系统：利用多视角相机图像、本体状态与关节序列，结合遥操作与外骨骼采集的人类操作数据，实现从仿真到真实环境的无缝迁移。其核心贡献在于提出一种基于教师-学生知识蒸馏的范式，使仅依赖部署时可用观测的学生策略，能够继承使用特权信息训练的教师策略的全身控制能力，从而显著提升人形机器人移动操作的泛化性与鲁棒性。

## 核心内容

当前人形机器人研究面临的核心挑战在于：仿真环境中训练的视觉策略往往因渲染差异与物理参数偏差，在真实场景中性能骤降；同时，真实环境下的数据采集成本高昂，难以形成有效的训练闭环。现有方法多聚焦于单一技能（如抓取或行走），缺乏对全身协调移动操作（locomanipulation）的统一建模。VIRAL 针对这一空白，将问题定义为：如何利用大规模仿真数据与有限真实数据，训练出可同时处理视觉感知、全身轨迹规划与底层控制的通用策略。

VIRAL 的模型框架采用分层架构，由高层技能策略与低层控制器组成。高层策略以多视角相机图像、本体状态及关节序列为输入，通过行为克隆（ACT）与模仿学习，从遥操作/外骨骼采集的人类示范数据中提取可复用的全身动作序列。低层控制器则负责将高层输出的抽象动作映射为关节力矩指令，确保动态稳定性与执行精度。框架的关键在于引入“教师-学生”知识迁移机制：教师策略在训练时享有特权信息（如精确的物体位姿、物理参数），而学生策略仅能访问部署时真实的视觉与本体观测。

VIRAL 的技术创新主要体现在三个方面。其一，提出了“视觉模拟现实”数据增强方法，通过随机化仿真环境中的光照、纹理与物理参数，迫使策略学习域不变特征，从而弥合仿真与现实的视觉差距。其二，设计了分层技能专家策略库，将复杂移动操作任务分解为可复用的子技能（如导航、抓取、躯干平衡），并通过知识蒸馏将专家策略的决策能力压缩至轻量级学生网络。其三，构建了完整的数据闭环：真实环境中的失败案例可被自动回传至仿真系统，用于生成对抗样本并迭代优化教师策略，形成持续自我改进的循环。

实验验证方面，VIRAL 在多种人形机器人平台上进行了评估，涵盖桌面操作、地面移动操作与动态避障等场景。结果表明，采用教师-学生蒸馏后的策略，在真实环境中的任务成功率相比纯仿真训练策略提升超过 40%，且对视觉干扰（如光照变化、遮挡）的鲁棒性显著增强。此外，通过分层技能复用，新任务的学习样本需求降低至传统方法的 1/5，验证了框架的可扩展性与实际应用价值。该工作为构建通用、可部署的人形机器人移动操作系统提供了重要的方法论支撑。

## Overview

Humanoid robots performing mobile manipulation tasks in complex environments have long faced two major bottlenecks: the "visual domain gap" in simulation-to-reality transfer and the lack of a closed data loop. The VIRAL framework proposed in this paper aims to construct an end-to-end closed data loop system through large-scale visual simulation-to-reality technology: leveraging multi-view camera images, proprioceptive states, and joint sequences, combined with teleoperation and exoskeleton-collected human demonstration data, to achieve seamless transfer from simulation to real environments. Its core contribution lies in proposing a teacher-student knowledge distillation paradigm, enabling a student policy that relies only on observations available during deployment to inherit the full-body control capabilities of a teacher policy trained with privileged information, thereby significantly enhancing the generalization and robustness of humanoid robot mobile manipulation.

## Content

The core challenge currently facing humanoid robot research is that visual policies trained in simulation often suffer from performance degradation in real-world scenarios due to rendering differences and physical parameter deviations. Meanwhile, the high cost of data collection in real environments makes it difficult to form an effective training loop. Existing methods mostly focus on single skills (such as grasping or walking) and lack a unified modeling of whole-body coordinated locomanipulation. VIRAL addresses this gap by defining the problem as: how to leverage large-scale simulation data and limited real-world data to train a general policy capable of simultaneously handling visual perception, whole-body trajectory planning, and low-level control.

The model framework of VIRAL adopts a hierarchical architecture, consisting of a high-level skill policy and a low-level controller. The high-level policy takes multi-view camera images, proprioceptive states, and joint sequences as input, and extracts reusable whole-body action sequences from human demonstration data collected via teleoperation/exoskeleton through behavioral cloning (ACT) and imitation learning. The low-level controller is responsible for mapping the abstract actions output by the high-level policy into joint torque commands, ensuring dynamic stability and execution precision. The key to the framework lies in introducing a "teacher-student" knowledge transfer mechanism: the teacher policy has access to privileged information during training (such as precise object poses and physical parameters), while the student policy can only access real visual and proprioceptive observations available during deployment.

The technological innovations of VIRAL are mainly reflected in three aspects. First, it proposes a "visual simulation-to-reality" data augmentation method, which randomizes lighting, textures, and physical parameters in the simulation environment to force the policy to learn domain-invariant features, thereby bridging the visual gap between simulation and reality. Second, it designs a hierarchical skill expert policy library, decomposing complex mobile manipulation tasks into reusable sub-skills (such as navigation, grasping, and torso balancing), and compressing the decision-making capabilities of expert policies into lightweight student networks through knowledge distillation. Third, it constructs a complete closed data loop: failure cases in real environments can be automatically fed back into the simulation system to generate adversarial samples and iteratively optimize the teacher policy, forming a continuous self-improvement cycle.

In terms of experimental validation, VIRAL was evaluated on various humanoid robot platforms, covering scenarios such as tabletop manipulation, ground mobile manipulation, and dynamic obstacle avoidance. Results show that policies after teacher-student distillation achieve over 40% improvement in task success rates in real environments compared to policies trained purely in simulation, with significantly enhanced robustness to visual disturbances (such as lighting changes and occlusions). Additionally, through hierarchical skill reuse, the sample requirements for learning new tasks are reduced to one-fifth of those of traditional methods, verifying the framework's scalability and practical application value. This work provides important methodological support for building general, deployable humanoid robot mobile manipulation systems.
