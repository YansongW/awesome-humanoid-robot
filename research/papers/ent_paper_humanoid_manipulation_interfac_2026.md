## 概述

## 核心内容

人形机器人的全身操作面临数据获取成本高、技能泛化能力弱两大瓶颈。传统方法通常依赖遥操作设备或手工编程，不仅限制了数据采集的规模与多样性，也难以覆盖复杂任务中全身协调与接触力调控的需求。本文针对这一问题，提出一种与机器人无关的示范采集方案，利用人类视频、动捕轨迹、外骨骼及触觉信号等多种模态数据，将人类操作行为与机器人状态同步记录，从而构建大规模、多模态的操作数据集。这一思路打破了机器人本体对数据采集的约束，使得示范数据可跨平台复用，为后续策略学习奠定了数据基础。

在方法层面，本文构建了一个分层学习与执行框架。底层采用近端策略优化（PPO）或强化学习（RL）策略，从原始传感器数据中学习全身轨迹与动作序列；中层则引入分层技能与专家策略，将连续动作空间离散化为可复用的技能单元，例如“抓取”、“行走”、“平衡调整”等。这些技能单元通过离线训练获得，并具备可路由性，即高层模块可根据任务上下文动态选择与组合技能，形成完整的操作序列。该框架的核心优势在于，它将端到端学习的灵活性与模块化策略的可解释性相结合，既保留了数据驱动的适应能力，又降低了策略迁移与任务扩展的难度。

关键技术创新体现在三个方面。其一，数据闭环的完整性：从人类示范采集到策略训练，再到低层控制器目标生成，形成闭环迭代，使得机器人可在实际部署中持续优化。其二，技能路由机制：通过将任务拆解为可路由的专家策略，高层模块能够根据实时环境反馈动态调整技能组合，从而应对非结构化场景中的不确定性。其三，全身控制与平衡维持的融合：本文特别强调运动跟踪与全身控制（whole-body control）的协同，确保机器人在执行操作任务时能够维持动态平衡，避免因上肢动作导致失稳。这一设计对于人形机器人在真实环境中的安全运行至关重要。

实验验证方面，本文通过项目页面展示了多种操作任务的仿真与实物实验，包括物体抓取、搬运、工具使用等。实验结果表明，基于分层技能框架训练的全身轨迹在成功率与泛化性上优于端到端RL基线方法。此外，通过引入接触力与触觉信号，机器人在精细操作任务中表现出更强的鲁棒性，例如在抓取易碎物品时能够自适应调整夹持力。这些结果验证了所提框架在降低数据采集成本、提升技能复用效率方面的实际价值，为人形机器人在家庭服务、工业装配等场景中的部署提供了可行路径。

## Overview

This paper proposes a whole-body operation interface for humanoid robots, aiming to address the closed-loop problem between robot operation data collection and skill generalization. The main motivation of the research is that existing methods often rely on expensive dedicated teleoperation devices or pre-programmed action libraries for robots, making it difficult to achieve efficient transfer from human natural demonstrations to whole-body robot motions. The core contribution of this paper lies in constructing a complete data pipeline from human videos, motion capture trajectories, teleoperation, and exoskeleton data to trainable and reusable whole-body trajectories and action sequences. It also introduces hierarchical skills and expert policy mechanisms, decomposing complex tasks into routable skill modules that are flexibly combined by high-level modules during execution, thereby enhancing the operational capability and generalization of humanoid robots in real-world scenarios.

## Content

Whole-body operation of humanoid robots faces two major bottlenecks: high data acquisition costs and weak skill generalization capabilities. Traditional methods typically rely on teleoperation devices or manual programming, which not only limits the scale and diversity of data collection but also struggles to cover the needs for whole-body coordination and contact force regulation in complex tasks. To address this issue, this paper proposes a robot-agnostic demonstration collection scheme that utilizes multiple modalities of data, including human videos, motion capture trajectories, exoskeletons, and tactile signals, to synchronously record human operational behaviors and robot states, thereby constructing large-scale, multimodal operation datasets. This approach breaks the constraints of robot hardware on data collection, allowing demonstration data to be reused across platforms and laying the data foundation for subsequent policy learning.

At the methodological level, this paper constructs a hierarchical learning and execution framework. The low-level layer employs Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) or Reinforcement Learning (RL) policies to learn whole-body trajectories and action sequences from raw sensor data; the middle layer introduces hierarchical skills and expert policies, discretizing the continuous action space into reusable skill units, such as "grasping," "walking," and "balance adjustment." These skill units are obtained through offline training and are routable, meaning high-level modules can dynamically select and combine skills based on task context to form complete operation sequences. The core advantage of this framework is that it combines the flexibility of end-to-end learning with the interpretability of modular policies, preserving data-driven adaptability while reducing the difficulty of policy transfer and task expansion.

Key technological innovations are reflected in three aspects. First, the completeness of the data loop: from human demonstration collection to policy training and then to low-level controller target generation, forming a closed-loop iteration that allows the robot to continuously optimize during actual deployment. Second, the skill routing mechanism: by decomposing tasks into routable expert policies, high-level modules can dynamically adjust skill combinations based on real-time environmental feedback, thereby addressing uncertainties in unstructured scenarios. Third, the integration of whole-body control and balance maintenance: this paper particularly emphasizes the synergy between motion tracking and whole-body control, ensuring that the robot maintains dynamic balance while performing operational tasks, avoiding instability caused by upper-body actions. This design is crucial for the safe operation of humanoid robots in real-world environments.

In terms of experimental validation, this paper demonstrates simulation and real-world experiments for various operational tasks through a project page, including object grasping, transportation, and tool use. Experimental results show that whole-body trajectories trained based on the hierarchical skill framework outperform end-to-end RL baseline methods in terms of success rate and generalization. Furthermore, by incorporating contact force and tactile signals, the robot exhibits stronger robustness in fine manipulation tasks, such as adaptively adjusting grip force when grasping fragile objects. These results validate the practical value of the proposed framework in reducing data collection costs and improving skill reuse efficiency, providing a feasible pathway for deploying humanoid robots in scenarios such as home services and industrial assembly.
