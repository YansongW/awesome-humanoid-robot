## 概述

人形机器人从实验室走向真实世界应用，面临一个核心瓶颈：缺乏大规模、高质量、多模态的全身运动与操作数据。现有数据采集系统往往局限于单一任务或固定场景，难以兼顾可扩展性与数据多样性。TWIST2 提出了一种可扩展、便携且整体的人形数据收集系统，通过将视觉、人类运动、遥操作等多源数据统一编码为多模态表征，并融合视觉语言模型（VLM）的语义理解与机器人状态/动作头，实现了从语言规划到全身运动控制的端到端数据驱动范式。该系统旨在为人形机器人构建行为基础模型提供高质量数据基础，推动全身控制与运动跟踪能力的协同提升。

## 核心内容

人形机器人研究长期受困于“数据匮乏”与“泛化困难”的双重挑战。传统数据采集方式如遥操作或动捕系统，往往需要昂贵的专用设备与受控环境，难以大规模部署；而基于仿真生成的数据又面临“仿真-现实”差距。此外，现有方法多将感知、规划与控制分离处理，缺乏能够同时捕捉语义理解与精细运动控制的统一数据框架。这一问题在需要全身协调的任务（如搬运、攀爬、动态平衡）中尤为突出，亟需一种既能保留高层语义推理能力，又能生成低层全身轨迹的数据采集与学习范式。

TWIST2 的系统框架围绕“多模态编码-统一表征-全身预测”三条主线展开。首先，系统将相机图像/多视角观测、人类视频/动捕轨迹、遥操作/外骨骼数据等异构输入，通过编码器转化为共享的多模态表征。这一步骤的关键在于对齐不同模态的时空尺度与语义层级，使得视觉特征、人类运动先验与机器人本体状态能够在同一隐空间中交互。随后，系统采用 ACT（行为克隆模仿学习）与 VLA（视觉-语言-动作）多模态动作模型，将上述表征映射为全身轨迹与动作序列。最后，通过全身控制器（WBC）或模型预测控制（MPC）将高层动作序列转化为低层控制器目标，实现从语义指令到关节力矩的完整闭环。

TWIST2 的核心技术创新在于“保留 VLM 语义理解的同时增加机器人状态和动作头”。传统方法要么将 VLM 仅用于语言规划，输出高层任务描述后交由独立控制器执行，导致语义信息与运动控制脱节；要么完全抛弃语言理解，仅依赖视觉-动作映射，限制了任务泛化能力。TWIST2 通过在 VLM 基础上附加专门的状态编码器与动作预测头，使得模型既能理解“拿起红色杯子”这样的语义指令，又能根据当前机器人关节角度、力矩等状态信息，直接预测“肩关节旋转30度、腕关节抓取力5N”等具体动作。这一设计避免了“只停留在语言规划”的局限，实现了语义-运动的一体化学习。

在实验验证方面，TWIST2 系统通过便携式硬件设计（如可穿戴外骨骼、轻量化相机阵列）支持在多种真实场景中采集数据，涵盖室内操作、动态行走、复杂地形适应等任务。初步结果表明，基于 TWIST2 收集的数据训练出的全身控制策略，在运动跟踪精度、任务成功率与跨场景泛化能力上均优于仅依赖单一模态或分离式架构的基线方法。该系统为人形机器人行为基础模型的构建提供了可复现的数据采集标准，有望加速从“特定任务编程”向“通用技能学习”的范式转变。其应用价值不仅限于学术研究，还可服务于工业装配、家庭服务、灾害救援等需要人形机器人灵活适应非结构化环境的领域。

## Overview

Humanoid robots face a core bottleneck when transitioning from laboratories to real-world applications: the lack of large-scale, high-quality, multimodal whole-body motion and manipulation data. Existing data collection systems are often limited to single tasks or fixed scenarios, making it difficult to balance scalability with data diversity. TWIST2 proposes a scalable, portable, and holistic humanoid data collection system. By uniformly encoding multi-source data—including vision, human motion, and teleoperation—into multimodal representations, and integrating the semantic understanding of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) with robot state/action heads, it achieves an end-to-end data-driven paradigm from language planning to whole-body motion control. This system aims to provide a high-quality data foundation for building behavioral foundation models for humanoid robots, driving the synergistic improvement of whole-body control and motion tracking capabilities.

## Content

Humanoid robot research has long been plagued by the dual challenges of "data scarcity" and "generalization difficulty." Traditional data collection methods, such as teleoperation or motion capture systems, often require expensive specialized equipment and controlled environments, making large-scale deployment difficult. Meanwhile, data generated through simulation faces the "sim-to-real" gap. Furthermore, existing methods typically separate perception, planning, and control, lacking a unified data framework that can simultaneously capture semantic understanding and fine-grained motion control. This issue is particularly pronounced in tasks requiring whole-body coordination (e.g., carrying, climbing, dynamic balancing), necessitating a data collection and learning paradigm that can both retain high-level semantic reasoning and generate low-level whole-body trajectories.

The system framework of TWIST2 revolves around three main threads: "multimodal encoding," "unified representation," and "whole-body prediction." First, heterogeneous inputs—such as camera images/multi-view observations, human videos/motion capture trajectories, and teleoperation/exoskeleton data—are transformed into shared multimodal representations via encoders. The key step here is aligning the spatiotemporal scales and semantic levels of different modalities, allowing visual features, human motion priors, and robot proprioceptive states to interact within the same latent space. Subsequently, the system employs ACT (Behavioral Cloning and Imitation Learning) and VLA (Vision-Language-Action) multimodal action models to map these representations into whole-body trajectories and action sequences. Finally, a Whole-Body Controller (WBC) or Model Predictive Control (MPC) converts the high-level action sequences into low-level controller targets, achieving a complete closed loop from semantic instructions to joint torques.

The core technological innovation of TWIST2 lies in "retaining VLM semantic understanding while adding robot state and action heads." Traditional methods either use VLM solely for language planning, outputting high-level task descriptions for execution by independent controllers—leading to a disconnect between semantic information and motion control—or completely discard language understanding, relying only on vision-action mappings, which limits task generalization. By appending specialized state encoders and action prediction heads to the VLM, TWIST2 enables the model to not only understand semantic instructions like "pick up the red cup" but also directly predict specific actions such as "rotate the shoulder joint by 30 degrees, apply a gripping force of 5N at the wrist" based on current robot joint angles, torques, and other state information. This design avoids the limitation of "stopping at language planning," achieving integrated semantic-motion learning.

In terms of experimental validation, the TWIST2 system supports data collection in various real-world scenarios through portable hardware designs (e.g., wearable exoskeletons, lightweight camera arrays), covering tasks such as indoor manipulation, dynamic walking, and complex terrain adaptation. Preliminary results indicate that whole-body control policies trained on data collected by TWIST2 outperform baseline methods relying solely on single modalities or separated architectures in terms of motion tracking accuracy, task success rate, and cross-scenario generalization. This system provides a reproducible data collection standard for building behavioral foundation models for humanoid robots, potentially accelerating the paradigm shift from "task-specific programming" to "general skill learning." Its application value extends beyond academic research to fields such as industrial assembly, home services, and disaster rescue, where humanoid robots need to flexibly adapt to unstructured environments.
