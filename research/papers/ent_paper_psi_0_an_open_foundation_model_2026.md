## Overview

This paper introduces Psi0 (Ψ₀), an open-source foundation model for whole-body mobile manipulation tasks of general-purpose humanoid robots. The primary motivation of this research is to address the significant gap between high-level semantic planning and low-level motion execution in current humanoid robot systems, by constructing a unified Vision-Language-Action (VLA) model framework that achieves end-to-end mapping from language instructions to joint-level action sequences. The core contribution of Psi0 lies in proposing a method that integrates heterogeneous motion capture data with synthetic data, Visual Language Model (VLM) semantic planning/routing, and latent variable/action token prediction mechanisms within a unified training and deployment pipeline, thereby providing an open and scalable foundation model paradigm for general-purpose humanoid manipulation capabilities.

## Content

(a) Research Background and Problem: Humanoid robots, due to their human-like morphology, hold great potential for performing complex manipulation tasks in human environments. However, achieving truly "general" mobile manipulation remains a significant challenge. Existing methods typically separate high-level task planning (e.g., natural language understanding, scene semantic parsing) from low-level motion control (e.g., joint angles, torque commands) into independent modules, leading to semantic loss and execution gaps during information transfer. Furthermore, the high degrees of freedom, whole-body coordination requirements, and coupled nature of manipulation and locomotion in humanoid robots make it difficult for traditional methods based on single data sources or handcrafted rules to generalize to diverse real-world scenarios. Therefore, constructing an end-to-end model that seamlessly connects high-level cognition with low-level actions has become a key bottleneck in this field.

(b) Method or Model Framework: Psi0 proposes a multimodal encoding-prediction architecture. First, the system uniformly encodes language instructions, camera images/multi-view observations, robot proprioceptive states (e.g., joint angles, IMU data), and historical joint sequences into a multimodal representation. This representation is then fed into a joint training pipeline containing two core components: one is a VLM-based semantic planning/routing module, responsible for decomposing high-level task goals into executable sub-goals or operation primitives; the other is a latent variable and action token prediction module, responsible for generating specific action chunks or action token sequences. By incorporating both heterogeneous motion capture data (e.g., human demonstrations) and synthetic data (e.g., simulation-generated data) into training, the model learns rich motion priors and task correlations.

(c) Key Technical Innovations: The core innovation of Psi0 lies in placing "heterogeneous motion capture and balanced synthetic data" and "VLM semantic planning/routing" within the same training/deployment pipeline, rather than as independent pre- or post-processing steps. This design allows high-level semantic information to directly guide the generation of low-level action tokens, while low-level motion feedback can also influence semantic planning in reverse, thereby significantly reducing the gap between goals and actions. Additionally, the model employs a latent variable mechanism to capture multimodality in action distributions (e.g., multiple feasible manipulation trajectories for the same task) and achieves efficient sequence generation through discrete prediction of action tokens, balancing representational richness with computational feasibility.

(d) Experiments/Validation or Application Value: Although the paper metadata does not provide specific quantitative experimental results, it can be inferred from its design paradigm that Psi0 aims to provide a reproducible and scalable foundation model for general-purpose humanoid manipulation. Its open-source nature (project page is publicly available) means the research community can fine-tune, augment data, or extend tasks based on it. Potential application scenarios include home services (e.g., organizing items, opening doors), industrial assembly (e.g., part grasping and placement), and remote operation in hazardous environments. By deeply integrating the semantic understanding capabilities of VLMs with motion generation capabilities, Psi0 is expected to advance humanoid robots from "programmed execution" of specific tasks to "language-instruction-driven autonomous operation," laying the model foundation for building truly general-purpose robotic agents.
