人形机器人在复杂环境中执行移动操作任务，面临从仿真到现实（Sim-to-Real）的视觉域差异与数据闭环缺失两大核心挑战。VIRAL 提出一种大规模视觉模拟现实框架，通过融合多视角相机观测、本体状态与遥操作数据，构建从人类操作到机器人可执行动作的完整数据闭环。该工作的主要贡献在于利用教师-学生知识蒸馏策略，将基于特权信息训练的教师策略能力迁移至仅依赖部署观测的学生策略，从而在无需真实环境标注的前提下实现高鲁棒性的视觉引导移动操作。

## Overview

Humanoid robots performing mobile manipulation tasks in complex environments face two core challenges: the visual domain gap between simulation and reality (Sim-to-Real) and the lack of a closed data loop. VIRAL proposes a large-scale visual simulation-to-reality framework that integrates multi-view camera observations, proprioceptive states, and teleoperation data to construct a complete data loop from human operations to executable robot actions. The main contribution of this work lies in leveraging a teacher-student knowledge distillation strategy to transfer the capabilities of a teacher policy trained with privileged information to a student policy that relies solely on deployment observations, thereby achieving highly robust vision-guided mobile manipulation without the need for real-world annotations.

## Content

**Research Background and Problem**  
Current research on mobile manipulation for humanoid robots faces two key bottlenecks: first, the visual perception gap between simulation environments and the real world, causing policies to fail during deployment due to differences in lighting, texture, occlusion, etc.; second, the lack of an efficient data loop mechanism to convert the vast heterogeneous data generated by human teleoperation (e.g., joint sequences, multi-view images) into reusable training signals. Existing methods often rely on handcrafted reward functions or precise state estimation, making them difficult to adapt to dynamic changes in open scenarios. To address these issues, VIRAL proposes an end-to-end solution centered on visual perception and bridged by knowledge distillation, aiming to close the data and policy gap between simulation training and real-world deployment.

**Method or Model Framework**  
The overall framework of VIRAL consists of two stages: teacher policy training and student policy distillation. In the teacher stage, the system uses privileged information (e.g., precise object poses, environmental geometry, joint torques) to train a high-performance expert policy in simulation. This policy decomposes and combines complex mobile manipulation tasks through hierarchical skills and an expert policy library. In the student stage, the behavior of the teacher policy is distilled into a student policy that relies only on deployment observations (e.g., monocular/multi-camera images, proprioceptive states). The distillation process combines behavior cloning (ACT) with imitation learning to ensure that the student policy replicates the action distribution of the teacher policy even under visual domain shifts. The framework also integrates a teleoperation/exoskeleton data collection module to provide diverse demonstration trajectories for the teacher policy.

**Key Technical Innovations**  
The core innovation of VIRAL lies in its "privileged information distillation" mechanism: the teacher policy has access to complete environmental states (e.g., precise object coordinates, contact forces) during training, while the student policy can only use visual observations and proprioceptive states during deployment. Through adversarial domain adaptation and attention distillation techniques, the student policy learns to implicitly infer missing privileged information from high-dimensional visual inputs. Additionally, the framework introduces a multi-view observation fusion module that uses cross-view attention mechanisms to enhance robustness against occlusion and viewpoint changes. Another innovation is the automated construction of a hierarchical skill library: the teacher policy decomposes long-horizon tasks into reusable atomic skills (e.g., grasping, placing, gait adjustment) and achieves zero-shot generalization to new tasks through skill combination strategies.

**Experiments/Validation and Application Value**  
Although the paper does not provide specific numerical experimental data, its validation paradigm can be inferred from the framework design: constructing mobile manipulation scenarios in simulation with various objects, obstacles, and lighting conditions, and comparing the teacher and student policies in terms of success rate, manipulation precision, and generalization ability. In terms of application value, VIRAL can be deployed in scenarios such as warehouse logistics and home services, where humanoid robots need to autonomously complete closed-loop tasks involving "walking, perception, and manipulation." Its data loop mechanism is particularly suitable for industrial scenarios requiring rapid collection of human demonstration data and conversion into robot skill libraries. Meanwhile, the teacher-student distillation paradigm reduces the reliance on high-precision sensors during real-world deployment, offering significant engineering practicality and scalability.
