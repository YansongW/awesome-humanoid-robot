## Overview

Soncini et al. (2025) introduce The Rosario Dataset v2, a multi-modal agricultural-robotics dataset collected in a soybean crop field. It comprises more than two hours of recordings from an Intel RealSense D435i stereo infrared/color camera with integrated IMU, three Emlid Reach GNSS receivers (two multi-band M2 units and one single-band M1 unit), and Hall-effect wheel-odometry sensors, covering approximately 7.3 km over six sequences.

The data-collection platform was designed to satisfy the requirements for rigorous multi-modal SLAM evaluation. A GNSS pulse-per-second (PPS) signal enables hardware time synchronization across sensors, and 6-DoF pose ground truth is generated by fusing dual GNSS post-processed kinematic (PPK) solutions with inertial measurements. Calibration sequences and evaluation utilities are released together with ROS rosbags and plain-file data.

The authors benchmark state-of-the-art multi-modal SLAM approaches—ORB-SLAM3, ORB-SLAM3+GNSS, and OpenVINS—using absolute pose error (APE) and relative pose error (RPE) metrics. Their experiments reveal persistent limitations in current systems, including motion blur, perceptual aliasing, and long-term drift in crop-row environments.

## Key Contributions

- Multi-sensor soybean-field dataset with stereo IR, RGB, IMU, multi-band GNSS, and wheel odometry.
- Hardware-plus-software time synchronization using a GNSS PPS signal.
- 6-DoF pose ground truth generated by fusing dual GNSS-PPK solutions with inertial measurements.
- Long trajectories with loop closures designed to test loop-closure algorithms.
- Open-source ROS rosbags, plain files, calibration sequences, and evaluation tools.

## Relevance to Humanoid Robotics

Although the platform is a weed-removing mobile robot rather than a humanoid, the underlying capabilities are directly transferable to humanoid deployment in industrial and outdoor settings. The advances in hardware-synchronized multi-sensor fusion, robust 6-DoF ground-truth generation, and long-trajectory outdoor localization address core challenges for legged or humanoid systems operating in unstructured, GNSS-degraded environments.

In particular, the paper’s treatment of perceptual aliasing, loop-closure failures, and drift over long agricultural trajectories mirrors the problems humanoid robots face when navigating repetitive factory floors, construction sites, or rural terrain. The dataset therefore serves as a relevant benchmark and methodological reference for improving the reliability of humanoid state estimation and SLAM.
