## Overview

This paper proposes a novel video world model framework called DreamGen, aimed at addressing the core challenge of insufficient generalization in humanoid robot manipulation tasks. By combining video prediction with reinforcement learning, the research enables robots to rehearse various manipulation scenarios in their imagination, thereby significantly improving their adaptability to unseen environments and objects. The main contribution lies in constructing a scalable visual reasoning paradigm, providing an effective pathway for transferring humanoid robot skills from simulation to the real world.

## Content

(a) Research Background and Problem  
Current humanoid robot manipulation learning primarily relies on large amounts of real-world data or precise simulation environments. However, when faced with variations in object shape, position, lighting, and other conditions, models often exhibit severe overfitting. Traditional reinforcement learning methods lack the ability for long-term reasoning about dynamic scenes, causing robots to fail to maintain stable performance on tasks outside the training distribution. DreamGen is specifically designed to address this "generalization bottleneck" by endowing robots with the ability to rehearse and plan through an internal world model.

(b) Method or Model Framework  
DreamGen constructs a world model based on video generation, which takes current visual observations and action sequences as input to predict the pixel-level evolution of future frames. The model employs a spatiotemporal autoencoder structure to compress high-dimensional images into latent representations and performs dynamics modeling in the latent space. During the training phase, the robot explores different action strategies in virtual video sequences through a cycle of "imagine-execute-correct," thereby learning a general understanding of object physical properties and interaction rules.

(c) Key Technical Innovations  
The core innovation of this work lies in upgrading the video generation model from a mere prediction tool to an interactive "thought sandbox." Specifically, DreamGen introduces an action-conditioned video diffusion process, ensuring that the generated future frames are not only visually realistic but also causally consistent with the robot's subsequent actions. Additionally, through a cross-task data augmentation strategy, the model mixes multiple manipulation scenarios (e.g., grasping, pushing/pulling, placing) during training, thereby learning shared visual-motor representations, which significantly enhances zero-shot generalization capability.

(d) Experiments/Validation or Application Value  
Experiments in both simulation environments and real humanoid robot platforms show that DreamGen improves task success rates by over 30% compared to baseline methods in generalization tests involving object position shifts, shape replacements, and lighting changes. Notably, the model maintains a manipulation success rate of over 70% on unseen object categories, demonstrating robust visual reasoning. This work provides key technical support for deploying humanoid robots in scenarios requiring high flexibility, such as home services and industrial assembly, while also opening new directions for the general application of video world models in robot learning.
