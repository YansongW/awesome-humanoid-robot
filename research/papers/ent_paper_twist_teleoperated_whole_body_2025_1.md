## 概述

TWIST（遥控全身模仿系统）聚焦于人形机器人领域长期存在的数据闭环难题，旨在构建一套从人类演示到机器人可复用动作序列的完整转化框架。该研究通过融合人类视频动捕、遥操作与外骨骼数据、接触力与触觉信号等多种模态信息，结合强化学习与行为克隆等算法，实现了从异构数据源到全身轨迹与低层控制目标的高效映射。其核心贡献在于将复杂任务拆解为可路由的技能模块或专家策略，并借助高层模块进行动态组合与执行，从而显著提升了机器人数据采集与策略泛化的效率与实用性。

## 核心内容

人形机器人的全身协调控制长期受限于高质量、可复用训练数据的匮乏。传统方法往往依赖单一数据源（如纯遥操作或预录轨迹），难以兼顾操作的精细度与泛化能力。TWIST 系统直面这一瓶颈，提出了一种多源数据融合与闭环反馈机制，将人类操作中的视觉信息、运动捕捉、力觉信号与机器人本体状态进行统一建模，从而为后续的模仿学习与策略训练奠定数据基础。该研究尤其关注数据从采集到训练再到执行的全链路可复用性，解决了异构数据格式不兼容、平衡控制与全身运动耦合等关键问题。

在方法框架层面，TWIST 构建了一条完整的数据处理与策略学习流水线。首先，系统通过人类视频、动捕轨迹、遥操作与外骨骼设备采集多模态操作数据，同时记录接触力与触觉信号，形成包含人类意图与机器人状态的原始数据集。随后，利用异构动捕技术与合成平衡数据对原始数据进行对齐与增强，使其适配人形机器人的运动学与动力学约束。在此基础上，系统采用近端策略优化（PPO）与强化学习（RL）策略进行初步训练，再通过行为克隆（ACT）与模仿学习对动作序列进行精细化调整，最终输出可复用的全身轨迹与低层控制器目标。

TWIST 的关键技术创新体现在任务分解与模块化策略路由机制上。系统将复杂的全身操作任务拆解为一系列可路由的技能单元或专家策略，例如抓取、行走、平衡调整等基本动作。每个技能单元均经过独立训练与验证，具备明确的输入输出接口与状态转移条件。在执行阶段，高层模块根据当前环境感知与任务目标，动态选择并组合这些技能单元，形成连贯的动作序列。这种设计不仅降低了端到端学习的训练难度，还提升了系统在未知场景下的适应性与鲁棒性，使得 TWIST 能够处理多阶段、长时域的操作任务。

在实验验证与应用价值方面，TWIST 系统已在多种人形机器人平台上进行了部署与测试。通过对比纯遥操作与 TWIST 框架下的数据采集效率，研究表明该系统的数据复用率与策略泛化能力显著提升。此外，基于技能路由机制的模块化设计使得机器人能够快速适应新任务，而无需从头训练整个模型。这一特性对于家庭服务、工业装配、灾害救援等需要频繁切换任务的场景具有重要应用前景。TWIST 的开源项目页面提供了详细的系统架构与实验演示，为后续研究提供了可复现的基准与扩展基础。

## Overview

TWIST (Teleoperated Whole-body Imitation System) focuses on the long-standing data closed-loop challenge in the humanoid robotics field, aiming to construct a complete transformation framework from human demonstrations to reusable robot action sequences. By integrating multimodal information such as human video motion capture, teleoperation and exoskeleton data, contact forces, and tactile signals, combined with algorithms like reinforcement learning and behavior cloning, this research achieves efficient mapping from heterogeneous data sources to whole-body trajectories and low-level control objectives. Its core contribution lies in decomposing complex tasks into routable skill modules or expert policies, which are dynamically combined and executed by high-level modules, thereby significantly improving the efficiency and practicality of robot data collection and policy generalization.

## Content

The whole-body coordinated control of humanoid robots has long been constrained by the scarcity of high-quality, reusable training data. Traditional methods often rely on a single data source (e.g., pure teleoperation or pre-recorded trajectories), making it difficult to balance operational precision and generalization capability. The TWIST system directly addresses this bottleneck by proposing a multi-source data fusion and closed-loop feedback mechanism that unifies visual information, motion capture, force signals, and robot proprioceptive states from human operations, thereby laying the data foundation for subsequent imitation learning and policy training. This research particularly emphasizes the full-chain reusability of data from collection to training to execution, solving key issues such as incompatible heterogeneous data formats and the coupling of balance control with whole-body motion.

At the methodological framework level, TWIST constructs a complete data processing and policy learning pipeline. First, the system collects multimodal operation data through human videos, motion capture trajectories, teleoperation, and exoskeleton devices, while simultaneously recording contact forces and tactile signals to form a raw dataset containing human intent and robot states. Subsequently, heterogeneous motion capture techniques and synthetic balance data are used to align and augment the raw data, adapting it to the kinematic and dynamic constraints of humanoid robots. On this basis, the system employs Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) and Reinforcement Learning (RL) strategies for initial training, followed by fine-tuning action sequences through Behavior Cloning (ACT) and imitation learning, ultimately outputting reusable whole-body trajectories and low-level controller objectives.

The key technological innovation of TWIST lies in its task decomposition and modular policy routing mechanism. The system breaks down complex whole-body manipulation tasks into a series of routable skill units or expert policies, such as basic actions like grasping, walking, and balance adjustment. Each skill unit is independently trained and validated, with clear input-output interfaces and state transition conditions. During execution, a high-level module dynamically selects and combines these skill units based on current environmental perception and task objectives, forming coherent action sequences. This design not only reduces the training difficulty of end-to-end learning but also enhances the system's adaptability and robustness in unknown scenarios, enabling TWIST to handle multi-stage, long-horizon manipulation tasks.

In terms of experimental validation and application value, the TWIST system has been deployed and tested on various humanoid robot platforms. By comparing data collection efficiency between pure teleoperation and the TWIST framework, the study demonstrates a significant improvement in data reuse rate and policy generalization capability. Furthermore, the modular design based on the skill routing mechanism allows robots to quickly adapt to new tasks without retraining the entire model. This feature holds important application prospects for scenarios requiring frequent task switching, such as home services, industrial assembly, and disaster rescue. The TWIST open-source project page provides detailed system architecture and experimental demonstrations, offering a reproducible benchmark and expansion foundation for subsequent research.
