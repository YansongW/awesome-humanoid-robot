## 概述

## 核心内容

移动操作（loco-manipulation）是人形机器人走向实际应用的关键能力，要求机器人在行走或站立过程中同时完成上肢操作任务。然而，非结构化场景中存在地形起伏、目标位置不确定以及接触力动态变化等挑战，传统基于模型的控制器难以实时适应环境变化。现有研究多将运动规划与操作控制分离，导致全身协调性不足。PILOT 针对这一问题，提出将感知信息直接融入低级控制回路，使机器人能够根据实时场景表征动态调整全身运动策略，从而弥合高层任务规划与底层执行之间的鸿沟。

PILOT 的方法框架分为感知表征层与策略控制层。在感知表征层，系统从本体状态与关节序列、仿真交互数据、接触力/触觉信号以及视觉线索中恢复场景、目标或运动表征。这些多模态信息被编码为统一的地形/场景表示，为后续决策提供结构化输入。在策略控制层，系统采用 PPO 强化学习训练与全身控制器（WBC/MPC）相结合的方式，生成全身轨迹、动作序列以及低层控制器目标。关键设计在于将整体任务拆解为可路由的技能或专家策略，并通过高层模块在执行过程中动态选择与组合，实现复杂任务的分层分解与灵活调度。

PILOT 的关键技术创新体现在三个方面。第一，感知与控制的一体化设计：不同于传统方法将感知作为独立模块，PILOT 将场景恢复与运动生成直接耦合，使控制器能够根据实时感知反馈调整全身动作。第二，分层技能路由机制：通过将复杂操作任务分解为可重用的技能单元，并利用高层模块进行在线组合，系统能够适应不同场景下的任务需求，提升了泛化能力。第三，多模态融合的低层控制：融合视觉、触觉与本体感知信息，使控制器在缺乏精确环境模型时仍能通过接触力反馈实现鲁棒操作，尤其适用于地形未知或目标动态变化的场景。

在实验验证方面，PILOT 在仿真环境与真实机器人平台上进行了移动操作任务的评估。典型场景包括机器人在碎石、斜坡等非结构化地形上行走的同时，执行抓取、推拉等上肢操作任务。实验结果表明，与纯模型预测控制（MPC）或单一强化学习策略相比，PILOT 的分层技能框架在任务成功率与运动平滑性上均有显著提升。此外，感知集成机制使机器人能够在接触力突变或视觉遮挡时快速调整策略，展现出较强的抗干扰能力。该工作为人形机器人在复杂环境中的实用化部署提供了可行的技术路径，尤其在灾害救援、工业巡检等需要移动与操作并行的场景中具有重要应用价值。

## Overview

Humanoid robots face significant challenges in integrating perception and control when performing mobile manipulation tasks in unstructured environments. Existing methods often decouple perception, planning, and control, making it difficult to achieve real-time coordination between complex terrains and dynamic targets. PILOT proposes a perception-integrated low-level controller that fuses proprioceptive states, visual cues, and tactile signals to decompose complex tasks into routable skill modules. By leveraging reinforcement learning and whole-body control strategies, it generates coherent full-body trajectories and action sequences. The core contribution of this work lies in constructing an end-to-end perception-control loop from scene representation to low-level control objectives, significantly enhancing the robot's robust manipulation capabilities in unstructured scenarios.

## Content

Mobile manipulation (loco-manipulation) is a key capability for humanoid robots to transition into practical applications, requiring the robot to simultaneously perform upper-body manipulation tasks while walking or standing. However, unstructured scenes present challenges such as uneven terrain, uncertain target positions, and dynamic changes in contact forces, making it difficult for traditional model-based controllers to adapt to environmental variations in real time. Existing research often separates motion planning from manipulation control, leading to insufficient whole-body coordination. To address this, PILOT proposes directly integrating perceptual information into the low-level control loop, enabling the robot to dynamically adjust its whole-body motion strategy based on real-time scene representations, thereby bridging the gap between high-level task planning and low-level execution.

The methodological framework of PILOT consists of a perception representation layer and a policy control layer. In the perception representation layer, the system recovers scene, target, or motion representations from proprioceptive states and joint sequences, simulation interaction data, contact force/tactile signals, and visual cues. These multimodal inputs are encoded into a unified terrain/scene representation, providing structured input for subsequent decision-making. In the policy control layer, the system combines PPO reinforcement learning training with whole-body controllers (WBC/MPC) to generate full-body trajectories, action sequences, and low-level controller objectives. A key design feature is the decomposition of the overall task into routable skills or expert policies, which are dynamically selected and combined during execution by a high-level module, enabling hierarchical decomposition and flexible scheduling of complex tasks.

The key technological innovations of PILOT are reflected in three aspects. First, the integrated design of perception and control: unlike traditional methods that treat perception as an independent module, PILOT directly couples scene recovery with motion generation, allowing the controller to adjust whole-body actions based on real-time perceptual feedback. Second, the hierarchical skill routing mechanism: by decomposing complex manipulation tasks into reusable skill units and utilizing a high-level module for online combination, the system adapts to task requirements across different scenarios, enhancing generalization capabilities. Third, multimodal fusion for low-level control: integrating visual, tactile, and proprioceptive information enables the controller to achieve robust manipulation through contact force feedback even in the absence of precise environmental models, particularly suitable for scenarios with unknown terrain or dynamically changing targets.

In terms of experimental validation, PILOT was evaluated on mobile manipulation tasks in both simulated environments and real robotic platforms. Typical scenarios include the robot walking on unstructured terrains such as gravel or slopes while performing upper-body manipulation tasks like grasping, pushing, or pulling. Experimental results show that compared to pure model predictive control (MPC) or single reinforcement learning strategies, PILOT's hierarchical skill framework significantly improves task success rates and motion smoothness. Additionally, the perception integration mechanism allows the robot to quickly adjust strategies in response to sudden changes in contact forces or visual occlusions, demonstrating strong anti-interference capabilities. This work provides a feasible technical pathway for the practical deployment of humanoid robots in complex environments, particularly valuable in applications such as disaster rescue and industrial inspection, where both mobility and manipulation are required.
