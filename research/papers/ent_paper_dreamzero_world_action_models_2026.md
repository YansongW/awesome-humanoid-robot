DreamZero 提出的方法框架以“世界动作模型”为核心，该模型将传统世界模型中的状态预测分支与一个隐式动作生成模块深度融合。具体而言，模型在训练阶段同时学习环境的转移动力学和动作的先验分布，使得其内部表征不仅能够预测未来视觉或物理状态，还能直接解码出达成该状态所需的关节力矩或末端执行器轨迹。在推理阶段，面对未见过的任务描述（如“抓取红色杯子”），模型仅需输入当前观测与目标条件，即可通过单次前向传播输出完整动作序列，无需任何在线规划或优化。这一设计将传统 MPC 中耗时的滚动优化过程压缩为前馈计算，显著提升了实时性。

## Overview

The dexterous manipulation tasks of humanoid robots in unstructured environments have long been constrained by the reliance on large amounts of task-specific demonstration data, making cross-scenario generalization difficult. To address this bottleneck, DreamZero proposes a novel framework called the "World Action Model," which aims to directly transform a pre-trained world model into a zero-shot policy, eliminating the need for fine-tuning or collecting additional data for new tasks. The main contribution of this research lies in unifying action generation with physical world prediction, for the first time validating the feasibility of zero-shot manipulation policies in both simulation and real humanoid robots, thereby providing a new paradigm for the deployment of general-purpose robot intelligence.

## Content

In recent years, methods based on Model Predictive Control (MPC) and reinforcement learning have achieved significant progress in robot manipulation. However, such approaches typically require redesigning reward functions or collecting large amounts of expert demonstrations for each new task. For humanoid robots, their high degrees of freedom and complex dynamics further exacerbate the difficulty of data acquisition. The research background of DreamZero stems precisely from this contradiction: how to leverage existing, large-scale pre-trained world models to directly generate action sequences suitable for new manipulation scenarios without undergoing additional training. This problem is formalized as "zero-shot policy learning," with the core challenge being to bridge the semantic gap between the world model (which only predicts future states) and the action policy (which must output specific control commands).

The methodological framework proposed by DreamZero centers on the "World Action Model," which deeply integrates the state prediction branch of traditional world models with an implicit action generation module. Specifically, during the training phase, the model simultaneously learns the environment's transition dynamics and the prior distribution of actions, enabling its internal representation not only to predict future visual or physical states but also to directly decode the joint torques or end-effector trajectories required to achieve those states. During the inference phase, when faced with an unseen task description (e.g., "grasp the red cup"), the model only needs to input the current observation and target condition to output a complete action sequence through a single forward pass, without any online planning or optimization. This design compresses the time-consuming rolling optimization process in traditional MPC into feedforward computation, significantly improving real-time performance.

Key technological innovations are reflected in three aspects. First, DreamZero proposes a loss function for "joint action-state modeling," which minimizes the difference between predicted and true states while simultaneously constraining the smoothness and physical feasibility of generated actions, thereby avoiding cascading errors between the world model and the policy model. Second, the model adopts a Transformer-based temporal architecture capable of handling continuous action sequences spanning several seconds, utilizing causal attention mechanisms to ensure temporal consistency between action generation and state prediction. Finally, the research team designs a "goal-conditioned latent variable" mechanism that allows the model to extract task intent from natural language or demonstration images and inject it into the action generation process, thereby achieving cross-task generalization.

In terms of experimental validation, DreamZero was evaluated in multiple simulation environments (e.g., ManiSkill2 and HumanoidBench) as well as on real humanoid robot platforms. Simulation results show that the model maintains a manipulation success rate of over 80% under unseen object poses, lighting conditions, and background disturbances, significantly outperforming fine-tuning-based baseline methods. In real robot experiments, DreamZero successfully achieved zero-shot object grasping, stacking, and drawer opening tasks without using any task-specific demonstration data. These results not only validate the effectiveness of the World Action Model as a zero-shot policy but also provide a feasible pathway for the rapid deployment of humanoid robots in dynamic environments such as homes and warehouses. The practical value of this work lies in its substantial reduction of the cost of acquiring robot skills, enabling non-expert users to drive robots to perform complex manipulations through simple instructions.
