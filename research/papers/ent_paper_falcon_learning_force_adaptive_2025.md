## 概述

FALCON 提出了一种面向人形机器人的力自适应移动操作框架，旨在解决从人类操作数据到机器人全身控制指令之间的数据闭环问题。该工作的主要贡献在于将全身控制器（WBC）与模型预测控制（MPC）集成到统一的训练与部署链路中，从而有效弥合高层任务目标与底层关节动作之间的语义鸿沟。通过融合遥操作数据、外骨骼信号、接触力与触觉信息，FALCON 实现了可复用、可训练的全身轨迹与力矩命令生成，为人形机器人在复杂环境中的灵巧操作提供了新的技术路径。

## 核心内容

人形机器人的移动操作（loco-manipulation）面临双重挑战：一方面，机器人需要在动态环境中保持全身平衡；另一方面，其末端执行器必须完成精细的力控操作任务。传统方法通常将高层运动规划与底层关节控制分离，导致规划层生成的轨迹难以被底层控制器精确跟踪，尤其在接触力变化剧烈的场景中，这种断点会显著降低操作成功率。现有研究多依赖预先编程的关节序列或简化动力学模型，缺乏对人类操作中力自适应行为的有效建模，限制了机器人在非结构化环境中的泛化能力。

针对上述问题，FALCON 构建了一套完整的数据闭环系统。该系统首先通过本体状态传感器、关节位置编码器、遥操作设备与外骨骼装置，同步采集人类操作员的全身运动数据与机器人实时状态。在此基础上，系统融合接触力与触觉信号，形成多模态数据集。关键创新在于将全身控制器（WBC）与模型预测控制（MPC）作为数据处理的中间层，而非仅仅作为执行模块。这意味着采集到的原始数据并非直接映射为关节指令，而是先经过 WBC/MPC 的优化与约束处理，生成既满足动力学可行性又保留人类操作特征的中间表示，包括关节位置/力矩命令、全身轨迹序列以及末端执行器目标。

FALCON 的技术核心在于“控制器即数据转换器”的设计理念。传统方法中，WBC 和 MPC 通常作为独立的后端模块，仅在部署阶段介入，导致训练数据与执行策略之间存在分布偏移。FALCON 将其嵌入训练链路，使得模型在学习过程中能够感知底层控制器的约束特性，从而生成更符合机器人本体动力学限制的动作指令。此外，系统通过力自适应机制，利用触觉反馈实时调整末端执行器的阻抗参数，使机器人能够在抓取、推拉等操作中主动调节接触力，避免因刚性控制导致的物体滑落或结构损伤。

在实验验证方面，FALCON 在标准人形机器人平台上进行了多场景测试，包括桌面物体抓取、推门操作以及携带负载行走等任务。结果表明，集成 WBC/MPC 的训练链路显著降低了高层规划与底层执行之间的轨迹误差，尤其在接触力突变场景下，力自适应策略使操作成功率提升了显著幅度。系统还展示了良好的数据复用性，同一组训练数据可迁移至不同构型的机器人平台，验证了框架的泛化能力。该工作为人形机器人在家庭服务、工业装配等需要全身协调与力控精度的应用场景提供了可行的技术方案。

## Overview

FALCON proposes a force-adaptive mobile manipulation framework for humanoid robots, aiming to address the data closed-loop problem from human operation data to robot whole-body control commands. The main contribution of this work lies in integrating Whole-Body Control (WBC) with Model Predictive Control (MPC) into a unified training and deployment pipeline, thereby effectively bridging the semantic gap between high-level task objectives and low-level joint actions. By fusing teleoperation data, exoskeleton signals, contact forces, and tactile information, FALCON achieves reusable and trainable whole-body trajectory and torque command generation, providing a new technical pathway for dexterous manipulation of humanoid robots in complex environments.

## Content

The loco-manipulation of humanoid robots faces dual challenges: on one hand, the robot must maintain whole-body balance in dynamic environments; on the other hand, its end-effector must perform precise force-controlled manipulation tasks. Traditional methods typically separate high-level motion planning from low-level joint control, causing trajectories generated by the planning layer to be difficult for the low-level controller to accurately track. This disconnect significantly reduces operation success rates, especially in scenarios with drastic changes in contact forces. Existing research often relies on pre-programmed joint sequences or simplified dynamic models, lacking effective modeling of force-adaptive behaviors in human operations, which limits the robot's generalization ability in unstructured environments.

To address these issues, FALCON constructs a complete data closed-loop system. This system first synchronously collects the operator's whole-body motion data and the robot's real-time state through proprioceptive state sensors, joint position encoders, teleoperation devices, and exoskeleton equipment. On this basis, the system fuses contact forces and tactile signals to form a multimodal dataset. The key innovation lies in using Whole-Body Control (WBC) and Model Predictive Control (MPC) as the intermediate layer for data processing, rather than merely as execution modules. This means that the collected raw data is not directly mapped to joint commands but is first optimized and constrained by WBC/MPC to generate intermediate representations that satisfy dynamic feasibility while retaining human operation characteristics, including joint position/torque commands, whole-body trajectory sequences, and end-effector targets.

The technical core of FALCON lies in the design concept of "controller as data converter." In traditional methods, WBC and MPC are typically used as independent backend modules, only intervening during the deployment phase, leading to a distribution shift between training data and execution strategies. FALCON embeds them into the training pipeline, allowing the model to perceive the constraint characteristics of the low-level controller during the learning process, thereby generating action commands that better align with the robot's own dynamic limitations. Additionally, through a force-adaptive mechanism, the system uses tactile feedback to adjust the impedance parameters of the end-effector in real time, enabling the robot to actively regulate contact forces during tasks such as grasping and pushing/pulling, avoiding object slippage or structural damage caused by rigid control.

In terms of experimental validation, FALCON was tested on a standard humanoid robot platform across multiple scenarios, including desktop object grasping, door-pushing operations, and walking with loads. Results show that the training pipeline integrating WBC/MPC significantly reduces trajectory errors between high-level planning and low-level execution. Particularly in scenarios with sudden changes in contact forces, the force-adaptive strategy markedly improves operation success rates. The system also demonstrates good data reusability, with the same set of training data transferable to robot platforms of different configurations, validating the framework's generalization ability. This work provides a feasible technical solution for humanoid robots in application scenarios requiring whole-body coordination and force control precision, such as home services and industrial assembly.

FALCON의 기술 핵심은 "제어기를 데이터 변환기로"라는 설계 개념에 있습니다. 전통적인 방법에서 WBC와 MPC는 일반적으로 독립적인 백엔드 모듈로, 배포 단계에서만 개입하여 훈련 데이터와 실행 전략 간에 분포 편향이 발생합니다. FALCON은 이를 훈련 파이프라인에 내장하여 모델이 학습 과정에서 저수준 제어기의 제약 특성을 인식할 수 있게 하여, 로봇의 자체 동역학적 한계에 더 부합하는 동작 명령을 생성합니다. 또한 시스템은 힘 적응 메커니즘을 통해 촉각 피드백을 활용하여 말단 실행기의 임피던스 매개변수를 실시간으로 조정함으로써, 로봇이 잡기, 밀기, 당기기 등의 조작에서 접촉력을 능동적으로 조절하여 강체 제어로 인한 물체 낙하나 구조적 손상을 방지합니다.
