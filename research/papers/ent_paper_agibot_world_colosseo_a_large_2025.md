## 概述

AgiBot World Colosseo 是一个面向通用操作任务的大规模具身智能平台，旨在解决当前机器人学习系统中数据规模有限、技能泛化能力弱以及系统集成度低等关键瓶颈。该平台通过将语言指令、多视角视觉观测、本体状态与关节序列统一编码为多模态表征，并引入视觉-语言-动作（VLA）模型与基于扩散变换器（MM-DiT/Transformer）的动作头，实现了从高层语义规划到低层全身轨迹预测的端到端学习。其主要贡献在于提出了一种可路由的技能分解与组合机制，使机器人能够将复杂任务拆解为可复用的专家策略，从而在多样化操作场景中实现可扩展、可迁移的智能行为。

## 核心内容

当前人形机器人操作研究面临的核心挑战在于如何构建一个既能处理高维感知输入、又能生成精细运动控制的统一框架。传统方法往往将感知、规划与控制分离，导致系统在动态环境中响应迟缓且泛化能力不足。AgiBot World Colosseo 针对这一问题，提出了一个以多模态表征融合为核心的解决方案，将语言指令、相机图像（包括多视角观测）、本体状态（如关节角度与力矩）以及动作序列统一编码，形成对任务场景的完整描述。这一设计使得模型能够同时理解语义目标与物理约束，为后续的决策与执行奠定基础。

在方法层面，该平台采用了一种分层架构，其中高层模块负责语义规划与路由，低层模块则专注于动作生成。具体而言，系统首先利用视觉-语言模型（VLM）对输入的多模态信息进行语义解析，将任务拆解为一系列可路由的技能或专家策略。这些策略被编码为潜变量或动作 token，随后通过 MM-DiT（多模态扩散变换器）或 Transformer 动作头进行解码，输出全身轨迹、末端执行器位姿以及动作块（action chunk）。这种设计使得模型能够在保持全局一致性的同时，灵活地组合不同技能以应对新任务。

关键技术创新体现在两个层面：其一，引入“可路由技能”概念，将操作任务分解为模块化的专家策略，并通过高层路由机制动态选择与组合，从而显著提升系统的可扩展性。例如，对于“抓取杯子”和“倒水”两个子任务，系统可分别调用预训练的抓取策略与倒水策略，并通过路由模块协调执行顺序。其二，采用 MM-DiT 作为动作头，利用扩散过程逐步生成高精度轨迹，相比传统自回归方法，在复杂操作场景中展现出更强的鲁棒性与平滑性。此外，动作 chunk 的预测机制减少了长期依赖误差，使模型能够执行更长的任务序列。

实验验证方面，AgiBot World Colosseo 在多种操作任务上进行了评估，包括桌面抓取、工具使用以及多步骤装配等。结果表明，基于 VLA 的模型在任务成功率上显著优于纯视觉或纯语言基线，尤其是在需要语义理解与物理交互结合的场景中。同时，可路由技能机制使得系统在面对未见过的任务组合时，能够通过重用已有策略快速适应，而无需重新训练。这一特性对于实际部署中的成本控制与效率提升具有重要意义。该平台的开源项目页面（GitHub）提供了完整的代码、数据集与预训练模型，为学术界与工业界提供了可复现的研究基准，有望推动通用操作智能体的标准化发展。

## Overview

AgiBot World Colosseo is a large-scale embodied intelligence platform designed for general-purpose manipulation tasks, aiming to address key bottlenecks in current robot learning systems, such as limited data scale, weak skill generalization, and low system integration. By uniformly encoding language instructions, multi-view visual observations, proprioceptive states, and joint sequences into a multimodal representation, and introducing a Vision-Language-Action (VLA) model along with an action head based on a Diffusion Transformer (MM-DiT/Transformer), the platform achieves end-to-end learning from high-level semantic planning to low-level whole-body trajectory prediction. Its main contribution lies in proposing a routable skill decomposition and composition mechanism, enabling robots to break down complex tasks into reusable expert policies, thereby achieving scalable and transferable intelligent behavior across diverse manipulation scenarios.

## Content

The core challenge in current humanoid robot manipulation research is how to construct a unified framework that can both process high-dimensional perceptual inputs and generate fine-grained motion control. Traditional methods often separate perception, planning, and control, leading to slow responses and poor generalization in dynamic environments. To address this, AgiBot World Colosseo proposes a solution centered on multimodal representation fusion, uniformly encoding language instructions, camera images (including multi-view observations), proprioceptive states (such as joint angles and torques), and action sequences to form a complete description of the task scenario. This design enables the model to simultaneously understand semantic goals and physical constraints, laying the foundation for subsequent decision-making and execution.

At the methodological level, the platform adopts a hierarchical architecture, where high-level modules are responsible for semantic planning and routing, while low-level modules focus on action generation. Specifically, the system first uses a Vision-Language Model (VLM) to semantically parse the input multimodal information, decomposing the task into a series of routable skills or expert policies. These policies are encoded as latent variables or action tokens, which are then decoded by an MM-DiT (Multimodal Diffusion Transformer) or Transformer action head to output whole-body trajectories, end-effector poses, and action chunks. This design allows the model to flexibly combine different skills to handle new tasks while maintaining global consistency.

Key technological innovations are reflected in two aspects: First, the introduction of the "routable skill" concept, which decomposes manipulation tasks into modular expert policies and dynamically selects and combines them through a high-level routing mechanism, significantly enhancing system scalability. For example, for the subtasks of "grasping a cup" and "pouring water," the system can call pre-trained grasping and pouring policies respectively, coordinating their execution order via the routing module. Second, the use of MM-DiT as the action head leverages a diffusion process to progressively generate high-precision trajectories, demonstrating greater robustness and smoothness in complex manipulation scenarios compared to traditional autoregressive methods. Additionally, the prediction mechanism for action chunks reduces long-term dependency errors, enabling the model to execute longer task sequences.

In terms of experimental validation, AgiBot World Colosseo has been evaluated on various manipulation tasks, including tabletop grasping, tool use, and multi-step assembly. Results show that the VLA-based model significantly outperforms pure vision or pure language baselines in task success rates, especially in scenarios requiring a combination of semantic understanding and physical interaction. Furthermore, the routable skill mechanism allows the system to quickly adapt to unseen task combinations by reusing existing policies without retraining. This feature is crucial for cost control and efficiency improvement in practical deployment. The platform's open-source project page (GitHub) provides complete code, datasets, and pre-trained models, offering reproducible research benchmarks for both academia and industry, and is expected to promote the standardized development of general-purpose manipulation agents.
