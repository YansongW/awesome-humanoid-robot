## 概述

HOMIE 提出了一种面向人形机器人全身移动操作的同构外骨骼驾驶舱系统，旨在解决从人类示教数据到机器人可复用技能之间的数据闭环问题。该工作通过整合本体状态、关节序列、遥操作外骨骼数据与仿真交互数据，构建了一套从数据采集到策略训练、再到全身控制部署的完整链路。其主要贡献在于将 PPO/RL 策略训练与全身控制器（WBC/MPC）置于同一训练与部署框架中，从而有效减少高层任务目标与低层运动执行之间的断点，为人形机器人在复杂地形下的灵巧操作提供了可复用的数据与模型基础。

## Overview

HOMIE proposes a homologous exoskeleton cockpit system for full-body mobile manipulation of humanoid robots, aiming to solve the data closed-loop problem from human demonstration data to reusable robot skills. By integrating proprioceptive states, joint sequences, teleoperation exoskeleton data, and simulation interaction data, this work constructs a complete pipeline from data collection to policy training and full-body control deployment. Its main contribution lies in placing PPO/RL policy training and whole-body controllers (WBC/MPC) within the same training and deployment framework, thereby effectively reducing the gap between high-level task objectives and low-level motion execution, providing a reusable data and model foundation for dexterous manipulation of humanoid robots in complex terrains.

**Research Background and Problem**  
The mobile manipulation capability of humanoid robots has broad application prospects in scenarios such as disaster rescue, home services, and industrial inspection. However, existing systems generally face two major bottlenecks: first, the lack of efficient and safe human-robot interaction data collection methods leads to high costs for obtaining high-quality demonstration data; second, there is a significant gap between high-level task planning and low-level motion control, with traditional approaches often separating policy learning from whole-body control, causing a break in the training and deployment pipeline. To address these issues, HOMIE proposes using a homologous exoskeleton cockpit as a human-robot interface to achieve an intuitive mapping from human operation intentions to robot actions, and designs a unified data closed-loop framework based on this.

**Method or Model Framework**  
The overall framework of HOMIE includes three core modules: the data collection layer, the policy training layer, and the whole-body control layer. In the data collection layer, the system uses a homologous exoskeleton cockpit to synchronously record the joint sequences of human operators and the proprioceptive states of the robot, while combining interaction data from simulation environments to form a multi-source heterogeneous dataset. The policy training layer employs reinforcement learning algorithms such as PPO to transform the collected demonstration data into generalizable terrain and scene representations. The whole-body control layer integrates a whole-body controller (WBC) and model predictive control (MPC), responsible for converting the reference trajectories output by the high-level policy into low-level joint torque commands. The three modules are connected through a unified interface protocol, forming an end-to-end training and deployment closed loop.

**Key Technological Innovations**  
The core innovation of HOMIE lies in placing reinforcement learning policy training and whole-body motion control within the same pipeline, rather than traditional hierarchical independent optimization. Specifically, the reward function for PPO training directly couples the constraints of WBC/MPC (such as joint limits, foot contact forces, and centroid stability), allowing the policy to perceive the execution capability boundaries of the low-level controller during the training phase. Additionally, the design of the homologous exoskeleton cockpit achieves proportional mapping of human upper and lower limb movements to the corresponding joints of the robot, significantly reducing the cognitive load in teleoperation and ensuring physical consistency in data collection. This paradigm of "homologous mapping + joint training" significantly reduces the transfer loss from human demonstration to autonomous robot execution.
