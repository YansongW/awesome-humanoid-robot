## 概述

人形机器人在复杂环境中执行灵巧操作任务时，面临如何将高层语义理解与底层运动控制有效结合的关键挑战。现有方法往往在视觉语言模型（VLM）的语言规划与动作执行之间存在鸿沟，难以实现从感知到行动的端到端闭环。ROVE 提出了一种基于强化学习的框架，通过将语言指令、多视角视觉观测、本体状态与关节序列编码为统一的多模态表征，并融合 PPO 强化学习策略、扩散策略与 VLA 多模态动作模型，实现了对人类干预信号的有效利用。该工作的核心贡献在于保留了 VLM 的语义理解能力，同时引入机器人状态与动作头，突破了传统方法仅停留在语言规划层面的局限。

## 核心内容

**研究背景与问题**：人形机器人的通用操作能力是迈向自主智能体的关键一环，然而现有方法在处理复杂、非结构化任务时面临显著瓶颈。一方面，基于视觉语言模型的方法擅长语义理解与任务规划，但生成的规划往往缺乏对机器人本体动力学与实时环境反馈的考虑，难以直接映射为精确的关节级动作序列。另一方面，纯强化学习方法虽然能够通过试错学习获得鲁棒的控制策略，但在高维状态-动作空间中训练效率低下，且难以融入人类先验知识。如何将 VLM 的语义推理优势与强化学习的动作优化能力有机结合，同时允许人类在训练与执行过程中进行有效干预，成为该领域亟待解决的核心问题。

**方法或模型框架**：ROVE 构建了一个多模态表征学习与策略优化的联合框架。其输入层将语言指令、多视角相机图像、机器人本体状态（如关节角度、末端执行器位姿）以及历史关节序列进行编码，形成统一的多模态嵌入空间。在策略学习阶段，框架并行采用两条技术路线：一是基于近端策略优化（PPO）的强化学习训练，通过与环境交互不断优化动作策略；二是利用扩散策略或流匹配方法生成平滑、连续的动作轨迹。最终，VLA（Vision-Language-Action）多模态动作模型将上述两种策略的输出进行融合，预测出可直接执行的动作命令。该框架的关键在于设计了一个共享的语义-运动表征层，使得 VLM 的语义特征能够与机器人状态特征在动作空间中协同作用。

**关键技术创新**：ROVE 的核心创新体现在“保留语义理解，增加状态-动作头”的设计哲学上。传统 VLA 方法往往将 VLM 的输出直接作为动作指令，导致模型仅能完成语言层面的规划而无法适应物理世界的动态变化。ROVE 通过在 VLM 基础上附加专门的机器人状态编码器与动作解码头，使得模型在保持原有语义理解能力的同时，能够感知本体状态并生成符合运动学与动力学约束的动作。此外，框架引入的人类干预机制允许操作员在训练过程中通过示教或纠正信号引导策略探索，从而加速收敛并提升策略的安全性。这种设计使得 ROVE 能够在不牺牲语言泛化能力的前提下，实现从“理解”到“执行”的无缝衔接。

## Overview

Humanoid robots face a key challenge when performing dexterous manipulation tasks in complex environments: how to effectively combine high-level semantic understanding with low-level motion control. Existing methods often exhibit a gap between the language planning of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and action execution, making it difficult to achieve an end-to-end closed loop from perception to action. ROVE proposes a reinforcement learning-based framework that encodes language instructions, multi-view visual observations, proprioceptive states, and joint sequences into a unified multimodal representation. By integrating PPO reinforcement learning strategies, diffusion policies, and VLA multimodal action models, it effectively utilizes human intervention signals. The core contribution of this work lies in preserving the semantic understanding capability of VLMs while introducing robot state and action heads, breaking through the limitation of traditional methods that remain at the level of language planning.

## Content

**Research Background and Problem**: The general manipulation capability of humanoid robots is a key step toward autonomous agents. However, existing methods face significant bottlenecks when handling complex, unstructured tasks. On one hand, methods based on Vision-Language Models excel at semantic understanding and task planning, but the generated plans often lack consideration of robot body dynamics and real-time environmental feedback, making it difficult to directly map them into precise joint-level action sequences. On the other hand, while pure reinforcement learning methods can learn robust control policies through trial and error, they suffer from low training efficiency in high-dimensional state-action spaces and struggle to incorporate human prior knowledge. How to organically combine the semantic reasoning advantages of VLMs with the action optimization capabilities of reinforcement learning, while allowing effective human intervention during training and execution, has become a core problem to be solved in this field.

**Method or Model Framework**: ROVE constructs a joint framework for multimodal representation learning and policy optimization. Its input layer encodes language instructions, multi-view camera images, robot proprioceptive states (such as joint angles and end-effector poses), and historical joint sequences into a unified multimodal embedding space. During the policy learning phase, the framework adopts two parallel technical routes: one is reinforcement learning training based on Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO), which continuously optimizes the action policy through interaction with the environment; the other uses diffusion policies or flow matching methods to generate smooth, continuous action trajectories. Finally, the VLA (Vision-Language-Action) multimodal action model fuses the outputs of the above two strategies to predict directly executable action commands. The key to this framework lies in designing a shared semantic-motion representation layer, enabling the semantic features of the VLM to synergize with robot state features in the action space.

**Key Technical Innovations**: The core innovation of ROVE is reflected in the design philosophy of "preserving semantic understanding while adding state-action heads." Traditional VLA methods often directly use the output of the VLM as action commands, resulting in models that can only perform language-level planning without adapting to the dynamic changes of the physical world. By appending a dedicated robot state encoder and action decoding head to the VLM, ROVE enables the model to perceive its own state and generate actions that comply with kinematic and dynamic constraints while maintaining its original semantic understanding capability. Additionally, the introduced human intervention mechanism allows operators to guide policy exploration during training through demonstrations or correction signals, thereby accelerating convergence and improving policy safety. This design allows ROVE to achieve a seamless transition from "understanding" to "execution" without sacrificing language generalization ability.

**Experiments/Validation and Application Value**: Although the paper does not provide specific experimental data, it can be inferred from the framework design that ROVE is suitable for scenarios requiring a combination of fine manipulation and semantic understanding, such as object grasping, tool use, and assembly tasks. By integrating reinforcement learning and diffusion policies under multi-view observations, this method is expected to achieve higher task success rates and sample efficiency in both simulation environments and real robot platforms compared to pure VLM planning or pure RL methods. Its application value lies in providing a scalable general manipulation paradigm for humanoid robots: on one hand, the semantic understanding capability of VLMs enables robots to comprehend natural language instructions and adapt to new tasks; on the other hand, the combination of reinforcement learning and human intervention ensures the physical feasibility and robustness of actions. This framework offers an important technical pathway for the future deployment of humanoid robots in domains such as home service and industrial collaboration.
