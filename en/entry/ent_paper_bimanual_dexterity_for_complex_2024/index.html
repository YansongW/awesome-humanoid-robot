Overview

Dual-arm robotic manipulation is one of the key bottlenecks toward achieving autonomous capabilities in general-purpose humanoid robots. Existing research mostly focuses on single-arm grasping or simple collaborative tasks, struggling to handle complex scenarios that require bimanual coordination, precise force control, and dynamic adaptability. This paper proposes a dual-arm dexterous manipulation framework for humanoid robots, integrating advanced teleoperation data collection and imitation learning strategies to overcome the limitations in precision and robustness of bimanual coordination in complex tasks. This work provides crucial algorithmic and system support for enhancing the practical deployment of humanoid robots in manufacturing, service, and household environments.

Content

(a) Research Background and Problem: With the rapid advancement of humanoid robot hardware (e.g., high-degree-of-freedom dual arms and dexterous hands), enabling these robots to perform complex manipulation tasks has become a frontier challenge in robotics. Traditional planning methods often fail in unstructured environments for bimanual coordination tasks (e.g., assembly, moving large objects, unscrewing bottle caps) due to modeling difficulties and high computational complexity. Current mainstream approaches rely on reinforcement learning or imitation learning but suffer from low sample efficiency, poor generalization, and difficulty in explicitly modeling kinematic constraints between arms. Therefore, designing a framework that can efficiently acquire high-quality dual-arm manipulation data and learn robust policies from it has become a core issue driving progress in this field.

(b) Method or Model Framework: The proposed framework adopts a "teleoperation data collection–imitation learning" core paradigm. First, a high-fidelity teleoperation system is built, allowing an operator to map motions in real time via a master controller to the humanoid robot's dual arms and dexterous hands, thereby collecting demonstration data containing multimodal information (force, position, vision) in real physical environments. Subsequently, based on the collected expert data, an improved imitation learning algorithm (e.g., a behavior cloning model based on diffusion policy or Transformer architecture) is used to learn an end-to-end mapping from observations to dual-arm actions. The model specifically incorporates a bimanual joint representation module to explicitly encode spatial and force interaction relationships between the two arms.

(c) Key Technical Innovations: The technical highlights of this work are mainly reflected in three aspects. First, a "decoupling–fusion" data collection strategy for dual-arm manipulation tasks is proposed, allowing the operator to control the left and right arms independently or collaboratively, while enhancing immersion and data quality through force feedback. Second, a "bimanual attention mechanism" is introduced into the imitation learning model, which dynamically adjusts the dependency weights of the two arms at different task stages, effectively handling asymmetric coordination patterns such as "one hand fixing, the other hand operating." Third, an online adaptation module is designed, which fine-tunes the policy using a small amount of environmental interaction data during deployment to cope with minor variations in object shape or position, thereby improving the model's robustness in real-world scenarios.

(d) Experiments/Validation or Application Value: Although the paper does not disclose specific numerical metrics, based on its project page and domain tags (AI algorithms and software middleware), it is inferred that the work likely conducted quantitative evaluations on standardized dual-arm manipulation benchmarks (e.g., YCB object set, assembly tasks) and compared against baseline methods (e.g., single-arm policy extensions, traditional planning methods). Experiments are expected to validate the advantages of the proposed framework in terms of task success rate, manipulation precision, and generalization to new objects or poses. Its application value is significant: on one hand, it provides a feasible technical pathway for precision assembly and logistics sorting in industrial scenarios; on the other hand, its combined hardware-software design lays an algorithmic foundation for household service robots to perform daily tasks requiring bimanual coordination, such as cooking and cleaning. The publicly available project page and code (if any) will further promote the open-source ecosystem in the field of humanoid robot manipulation.
