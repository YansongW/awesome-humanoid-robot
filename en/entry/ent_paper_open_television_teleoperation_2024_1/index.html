Summary

Open-TeleVision primarily addresses data closed loops: capturing human operations and robotic states with camera images/multi-view observations, proprioceptive states and joint sequences, teleoperation/exoskeleton data, and Open-TeleVision, then through Act/behavior clone imitation learning, diffusion strategy/flow matching, MM-DiT/Transformer action head to trainable and reusable joint position/torque commands, End-effector/wrist-hand target, action chunk/token. The key point is to treat action generation as a conditional generation problem, and use diffusion or flow matching to sample executable trajectories in multimodal action distributions.

Overview

Open-TeleVision proposes an immersive active visual teleoperation system for humanoid robots, aiming to address the data loop closure problem in robot data collection and skill learning. By integrating multi-view camera observations, proprioceptive states, and joint sequences, the system transforms human manipulation behaviors into trainable and reusable robot action commands. Its core contribution lies in modeling action generation as a conditional generation problem, leveraging diffusion models or flow matching methods to sample executable trajectories from multimodal action distributions, thereby improving the data quality of teleoperation and the generalization capability of imitation learning.

Content

Research Background and Problem The dexterous manipulation and autonomous behavior learning of humanoid robots heavily rely on high-quality, large-scale demonstration data. However, traditional teleoperation schemes have limitations in the immersion and real-time performance of visual feedback, making it difficult for operators to precisely control the robot's end-effector and wrist-hand targets. Additionally, the action distribution of robots exhibits multimodal characteristics, and a single deterministic mapping cannot cover multiple feasible strategies in complex tasks. Existing data collection systems often suffer from a broken data loop, where the mapping between human operations and robot states lacks a reusable generative framework, limiting the generalization performance of imitation learning (e.g., behavioral cloning, ACT).

Method or Model Framework Open-TeleVision constructs an end-to-end teleoperation data collection and action generation framework. The front-end of the system employs a multi-view camera array and proprioceptive sensors to capture operator actions and robot states in real time, forming a multimodal data stream including image observations, joint sequences, and end-effector poses. The back-end introduces a conditional generative model based on diffusion policy or flow matching, treating action generation as a process of sampling executable trajectories from multimodal distributions. Specifically, the model uses MM-DiT (Multimodal Diffusion Transformer) or Transformer action heads to map observation conditions to joint position/torque commands or action chunks/tokens, achieving a trainable transformation from human demonstrations to robot instructions.

Key Technical Innovations The innovations of this work are reflected in three aspects: First, it proposes an "immersive active visual feedback" mechanism, where operators can obtain a first-person view aligned with the robot's perspective through a head-mounted display, supporting active viewpoint switching, significantly enhancing the sense of presence and fine manipulation capability in remote operation. Second, it redefines the action generation problem as conditional generative modeling, using diffusion or flow matching methods to handle multimodal action distributions, avoiding the mode collapse problem of traditional deterministic policies in complex tasks. Third, the system design emphasizes the completeness of the data loop, forming a unified pipeline from collection and annotation to training and deployment, enabling the collected teleoperation data to be directly used for pre-training and fine-tuning of downstream imitation learning policies.

Experiments/Validation and Application Value Although the paper does not provide specific experimental data, it can be inferred from its system architecture and domain tags (e.g., deployment, real_world, sensor_suite) that this work has been deployed and validated on real robot platforms. The value of Open-TeleVision lies in providing the robotics community with an open-source, reproducible teleoperation infrastructure, particularly suitable for tasks requiring high-precision wrist-hand manipulation (e.g., assembly, grasping). By combining data collection with generative action modeling, this method is expected to reduce the demonstration cost of humanoid robot skill learning, accelerate the transfer from simulation to the real world, and provide a standardized data loop benchmark for subsequent research.
