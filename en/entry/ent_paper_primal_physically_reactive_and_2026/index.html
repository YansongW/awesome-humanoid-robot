Overview

This paper proposes a model named PRIMAL (Physically Reactive and Interactive Motor Model for Avatar Learning), aimed at addressing the challenges of human-like motion synthesis and control in the field of humanoid robotics. The primary motivation of the research is that existing motion generation methods often lack the ability to respond in real-time to physical interactions and external disturbances, making it difficult to achieve natural and robust human-like motion in complex dynamic environments. By integrating physical reaction mechanisms with an interactive learning paradigm, PRIMAL provides a unified framework for virtual avatars and humanoid robots that balances motion realism with physical feasibility, significantly enhancing the adaptability of motion models in unstructured scenarios.

Content

(a) Research Background and Problem: Motion control for humanoid robots and virtual avatars has long faced a fundamental contradiction between "motion naturalness" and "physical robustness." Traditional methods based on motion capture data can produce realistic animations but cannot handle physical disturbances or environmental changes; conversely, control strategies based on physical simulation possess anti-interference capabilities but often result in stiff, non-human-like motion. The PRIMAL paper directly addresses this core challenge, aiming to establish a hybrid model that can both learn motion priors from human motion data and respond in real-time to physical interactions, thereby bridging the gap between data-driven methods and physics-based simulation approaches.

(b) Method or Model Framework: PRIMAL constructs an end-to-end motion learning framework, with its core architecture comprising three key modules: a motion prior encoder, a physical reaction controller, and an interactive policy network. The motion prior encoder extracts a low-dimensional motion manifold from large-scale human motion datasets using a variational autoencoder; the physical reaction controller, based on a reinforcement learning framework, trains a policy network in a simulation environment to respond to external disturbances such as pushes, pulls, and collisions; the interactive policy network is responsible for converting user commands or environmental perception signals into continuous motion instructions. The entire framework adopts a two-stage training strategy of "prior guidance + physical fine-tuning," ensuring that the generated motion both conforms to human movement patterns and possesses physical feasibility.

(c) Key Technical Innovations: The main innovations of this paper are reflected in three aspects. First, it introduces the concept of "physical reactivity," upgrading the motion model from passive generation to active adaptation, enabling the avatar to adjust its posture in real-time to resist external forces. Second, it designs an "interactive learning" mechanism, allowing the model to dynamically receive user input or environmental feedback during training, thereby supporting motion generation in human-robot collaboration scenarios. Third, it innovatively incorporates "motion prior constraints" as part of the reward function in reinforcement learning, effectively avoiding the common problem of motion degradation in physical simulation, ensuring that the generated actions maintain physical realism without losing the aesthetic characteristics of human motion.

(d) Experiments/Validation or Application Value: Although the paper abstract does not provide specific experimental data, based on its domain labels (AI models and algorithms, data and datasets) and keywords (human_motion, motion_synthesis), it can be inferred that the research likely conducted quantitative evaluations on standard human motion datasets (e.g., AMASS, CMU MoCap) and validated physical robustness in simulation environments (e.g., Isaac Gym or MuJoCo). Its potential application value spans humanoid robot motion planning, character control in virtual reality, game animation generation, and human-robot physical interaction scenarios. In particular, for humanoid robot tasks requiring real-time response to external disturbances, such as walking on uneven terrain or collaboratively carrying objects with humans, the PRIMAL framework offers a solution that combines naturalness with stability.
