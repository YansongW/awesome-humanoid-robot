Overview

Humanoid robots performing whole-body locomotion-manipulation tasks in complex environments face significant challenges in the tight coupling of perception, planning, and control. Existing methods often separate motion and manipulation, making it difficult to achieve dexterous and robust holistic behavior. Cybo-Waiter proposes a physical agent framework that models action generation as a conditional generation problem, leveraging diffusion strategies and flow matching techniques to sample executable trajectories from multimodal action distributions, thereby unifying whole-body motion and manipulation. This framework recovers scene, goal, and motion representations from language instructions and visual observations, and combines VLM-based semantic planning with closed-loop error correction mechanisms, offering a new paradigm for autonomous humanoid robot operation in service scenarios.

Content

Whole-body locomotion-manipulation for humanoid robots is a core challenge in robotics. Traditional methods typically separate walking and grasping into independent modules, leading to difficulties in coordinating upper and lower body movements in dynamic environments, especially in scenarios requiring simultaneous balance maintenance, obstacle avoidance, and task execution. Furthermore, existing systems lack sufficient integration of scene understanding and task semantics, making it hard to directly map natural language instructions into continuous whole-body trajectories. Cybo-Waiter addresses this research gap by aiming to build an end-to-end physical agent framework that enables robots to autonomously generate and execute whole-body action sequences based on language descriptions and visual input.

The core of the Cybo-Waiter framework lies in treating action generation as a conditional generation problem. Specifically, the system first extracts scene geometry, object poses, and motion prior representations from language instructions and camera images or multi-view observations. Then, using diffusion strategies or stream matching techniques, it samples from multimodal action distributions under the constraints of these representations to generate continuous whole-body trajectories. This design avoids the mode collapse issues of traditional deterministic planning methods in complex distributions, allowing the robot to adapt to diverse task requirements and dynamic environmental changes.

The key technological innovations of this framework are reflected in three aspects. First, vision-language-action joint representation: leveraging pre-trained vision-language models (VLMs) for semantic planning and routing, decomposing high-level task instructions into executable sub-goal sequences. Second, closed-loop error correction and human intervention mechanisms: during execution, the system continuously receives visual feedback to correct trajectory deviations in real time, and allows human intervention via language or gestures, enhancing system robustness and safety. Third, multimodal action distribution modeling: utilizing the stepwise denoising property of diffusion processes to sample trajectories from high-dimensional action spaces that satisfy both physical constraints and task objectives, effectively handling the coupling between motion and manipulation.

In terms of experimental validation, Cybo-Waiter was evaluated in both simulated environments and on real humanoid robot platforms. The experimental tasks cover typical service scenarios such as desktop object grasping, mobile delivery, and dynamic obstacle avoidance. Results show that the framework outperforms rule-based or traditional optimization-based baselines in task success rate, trajectory smoothness, and execution efficiency. Particularly in composite tasks requiring simultaneous walking and upper-limb manipulation, Cybo-Waiter demonstrates significant whole-body coordination capabilities, validating the effectiveness of the conditional generation paradigm for humanoid robot whole-body control. The modular design of the framework also facilitates its transfer to humanoid robot platforms of different configurations, offering high engineering application value.

From an application perspective, Cybo-Waiter provides a feasible technical pathway for service robots, home assistants, and industrial collaboration scenarios. Its natural interaction approach based on language and vision lowers the barrier for user adoption, while the closed-loop error correction and human intervention mechanisms enhance system reliability in unstructured environments. In the future, this framework can be further extended to multi-robot collaboration, complex assembly tasks, and human-robot integration scenarios, advancing humanoid robots from the laboratory to real-world deployment.
