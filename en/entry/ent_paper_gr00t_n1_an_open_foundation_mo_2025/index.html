Summary

The implementation path of GR00T n 1 is to first encode language instructions, camera images/multi-view observations, ontological states, and joint sequences into multimodal representations, which can be used to generate a multi-modal representation, then, action/behavior clone imitation learning, Diffusion Policy/flow matching, and VLA multimodal action model are used to predict executable action commands. The key point is to retain the semantic understanding of VLM, while increasing the robot state and action head, so as to avoid remaining in the language planning.

Overview

GR00T N1 is an open foundation model study for general-purpose humanoid robots, aiming to address the current disconnect between language understanding and physical execution in robot models. This research proposes an end-to-end Vision-Language-Action (VLA) framework that preserves the semantic understanding capabilities of large language models while introducing robot state encoding and action prediction heads, enabling direct mapping from language instructions to physical actions. Its main contribution is providing an open-source multimodal foundation model, offering a reproducible benchmark solution for humanoid robot generalization tasks in complex environments.

Content

(a) Research Background and Problem: Current mainstream approaches in humanoid robotics often separate language understanding from action execution, first using a Vision-Language Model (VLM) to generate high-level semantic plans, then converting them into joint commands through an independent motion planning module. This cascaded architecture suffers from information loss and struggles to handle real-time feedback in dynamic environments. Addressing this bottleneck, GR00T N1 proposes to uniformly encode language instructions, multi-view visual observations, proprioceptive states, and joint sequences into a multimodal representation, fundamentally avoiding the gap between semantic planning and physical execution.

(b) Method or Model Framework: The model adopts an encoder-decoder architecture. It first uses a pre-trained Vision-Language Model to jointly encode language instructions and camera images, while introducing a proprioceptive state encoder to process robot joint angles, torques, and other proprioceptive information. During the decoding phase, the model employs behavioral cloning via imitation learning (ACT) combined with diffusion policy/flow matching techniques to convert the multimodal representation into a continuous sequence of joint actions. The entire framework is centered on the VLA multimodal action model, ensuring that output actions both comply with semantic instructions and satisfy physical feasibility.

(c) Key Technological Innovations: The core innovation of GR00T N1 lies in its "semantic preservation" mechanism—adding robot state heads and action heads on top of the VLM, rather than simply using the VLM as a front-end planner. This allows the model to simultaneously leverage the VLM's common-sense knowledge and the robot's specific kinematic constraints during inference, avoiding the pitfall of "planning without execution." Additionally, the model adopts flow matching as the action generation strategy, which offers faster sampling speeds and smoother action trajectories compared to traditional diffusion models, making it particularly suitable for the high-frequency control requirements of humanoid robots.

(d) Experiments/Validation and Application Value: The research team conducted large-scale training and evaluation based on the GR00T-X embodied simulation dataset, which contains operation trajectories of various humanoid robots in different scenarios. Experimental results show that GR00T N1 exhibits strong zero-shot generalization capabilities in general manipulation tasks (e.g., grasping, placing, assembly) and is robust to multi-view observations. As an open foundation model, its open-source nature will lower the barrier to entry for humanoid robot research, promoting the VLA paradigm from the laboratory to practical applications, especially in industrial and service scenarios requiring rapid deployment.
