Definition and Positioning of Diffusion Policy

Diffusion Policy belongs to the method type. Its domains include: AI models and algorithms, data and datasets. Value chain level: intelligence layer. Modeling action distributions as denoising diffusion models, visual motor policies can generate multimodal and smooth robot actions. The English name is Diffusion Policy. The Korean name is 디퓨전 정책.

Mathematical and Theoretical Foundations of Diffusion Policy

Diffusion Policy is built upon relevant mathematical theories and physical laws. Understanding its underlying assumptions, constraints, and derivation processes is a prerequisite for correctly applying this method. Specifically, attention must be paid to its input space, output space, objective function, and convergence or stability guarantees. In humanoid robots, which are high-dimensional, underactuated, and strongly coupled systems, Diffusion Policy typically needs to balance real-time performance, precision, and robustness.

Algorithm Steps and Implementation Key Points

When implementing Diffusion Policy in practice, it is necessary to clarify initialization conditions, iteration rules, stopping criteria, and parameter tuning strategies. Choosing appropriate numerical methods, linear algebra solvers, and parallel computing strategies can significantly improve computational efficiency and stability. At the same time, engineering constraints such as model errors, sensor noise, and actuator saturation should be fully considered to ensure reliable algorithm operation on real platforms.

Typical Applications and Limitations

Diffusion Policy can be applied to various aspects of humanoid robots, including motion planning, control optimization, state estimation, and learning algorithms. However, its computational complexity, dependence on model accuracy, and online adaptation capabilities remain key issues to address in practical deployment.
