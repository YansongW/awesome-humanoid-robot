Definition and Positioning of Quasi-Direct Drive Actuators

Quasi-direct drive actuators belong to the technology type. Their fields include: AI models and algorithms, components. Value chain level: intelligence layer, upstream. An actuator design that achieves high bandwidth and force transparency by employing low reduction ratio gears and high-torque motors. The English name is Quasi Direct Drive Actuator. The Korean name is Quasi Direct Drive Actuator.

Working Principle and Technical Architecture of Quasi-Direct Drive Actuators

The core mechanism of quasi-direct drive actuators determines their performance boundaries in humanoid robot systems. Understanding their internal structure, signal flow, and control interfaces facilitates system integration and optimization. During selection and integration, attention must be paid to compatibility with controllers, communication buses, power systems, and mechanical structures.

Key Parameters and Selection Considerations

In engineering practice, selecting quasi-direct drive actuators requires comprehensive consideration of performance indicators, reliability, cost, supply chain maturity, and compatibility with the overall system. Key parameters typically include precision, bandwidth, torque, power consumption, weight, interface protocols, and environmental adaptability. For different application scenarios, trade-offs between performance and cost may be necessary, with appropriate redundancy and safety margins reserved.

Typical Applications and Development Trends

Quasi-direct drive actuators have been widely used in prototype validation, academic research, and early commercial products of humanoid robots. In the future, as the industry chain matures, their integration level, intelligence, and cost-effectiveness are expected to continue improving.
