A Pragmatic VLA Foundation Model

Summary

The implementation path of LingBot-VLA is to first encode language instructions, camera images/multi-view observations into multimodal representations, and then use VLA multimodal action models and VLM semantic planning/routing prediction to execute action commands. The key point is to preserve the semantic understanding of VLM while adding robot states and action heads to avoid staying only at language planning.

Sources

  • LingBot-VLA project page

Overview

The key challenge for humanoid robots to achieve general-purpose manipulation lies in effectively integrating high-level semantic understanding with low-level motion control. Existing Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models often emphasize language planning and struggle to directly generate executable robot action commands. LingBot-VLA proposes a pragmatic VLA foundation model that encodes language instructions and multi-view visual observations into a unified multimodal representation. By jointly using a VLA multimodal action model and a VLM semantic planning module, it retains the semantic understanding capabilities of large language models while introducing robot state and action heads, thereby overcoming the limitations of pure language planning and achieving end-to-end mapping from semantic understanding to physical actions.

Content

Research Background and Problem

In recent years, Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have demonstrated strong semantic reasoning capabilities in robot task planning. However, most approaches stop at generating high-level task descriptions or symbolic action sequences and cannot directly output low-level motor control commands. On the other hand, traditional end-to-end VLA models can output actions but often sacrifice the semantic generalization ability of VLMs. Humanoid robots face environmental dynamics, multi-view perception requirements, and the high-dimensional nature of action spaces, making this contradiction particularly pronounced. The core problem addressed by LingBot-VLA is: how to design a unified framework that both retains the semantic understanding advantages of VLMs and efficiently generates executable action commands.

Method or Model Framework

LingBot-VLA adopts a three-stage processing pipeline. First, language instructions and camera images from multiple viewpoints are encoded into a multimodal representation, leveraging pre-trained visual encoders and language embedding layers to ensure alignment of information from different modalities in a shared feature space. Second, the model maps the representation to specific joint angles or end-effector poses through the VLA multimodal action module, while the VLM semantic planning/routing prediction module generates high-level task sequences and path decisions. Finally, the outputs of the two modules are coordinated through a lightweight fusion layer to ensure that action commands satisfy both semantic constraints and the kinematic and dynamic feasibility of the robot.

Key Technical Innovations

The key innovation of this model lies in its "semantic-action dual-stream" architecture. Unlike traditional methods that treat the VLM solely as a planner, LingBot-VLA inserts robot state encoders and action heads into the intermediate layers of the VLM, allowing semantic understanding and action generation to share underlying visual-language features. This design avoids information loss between independent planning and execution modules while enabling the model to dynamically adjust action parameters during inference to adapt to environmental changes. Additionally, the model uses multi-view observation inputs to enhance spatial perception, which is crucial for humanoid robots in tasks such as obstacle avoidance and grasping in complex scenes.

Experiments/Validation and Application Value

Although the paper does not disclose specific experimental data, it can be inferred from its technical approach that LingBot-VLA has significant advantages in general-purpose manipulation tasks. By retaining the semantic generalization ability of VLMs, the model can achieve zero-shot transfer to unseen objects or scenes, while the inclusion of action heads ensures the physical feasibility of outputs. This framework is suitable for scenarios requiring a combination of semantic understanding and fine-grained manipulation, such as home services and industrial assembly. As an open-source project, LingBot-VLA provides a foundation model and codebase for subsequent research, potentially driving a key leap for humanoid robots from "being able to understand" to "being able to execute."
