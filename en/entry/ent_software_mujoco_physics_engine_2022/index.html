Definition and Positioning of the MuJoCo Physics Engine

The MuJoCo physics engine belongs to the software platform category. Its domains include software middleware, AI models and algorithms. It is positioned at the intelligence layer of the value chain. It is a high-fidelity physics engine with rich contact dynamics, widely used in humanoid control research. Its English name is MuJoCo Physics Engine. Its Korean name is MuJoCo 물리 엔진.

Working Principle and Technical Architecture of the MuJoCo Physics Engine

The core mechanism of the MuJoCo physics engine determines its performance boundaries in humanoid robot systems. Understanding its internal structure, signal flow, and control interfaces facilitates system integration and optimization. During the selection and integration process, attention must be paid to its compatibility with controllers, communication buses, power systems, and mechanical structures.

Key Parameters and Selection Criteria

In engineering practice, selecting the MuJoCo physics engine requires comprehensive consideration of performance indicators, reliability, cost, supply chain maturity, and compatibility with the overall system. Key parameters typically include precision, bandwidth, torque, power consumption, weight, interface protocols, and environmental adaptability. For different application scenarios, trade-offs between performance and cost may be necessary, with appropriate redundancy and safety margins reserved.

Typical Applications and Development Trends

The MuJoCo physics engine has been widely used in prototype validation, academic research, and early commercial products for humanoid robots. As the industry chain matures, its integration level, intelligence, and cost-effectiveness are expected to continue improving.
