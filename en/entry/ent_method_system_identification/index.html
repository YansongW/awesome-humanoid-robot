Definition and Positioning of System Identification

System identification belongs to the method type. Its domains include: AI models and algorithms, data and datasets. Value chain level: intelligence layer. The process of establishing a mathematical model of a dynamic system based on measured input-output data to align simulation with reality. The English name is System Identification. The Korean name is 시스템 동정.

Mathematical and Theoretical Foundations of System Identification

System identification is built upon relevant mathematical theories and physical laws. Understanding its assumptions, constraints, and derivation process is a prerequisite for correctly applying this method. Specifically, attention should be paid to its input space, output space, objective function, and convergence or stability guarantees. In humanoid robots, which are high-dimensional, underactuated, and strongly coupled systems, system identification typically requires a balance between real-time performance, accuracy, and robustness.

Algorithm Steps and Implementation Key Points

When implementing system identification in practice, it is necessary to clarify initialization conditions, iteration rules, stopping criteria, and parameter tuning strategies. Reasonable selection of numerical methods, linear algebra solvers, and parallel computing strategies can significantly improve computational efficiency and stability. At the same time, engineering constraints such as model errors, sensor noise, and actuator saturation should be fully considered to ensure reliable algorithm operation on real platforms.

Typical Applications and Limitations

System identification can be applied to various aspects of humanoid robots, including motion planning, control optimization, state estimation, and learning algorithms. However, its computational complexity, dependence on model accuracy, and online adaptability remain key issues to address in practical deployment.

Role in Humanoid Robot Systems

As one of the key methods in the humanoid robot industry chain, system identification plays an important role in system design, performance optimization, and industrial application. It interacts with multiple subsystems such as perception, decision-making, actuation, energy, structure, and verification, collectively determining overall robot performance. Related research and applications are continuously advancing to further improve its reliability, efficiency, and cost-effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
