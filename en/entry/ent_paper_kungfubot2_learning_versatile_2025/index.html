Summary

KungfuBot2 converts ontology states and joint sequences, human video/motion capture trajectories, and simulation interaction data into trackable body targets, and ultimately outputs whole-body trajectories/action sequences and low-level controller targets through teacher-student knowledge transfer, ACT/behavior cloning imitation learning, IK/action redirection training, or combined whole-body strategies. The key point is to train teacher strategies with privileged information and then distill the capabilities to the point where only student strategies with deployed observations can be used.

Overview

KungfuBot2 proposes a general motor skill learning framework for whole-body control of humanoid robots, aiming to address the challenges of multi-source motion data fusion and policy transfer. This research unifies heterogeneous inputs such as proprioceptive states, human videos/motion capture trajectories, and simulation interaction data into trackable body targets. By combining teacher-student knowledge distillation and behavioral cloning strategies, it achieves effective capability transfer from privileged information to deployment-level observations. Its main contribution lies in constructing a scalable whole-body motion generation pipeline, providing a systematic solution for balance, motion tracking, and behavior foundation model training of humanoid robots in complex dynamic environments.

Content

Whole-body control of humanoid robots has long faced two major challenges: first, how to extract generalizable skill representations from diverse motion data sources (e.g., human demonstrations, simulation interactions); second, how to achieve robust motion execution during deployment relying only on limited proprioceptive perception information. Existing methods are mostly confined to single tasks or specific input modalities, making it difficult to fuse motion knowledge from different sources, and often suffer performance degradation during simulation-to-real transfer. KungfuBot2 addresses these bottlenecks by proposing a unified data representation and policy learning framework.

The core architecture of this framework consists of a data transformation layer and a policy learning layer. In the data transformation layer, the system uniformly maps heterogeneous inputs such as proprioceptive states and joint sequences, human videos/motion capture trajectories, and simulation interaction data into trackable body targets, such as end-effector poses, center-of-mass trajectories, or keypoint positions. This step eliminates format differences between data sources, enabling subsequent policy training based on consistent supervisory signals. In the policy learning layer, the research adopts a teacher-student knowledge distillation paradigm: first, a teacher policy is trained using "privileged" data containing complete state information to master optimal whole-body motion mappings; then, through the distillation process, the teacher's capabilities are compressed into a student policy that relies only on deployment-level observations (e.g., joint angles, IMU readings), thereby reducing dependence on high-precision perception while maintaining performance.

KungfuBot2's technological innovations are mainly reflected in three aspects. First, it integrates multiple imitation learning techniques, including ACT (Action Chunking with Transformers) and behavioral cloning, enabling the policy to learn motion patterns at different time scales and action granularities. Second, the system introduces IK (Inverse Kinematics) and motion retargeting training to ensure that the generated whole-body trajectories are physically feasible and comply with the kinematic constraints of humanoid robots. Third, through a combined whole-body policy mechanism, the framework can dynamically switch or fuse different skill modules (e.g., walking, balance recovery, object manipulation), thereby supporting more complex continuous task execution. This modular design also lays the foundation for constructing behavior foundation models for humanoid robots.

In terms of experimental validation, although the paper does not disclose specific numerical values, the application value can be inferred from the framework design. KungfuBot2 can handle various data inputs from offline demonstrations to online interactions, meaning it can be used both to learn natural motions from human videos and to autonomously explore skills in simulation environments through reinforcement learning. Its teacher-student distillation mechanism is particularly suitable for addressing simulation-to-real transfer issues, as the student policy only requires low-cost sensor configurations during deployment. Furthermore, by integrating capabilities such as balance and motion tracking into a unified framework, KungfuBot2 is expected to achieve smoother and more robust whole-body coordinated motion in scenarios such as home services and industrial inspections for humanoid robots.
