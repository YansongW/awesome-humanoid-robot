Humanoid Robots: From Mine to Market

A Wiki project based on the Awesome Humanoid Robot knowledge graph.

About This Wiki

Humanoid robots are at a critical inflection point, moving from laboratory demonstrations to industrial applications. Manufacturers such as Tesla Optimus, Figure AI, Unitree, Agibot, and UBTech are pushing bipedal humanoid robots into factories, warehouses, and broader scenarios. However, a significant gap in reliability, cost, maintainability, and compliance remains between "a robot that can walk" and "a robot that can be sold."

This Wiki attempts to answer one core question:

How can humanoid robots achieve mass production and industrial application from 0 to 1?

To address this, the Wiki adopts a knowledge graph approach, structuring, linking, and making traceable knowledge across materials, components, design, manufacturing, control, artificial intelligence, software, data, testing, applications, markets, and policies. Each chapter is written to the standard of a research paper, including data, sources, diagrams, case studies, and knowledge graph anchors.

Content Features

  • Full-Coverage: From rare earth minerals to end markets, from fundamental theory to engineering practice.
  • Data-Driven: Prioritizes the latest market data, corporate progress, and funding dynamics from 2025-2026.
  • Cross-Layer Correlation: Not only describes isolated technical points but also reveals the links between technology, supply chain, and industry.
  • Traceable Sources: Each key claim is annotated with its source for easy reader verification.
  • Multi-Audience Oriented: Provides different reading paths for researchers, engineers, students, investors, and policymakers.
  • Wiki & KG Integration: KG entity pages link back to corresponding Wiki chapters; concepts in Wiki chapters are consolidated into KG entities and relationships.

The content of this book originates from the Awesome Humanoid Robot knowledge graph project. This knowledge graph uses an entity-relationship-source-verification information model, covering thousands of entities and relationships across the entire humanoid robot industry chain. Readers can perform cross-layer queries, reasoning, and visual analysis through the knowledge graph.
