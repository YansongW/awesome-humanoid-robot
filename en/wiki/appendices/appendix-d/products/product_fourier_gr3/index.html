Product Overview

Fourier GR-3 (internal codename Care-bot) is Fourier's first full-size humanoid robot focused on interactive companion scenarios. The GR-3 stands 165 cm tall, weighs 71 kg, features 55 degrees of freedom throughout the body, and is equipped with a 12 DOF dexterous hand. Unlike traditional metallic robots, the GR-3 adopts a Morandi warm color scheme, supercar-grade interior wrapping materials, and a "GFOAM" soft padding interior, emphasizing affinity and a sense of security.

The GR-3 is equipped with Fourier's self-developed full-sense interactive system, integrating three modules: hearing, vision, and touch, supporting emotional feedback and natural interaction. Its dual-battery hot-swap architecture provides a single-charge battery life of approximately 3 hours, allowing battery replacement without interrupting power supply. It is suitable for scenarios such as companionship for the elderly living alone, child interaction, and rehabilitation assistance.

Application Scenarios

  • Elderly Companionship: Daily care for the elderly living alone, health reminders, emotional communication, and emergency call assistance.
  • Child Interaction: Early education companionship, interactive games, and social training support for children with autism.
  • Rehabilitation Assistance: Mild rehabilitation training and exercise encouragement in home and community settings.

Selection and Deployment Suggestions

  • Elderly care institutions or home users should pay close attention to the GR-3's privacy protection, fall detection, and emergency call mechanisms.
  • It is recommended to conduct small-scale trials before deployment to evaluate user acceptance, battery life satisfaction, and long-term maintenance costs.
