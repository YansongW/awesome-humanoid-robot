Product Overview

Baowu Magnesium alloy ingots are raw materials for lightweight metal structural components, covering mainstream series such as AZ, AM, AE, and AS. The products are manufactured through raw magnesium smelting, alloying refining, and ingot casting, with strict impurity control and uniform composition, and can be directly used for high-pressure die casting, extrusion, forging, and semi-solid forming.

Magnesium alloy has a density of only about 2/3 that of aluminum alloy and 1/4 that of steel, while also possessing excellent damping and electromagnetic shielding properties. It is an important material choice for lightweighting in humanoid robots, new energy vehicles, and 3C products.

Selection and Deployment Recommendations

  • When selecting materials, choose the grade based on strength, corrosion resistance, and forming process: AZ91D has high strength but average corrosion resistance; AM60B has good toughness.
  • Before die casting, fully preheat the mold and control the pouring temperature to reduce defects such as cold shuts and porosity.
  • For surface treatment, micro-arc oxidation, painting, or chemical conversion coatings are recommended to improve corrosion and wear resistance.
