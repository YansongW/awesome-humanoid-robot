Product Overview

Fourier GR-1 is the first general-purpose humanoid robot launched by Fourier Intelligence and one of the earliest bipedal platforms in China to achieve mass production and delivery. The GR-1 stands 165 cm tall, weighs 55 kg, has 44 degrees of freedom across the body, a walking speed of up to 5 km/h, and a maximum joint torque of 230 N·m. Fourier has transferred the force control technology accumulated in the field of rehabilitation exoskeletons to the GR-1, enabling compliant control capabilities during contact tasks.

The GR-1 uses Fourier's self-developed FSA (Fourier Self-Actuator) integrated actuator, which integrates the motor, driver, reducer, and encoder. Leveraging its customer base in the medical rehabilitation sector, the GR-1 has entered the rehabilitation departments of several top-tier hospitals for gait training and rehabilitation therapy assistance.

Application Scenarios

  • Medical Rehabilitation: Gait training, motor function assessment, and rehabilitation therapy assistance in rehabilitation departments of top-tier hospitals.
  • Industrial Precision Assembly: Electronic component assembly, screw driving, and insertion tasks under force-controlled compliant operation.
  • Scientific Research and Education: Research platforms for robot control algorithms, human-robot interaction, and rehabilitation engineering.
